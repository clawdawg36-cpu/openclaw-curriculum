## ✅ Passing Score Recommendation

**Recommended passing score: 7/10 (70%)**

**Rationale:**
- Questions 1, 2, and 5 are foundational/easy — students should consistently get these correct after completing Module 1
- Medium-difficulty questions test applied understanding; students who engaged with the material should score well here
- Hard questions (4 and 9) test deeper conceptual understanding; partial misses here are acceptable for a first pass
- A 70% threshold ensures students have grasped the core concepts without being overly punitive for nuanced questions

**Score interpretation:**
| Score | Interpretation | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| 9–10 | Excellent — strong conceptual grasp | Move to Module 2 |
| 7–8 | Passing — solid foundation | Review missed questions, then proceed |
| 5–6 | Near-miss — gaps in understanding | Review Module 1 materials, retake quiz |
| 0–4 | Did not pass — significant gaps | Full Module 1 review before retake |

## 🔄 Retry Policy

**Maximum attempts:** 3  
**Cooldown between attempts:** 24 hours (recommended)  
**Question randomization:** Shuffle question order on each attempt if your platform supports it (do not change answers)

**Retry guidance for students:**
- After a failed attempt, review the explanations for every question you missed
- Re-read the relevant Module 1 sections before retaking
- Don't just memorize answers — understand *why* each answer is correct

**After 3 failed attempts:**
- The student should contact their instructor or facilitator before attempting again
- A brief 1:1 check-in can identify misconceptions that the quiz alone can't surface
- Consider assigning supplemental reading or a different learning activity before allowing further attempts

## 📊 Formative Assessment: Using Quiz Results

This quiz is designed as a **formative assessment** — its primary purpose is to identify learning gaps and guide instruction, not to assign a final grade.

### For Instructors

**1. Look at class-wide patterns, not just individual scores**
- If >30% of students miss Q4 (narrow AI), plan a brief re-teach on AI vs. AGI
- If >30% miss Q9 (algorithmic bias), spend time on the ethics/bias discussion before Module 2
- Easy questions (1, 2, 5) missed by many students signal the student didn't engage with foundational content

**2. Use Q10 (Short Answer) qualitatively**
- Read responses to understand how students are connecting AI concepts to their lives
- Surface interesting student examples in the next class discussion
- Vague or incorrect responses often reveal specific misconceptions worth addressing

**3. Group students for peer discussion**
- Pair students who scored 9–10 with those who scored 5–6 for a brief "explain your thinking" discussion
- Peer explanation often cements understanding better than re-reading

**4. Track scores over time**
- If this quiz is used across cohorts, track average scores to identify which questions consistently confuse students — those may need content or wording improvements

### For Self-Directed Learners

- Use your score and the explanations to identify which concepts need more review
- If you missed Q6 or Q9, read more about AI bias before moving on — it's foundational to responsible AI use
- Q10 is a self-check: if you struggled to think of an example, spend 10 minutes looking around your digital life for AI
