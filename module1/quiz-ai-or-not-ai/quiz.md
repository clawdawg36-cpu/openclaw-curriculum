## Question 1 *(Multiple Choice | Easy)*

**Which of the following is the best example of Artificial Intelligence in action?**

- A) A calculator adding two numbers
- B) A thermostat turning on the heat at a set time
- C) A spam filter learning to recognize junk email based on patterns
- D) A smoke alarm beeping when it detects smoke

**✅ Correct Answer:** C

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

A spam filter that *learns* from patterns is AI — it improves over time based on data. The other options follow fixed, pre-programmed rules with no learning involved. AI systems adapt and improve; rule-based systems just follow instructions.

</details>

## Question 2 *(Multiple Choice | Easy)*

**What does "machine learning" mean?**

- A) Teaching robots to walk and talk
- B) Programming a computer with every possible rule it needs
- C) Allowing a computer to learn patterns from data without being explicitly programmed for every case
- D) Using a computer to control physical machines in a factory

**✅ Correct Answer:** C

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

Machine learning (ML) is a subset of AI where systems learn from data rather than following hard-coded rules. The computer finds patterns on its own — the programmer doesn't need to specify every scenario in advance.

</details>

## Question 3 *(Multiple Choice | Medium)*

**A recommendation engine on a streaming platform suggests shows based on your watch history. Which AI concept best describes this?**

- A) Computer vision
- B) Collaborative filtering / recommendation system
- C) Natural language processing
- D) Robotic process automation

**✅ Correct Answer:** B

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

Recommendation systems use *collaborative filtering* (comparing your behavior to similar users) or *content-based filtering* (analyzing what you've already watched). Computer vision deals with images, NLP with language, and RPA with automating repetitive software tasks.

</details>

## Question 4 *(Multiple Choice | Hard)*

**Which of the following describes "narrow AI" (also called weak AI)?**

- A) An AI system that is smarter than humans in every domain
- B) An AI system that can perform any intellectual task a human can
- C) An AI system designed and trained to perform one specific task very well
- D) An AI system that has emotions and self-awareness

**✅ Correct Answer:** C

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

All AI systems in existence today are *narrow AI* — highly capable at specific tasks (like chess, image recognition, or language translation) but unable to generalize across domains. "General AI" (AGI) — which can do anything a human can — does not yet exist.

</details>

- B) False

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

## Question 8 *(Scenario-Based | Medium)*

**Scenario:** Sarah uploads a photo of a skin rash to a health app. The app immediately responds: *"This appears to be eczema with 87% confidence. Consider seeing a dermatologist."*

**Which of the following AI technologies is most likely powering this feature?**

- A) Natural language processing (NLP)
- B) Reinforcement learning
- C) Computer vision / image classification
- D) Rule-based expert system

**✅ Correct Answer:** C

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

Analyzing an image and classifying what it shows is *computer vision* — a branch of AI trained on thousands (or millions) of labeled images. NLP handles text/language. Reinforcement learning trains agents through trial-and-error. A rule-based expert system uses human-written rules, not learned patterns from images.

</details>

## Question 9 *(Scenario-Based | Hard)*

**Scenario:** A bank's loan approval system was found to reject applications from certain zip codes at much higher rates than others, even when applicants had similar credit profiles. An audit revealed the model was trained on 20 years of historical loan data.

**What is the most likely cause of this problem?**

- A) The computer ran out of memory while processing applications
- B) The AI model was trained on biased historical data that reflected past discriminatory lending practices
- C) The model wasn't given enough data to make decisions
- D) The programmers intentionally coded discrimination into the system

**✅ Correct Answer:** B

<details>
<summary>💡 Show Explanation</summary>

This is a classic example of *algorithmic bias*. Historical lending data often reflects past discrimination (e.g., redlining). An AI trained on that data learns to replicate those patterns — without any programmer intentionally writing discriminatory rules. This is why AI audits, diverse training data, and fairness testing are essential in high-stakes domains like finance, healthcare, and criminal justice.

</details>
