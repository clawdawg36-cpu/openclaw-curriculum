# ✅ Exit Ticket — Teacher Reference

**What to look for in responses:**

### Things AI Does Well (strong answers include):
- Generating fluent, grammatically correct text
- Answering factual questions quickly and accurately
- Recognizing patterns in data
- Being consistent, patient, and tireless
- Translating languages, summarizing information
- Playing games like chess or Go
- Producing "convincing" responses in the Turing Test

### Things AI Struggles With (strong answers include):
- Genuine empathy or emotional understanding
- Common sense in new or unexpected situations
- Knowing when it's wrong or uncertain
- Having real opinions or personal experiences
- Understanding context the way humans do
- Creativity rooted in lived experience
- Asking the *right* questions (not just answering them)

### Flags to follow up on:
- Students who say AI is "just as good as humans at everything" → needs more nuance
- Students who say AI "can't do anything right" → missed the point of the activity
- Students whose lingering questions are rich and specific → great for future lessons or differentiation

### Sample Strong Responses:

**Pair 1 (AI does well):** "AI is really good at answering questions fast — like it knew exactly how far the moon was with all the details."

**Pair 2 (AI struggles):** "AI struggles with actually feeling things. Like the empathy responses sounded okay but they just gave advice right away instead of actually listening."

**Summary sentence:** "The Turing Test is a way to find out if a machine can trick a human into thinking it's also human."

**Lingering question:** "If AI gets better at faking emotions, should we be allowed to use it for therapy?"
