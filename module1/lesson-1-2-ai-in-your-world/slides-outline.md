## Slide 7 — How Recommendation Algorithms Work

**Heading:** Why Does It Always Know What You Want?

**Body:**
- Recommendation systems watch what you **click, watch, like, and skip**
- They find people with similar patterns → "People like you also watched…"
- The goal: keep you engaged as long as possible
- Netflix: saves $1B/year in avoided cancellations through recommendations

**Visual:** Simple diagram — User behavior → Pattern matching → Recommendation

*Presenter: "Here's the eerie part. These systems don't know *you* — they know your patterns. They treat you as a data point that looks like millions of other data points. What does that feel like?" Wait for reactions.*

---

## Slide 8 — Automation vs. Intelligence

**Heading:** Wait — Is All of This Actually "AI"?

**Body:**
- **Automation:** following pre-set rules (if-then-else)
  - A thermostat that turns on at 68°F → not really AI
- **AI:** learning patterns from data and making predictions
  - A Nest thermostat that *learns* your schedule → AI

**Key question:** Where is the line?

**Visual:** Side-by-side comparison — rule-based flowchart vs. learned model

*Presenter: "This is a real debate in the field. Some people say AI is just fancy automation. Others say there's a meaningful difference when a system learns. How would you draw the line?"*

---

## Slide 9 — The Benefits Side

**Heading:** When AI Helps: The Good Stuff

**Body:**
- Doctors catching cancer earlier with AI-assisted scans
- Navigation apps preventing millions of hours of gridlock
- Voice assistants making smartphones accessible to people who can't type
- Spam filters protecting inboxes from billions of daily attacks
- Language translation connecting people across 100+ languages

**Visual:** Split icons — medical cross, map pin, accessibility icon, shield, speech bubbles

*Presenter: "Before we get critical, let's acknowledge: this stuff is genuinely useful. AI is solving real problems. Keep these examples in mind when we get to the risk side."*

---

## Slide 10 — The Risk Side

**Heading:** When AI Gets It Wrong — Or Gets Used Wrong

**Body:**
- **Bias:** AI trained on unfair data produces unfair decisions (hiring, lending, policing)
- **Surveillance:** AI-powered facial recognition in public spaces
- **Addiction design:** Recommendation algorithms optimizing for engagement, not wellbeing
- **Privacy:** Your data trains models you have no control over
- **Misinformation:** AI-generated text and deepfakes spread faster than corrections

**Visual:** Warning/caution icons with each point

*Presenter: "These aren't hypothetical. All of these are documented cases happening now. The question isn't whether to worry — it's what to do about it."*

## Slide 11 — The One You'd Miss Most
