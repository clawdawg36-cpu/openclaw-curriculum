# Lab 1.3b — Customization Guide: Make Your OpenClaw Yours

> **Lab:** 1.3b — Deploy Your Own OpenClaw
> **Use after:** Completing the Setup Guide
> **Time:** 20–30 minutes

---

You've got your OpenClaw running. Now let's make it actually feel like *yours.* This guide walks you through naming it, setting its personality, and writing your very first system prompt. By the end, your assistant will feel like a completely different (and way cooler) version of the default.

---

## 1. Give It a Name 🏷️

Your assistant has a name right now — probably something generic like "Assistant" or whatever you typed during setup. Time to change that.

**How to rename your assistant:**

1. Go to **Settings** (the ⚙️ gear icon or your profile picture).
2. Look for "Assistant Name" or "Your AI's Name."
3. Type the name you want to use.
4. Click **Save**.

**What makes a good name?**
- Something short and easy to remember (1–2 words is perfect)
- Something that feels like *you* — a nickname, a character you like, a made-up word
- Don't overthink it — you can always change it later!

**Examples from other students:**
- Bolt
- Pixel
- Clawdawg Jr.
- Nova
- Biscuit (yes, really)

> **Write your assistant's name in your notebook.** You'll need it for the checklist.

---

## 2. Set a Personality 🎭

Your assistant can have a personality — a general vibe that shapes how it talks to you. Think about what kind of helper you want:

| Personality | What It Means | Good For |
|---|---|---|
| **Friendly** | Warm, encouraging, lots of positive energy | When you want to feel supported |
| **Focused** | Direct, no fluff, just the facts | Homework help, getting things done |
| **Creative** | Imaginative, suggests wild ideas, loves brainstorming | Art, writing, coming up with projects |
| **Curious** | Asks you questions back, loves to explore ideas | Debates, thinking through problems |
| **Mix it up** | A blend of two styles | Why not? |

**How to set a personality:**
1. You'll do this in your system prompt (see the next section — they're connected!).
2. Some apps have a personality selector — if yours does, explore it.
3. Otherwise, you'll describe the personality in your system prompt below.

## 3. Your First System Prompt 📝

### What Is a System Prompt? (Plain Language Version)

Okay, here's the deal. When you talk to your AI assistant, it sees two kinds of messages:

1. **Your messages** — what you type in the chat
2. **The system prompt** — secret instructions that run in the background *before* every conversation

Think of the system prompt like a note you tape to the inside of the robot's head before you turn it on. It says things like:
- *"Your name is Nova."*
- *"You are friendly and love helping with science questions."*
- *"Always answer in short paragraphs."*

The AI reads these instructions first, then uses them as its "personality settings" every time it responds to you.

**The cool part:** You're the one writing it. You're programming your AI — just using plain English instead of code.

---

### Write Your First System Prompt

Here's a simple template to get you started. Fill in the blanks:

```
Your name is [NAME].

You are a personal AI assistant for a [GRADE]-grade student named [YOUR NAME].

Your personality is [PICK ONE OR TWO: friendly / focused / creative / curious / encouraging].

You love helping with [LIST 2-3 THINGS YOU WANT HELP WITH, like: homework, writing, science questions, creative ideas].

When you answer, [ADD A STYLE RULE: keep it short / use bullet points / explain things simply / ask me a follow-up question].
```

**Example (what a student wrote):**
> Your name is Nova.
>
> You are a personal AI assistant for a 7th-grade student named Jordan.
>
> Your personality is creative and encouraging. You love helping with writing, brainstorming story ideas, and figuring out hard math problems.
>
> When you answer, keep things short and use examples I can picture in my head. If I'm stuck, ask me one helpful question to get me unstuck.

---

### How to Add Your System Prompt

1. Go to **Settings** → look for "System Prompt," "Instructions," or "Custom Instructions."
2. Paste or type your system prompt into the text box.
3. Click **Save**.

> **Don't see it?** Some versions of the app put it under "Advanced Settings" or "About My Assistant." Ask your teacher if you can't find it.

---

## 4. Save and Test 🧪

You've done the setup. Now test it!

1. **Start a new conversation** (look for a "New Chat" button or refresh the page).
2. Type: `Hey, introduce yourself.`
3. Read the response. Does it use the name you gave it? Does the personality come through?

**If yes:** 🎉 It's working!

**If no:** Go back to Settings and double-check that you saved your system prompt. Sometimes you need to start a brand-new chat for the changes to kick in.

**Try one more test:**
- Ask it to help you with something you actually need help with right now — homework, an idea, a question you've had.
- Notice: does it *feel* different from before you customized it?

## 5. Ideas for Making It Uniquely Yours 💡

You've got the basics. Here are some ideas to push further — try one or save them for later:

### Level 1 (Easy)
- Add a fun fact your assistant knows about you: `You know that I love [soccer / drawing / dogs / etc.]`
- Give it a catchphrase: `End every response with a short encouraging note.`
- Make it ask you questions: `After answering, always ask me one follow-up question.`

### Level 2 (Medium)
- Add a subject specialty: `You are an expert at explaining science concepts to middle schoolers.`
- Change its tone for different contexts: `When I ask for homework help, be focused and clear. When I just want to talk, be casual and fun.`
- Give it a backstory: `You were created by a 7th grader who wants to understand how AI works.`

### Level 3 (Advanced)
- Add rules about what it should and shouldn't do: `Never just give me the answer to a math problem — always explain the steps.`
- Give it a teaching personality: `If I get something wrong, gently correct me and explain why.`
- Chain multiple instructions: `You love analogies. Whenever you explain something hard, compare it to something from everyday life.`

---

> **Remember:** This is YOUR assistant. There's no wrong answer here.
> The best system prompts are written by people who know exactly what they want — and you're the expert on yourself.
