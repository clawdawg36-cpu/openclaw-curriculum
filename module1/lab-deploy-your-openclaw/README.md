# Lab 1.3b — Deploy Your Own OpenClaw

> **Module:** 1 — What Is AI?
> **Position:** Between Lesson 1.3 (First Contact) and Lesson 1.4 (The Turing Test)
> **Duration:** 1–2 class periods (~50 min each)
> **Format:** Hands-on lab (individual + optional partner work)

---

## Lab Overview

This is the hands-on deployment lab at the heart of Module 1. Students don't just learn *about* AI in this lab — they set up, configure, and personalize their own AI assistant. Every student leaves with a working OpenClaw instance they own and can customize throughout the rest of the course.

This lab is one of the most distinctive elements of the curriculum: instead of treating AI as something that happens *to* students, it positions them as AI operators who make intentional choices about how their assistant behaves.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab, students will be able to:

- **Deploy** a personal AI assistant by completing the account creation and setup process independently
- **Configure** basic settings (name, personality, system prompt) to customize assistant behavior
- **Explain** what a system prompt is and how it shapes AI responses using plain language
- **Evaluate** AI responses critically, noticing when they reflect — or don't reflect — their configuration choices
- **Reflect** on the difference between *using* an AI tool and *deploying* one

```
Lesson 1.1 — What Is AI?
Lesson 1.2 — AI in Your World
Lesson 1.3 — First Contact (students use OpenClaw for the first time)
  ↓
► LAB 1.3b — Deploy Your Own OpenClaw  ◄  (THIS LAB)
  ↓
Lesson 1.4 — The Turing Test
...
```

**Why here?** Lesson 1.3 ("First Contact") gives students their first experience *talking* to an AI. Lab 1.3b takes the logical next step: *what does it mean to have your own AI?* By the time students reach Lesson 1.4 and the Turing Test discussion, they have personal stakes in the question — they've deployed something, they understand the configuration choices behind it, and they're ready to ask deeper questions about intelligence, identity, and how AI actually works.

## Implementation Notes

### First-Time Teachers

- **Read `teacher-setup-guide.md` completely** before running this lab. The setup logistics are the hardest part.
- Run through the full student experience yourself on the same device type your students will use.
- The biggest pitfall: students who can't create an account slow the whole class down. Pre-make backup accounts.

### Pacing Tips

- **Period 1:** Account creation + first conversation. Don't rush to customization — getting everyone logged in is the win for Day 1.
- **Period 2:** System prompt + verification activity. This is where the "magic" happens.
- **Early finishers:** Direct them to Level 2 and Level 3 customization ideas in `customization-guide.md`.

### What Success Looks Like

Every student:
1. Has a working account they can log into
2. Has had at least one full conversation with their assistant
3. Has given their assistant a name
4. Has written a system prompt (even a short one)
5. Can show you their assistant introducing itself

### What to Do If You Run Short on Time

If you only have one period:
- Cut the customization guide to: name only + one-sentence personality line in the system prompt
- Use a pre-written system prompt template (see `customization-guide.md` — the template is ready to go)
- Run the verification activity as the last 10 minutes

## Connection to Later Modules

This lab doesn't end when the period ends. The assistant students set up here is their **companion for the entire curriculum**:

| Module | How Students Use Their OpenClaw |
|---|---|
| Module 1 (this module) | First setup; learn what configuration does |
| Module 2 — How AI Learns | Experiment with different prompts; observe how responses change |
| Module 3 — Building with AI | Use their assistant as a collaborator on project work |
| Module 4 — AI and Society | Ask their assistant ethical questions; compare its reasoning to their own |
| Modules 5–7 | Ongoing personal AI literacy tool — research, writing, reflection |

Students who put more care into their setup in this lab often get more out of later labs. This is worth mentioning to them.

## Assessment

This lab uses the **Deployment Checklist** (`deployment-checklist.md`) as its primary assessment artifact. It is designed as a completion grade rather than a rubric grade — the goal is ensuring every student has a working setup, not evaluating the quality of their system prompt.

Optional additional assessment:
- Include the "Student Reflection" section of the checklist as a formative writing grade
- Use the "introduce yourself" share-out as a speaking/listening observation
- Have students screenshot their system prompt and submit via your LMS
