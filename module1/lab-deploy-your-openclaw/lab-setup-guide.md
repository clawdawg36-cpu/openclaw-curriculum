# Lab 1.3b — Student Setup Guide: Deploy Your Own OpenClaw

> **Lab:** 1.3b — Deploy Your Own OpenClaw
> **Time:** 1–2 class periods (or as homework after the first period)
> **For:** Students

---

## 1. What You're About to Do 🚀

Get ready, because this is one of the coolest things you'll do in this class. By the end of this lab, you won't just be *using* an AI — you'll have *deployed* your own personal AI assistant. That means you'll have your own version of OpenClaw: an AI that you set up, you name, and you customize to work exactly the way you want. Think of it like getting your own pet robot — except it lives on your phone or computer, helps you think through problems, answers questions, and learns your style the more you use it. You're not a user anymore. You're an AI operator. Let's go.

---

## 2. What You Need Before Starting

Before you dive in, make sure you have these things ready:

- [ ] A device with internet access (phone, tablet, laptop, or school computer)
- [ ] An email address you can access (or ask your teacher about a shared class account)
- [ ] About 20–30 minutes of focused time
- [ ] Your notebook or a place to write down a few things
- [ ] Your teacher's setup code or invite link (your teacher will give you this)

> **Don't have one of these?** No worries — tell your teacher before you start. There are backup options for everyone. See the Troubleshooting section at the end.

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Setup

Follow each step in order. Don't skip ahead!

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Go to the URL your teacher gives you (or open the OpenClaw app if it's already installed).
2. Click **"Sign Up"** or **"Create Account"**.
3. Enter your email address and create a password.
   - Use a password you'll remember but won't forget (write it in your notebook!)
   - If you're using a school email, make sure you have access to it
4. Check your email for a verification link and click it.
5. Come back to the app and log in.

> **Screenshot placeholder:** [Image: Sign-up screen with arrows pointing to email and password fields]

### Step 2: Basic Configuration

Once you're logged in, you'll land on the setup screen. Here's what to do:

1. You'll be asked to give your assistant a name. For now, enter something temporary like "My Assistant" — we'll change this in the Customization Guide.
2. Choose a language (pick the one you're most comfortable in).
3. Select your grade level if asked.
4. Click **"Save"** or **"Next"**.

> **Screenshot placeholder:** [Image: Setup screen with name field, language selector]

### Step 3: Your First Test Conversation

Time to say hello! This is the moment you've been waiting for.

1. Find the chat box at the bottom of the screen — it might say "Type a message…"
2. Type this exact message: `Hello! Tell me a little bit about yourself.`
3. Press **Enter** or tap the send button (it usually looks like a paper airplane ✈️).
4. Wait a few seconds and read the response.

**What did it say?** Write down one sentence from its response in your notebook. You'll need this for the verification activity.

> **Tip:** If nothing happens after 10 seconds, refresh the page and try again. If it still doesn't work, raise your hand.

### Step 4: Have a Real Conversation

Now try asking it a few real questions. Here are some ideas — try at least 2:

- "What is machine learning?"
- "Help me come up with a fun idea for a school project."
- "Explain photosynthesis like I'm 10 years old."
- Ask it anything you're genuinely curious about!

Notice how it responds. Does it sound helpful? Confusing? Surprising? Jot down one observation.

### Step 5: Explore the Settings

Before you move on, take 2 minutes to look around:

1. Find the **Settings** menu (usually a gear icon ⚙️ or your profile picture).
2. Notice what options are available — don't change anything yet, just explore.
3. Find where the assistant's name is displayed.

> You'll use these settings in the Customization Guide (the next file in this lab).

## 4. Troubleshooting Tips

Something not working? Try these fixes first before asking for help.

| Problem | Try This |
|---|---|
| "I didn't get a verification email" | Check your spam/junk folder. Wait 2 minutes, then try resending. |
| "I forgot my password already" | Click "Forgot Password" on the login screen and check your email. |
| "The page just loads forever" | Refresh the page. Try a different browser (Chrome works best). |
| "I don't have my own email" | Ask your teacher — they have shared class accounts ready to go. |
| "It gave a really weird or wrong answer" | That's actually normal sometimes! AI can make mistakes. Write it down — it's good data. |
| "I can't type in the chat box" | Make sure you're logged in. Try clicking directly on the chat box first. |
| "Nothing on screen makes sense" | Ask a neighbor or raise your hand. You're not the first person to be confused. |

## 5. "You Did It!" Verification ✅

You're not done until your OpenClaw can introduce itself. Here's how to prove you set it up successfully:

### The Verification Challenge

1. Go to your chat with your OpenClaw assistant.
2. Type this message: `Introduce yourself to my class. Keep it to 3 sentences.`
3. Read the response.
4. **If it introduces itself:** You're done! Screenshot it or be ready to share it with your teacher.
5. **If it says something weird or unhelpful:** Try again, or check the Troubleshooting section.

### Share Out (if your teacher asks)

Be ready to share:
- Your assistant's current name (even if it's just "My Assistant" for now)
- One thing it said that surprised you
- One thing you want to customize about it
