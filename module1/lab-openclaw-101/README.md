# Lab — OpenClaw 101

**Module:** 1 — What Is AI? — Meet Your Digital Brain  
**Used In:** Lesson 1.3 (integrated), can also be assigned as standalone lab  
**Duration:** 45–60 minutes  
**Format:** Individual, device required (OpenClaw access)

## Overview

A structured 10-challenge exploration lab where students systematically probe OpenClaw's capabilities and limitations. Each challenge is a different type of interaction — factual, creative, logical, emotional, trick questions — designed to build a complete mental model of what AI can and can't do. Students document both their prompts and the AI's responses, then reflect on each challenge.

## The 10 Challenges

| # | Challenge Type | What Students Explore |
|---|---------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Factual question | Knowledge accuracy + how to verify |
| 2 | Creative request (haiku) | Creative generation + syllable awareness |
| 3 | Opinion question | How AI handles subjectivity |
| 4 | Multi-step planning task | Complex instruction following |
| 5 | Trick/gotcha question | AI limitations and self-awareness |
| 6 | Collaborative story | Turn-taking and narrative continuity |
| 7 | Math/logic problem | Step-by-step reasoning |
| 8 | Debate both sides | Argumentation and balance |
| 9 | Summarize this | Compression and comprehension |
| 10 | Limits test | Ethical guardrails and refusals |

## Notes

- Ensure all students have active OpenClaw access before the lab begins
- Challenge 5 (trick question) often generates the most class discussion — save time for it
- Fast finishers: send to the 4 extension challenges in `lab-guide.md`
- This lab works well as a take-home assignment for classes with limited device time
