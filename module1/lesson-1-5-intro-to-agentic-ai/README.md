# Lesson 1.5 — Intro to Agentic AI

**Module:** 1 — What Is AI and Why Does It Matter?
**Duration:** ~50 minutes (1 class period)
**Grade Level:** 6th–8th
**Placement:** After Lab 1.3b | Last lesson of Module 1 | Bridge to Module 2

---

## Overview

This lesson introduces students to the concept of **agentic AI** — AI systems that don't just answer questions, but take actions in the world on your behalf. Coming immediately after students deploy their own personal AI assistant in Lab 1.3b, this lesson reframes that tool as an *agent* and sets the conceptual stage for the rest of the curriculum.

This is an **introductory** treatment — a seed, not the full harvest. Students who want to go deeper will find it in Module 6, where they build their own agentic workflows.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will:

1. Define "AI agent" and distinguish it from a simple chatbot
2. Apply the **Perceive → Reason → Act** loop to real-world examples
3. Identify at least 3 real-world agentic AI systems
4. Reflect on their deployed OpenClaw as an agent
5. Articulate one benefit and one risk of autonomous AI

## Teaching Notes

- The **ClawDawg Kanban worker** example is a highlight — this is a real agent that created this lesson. Students find it relatable and slightly mind-bending.
- The **"What would YOUR agent do?"** activity generates great discussion and reveals students' values around privacy, trust, and automation.
- For **"will AI take over?"** anxieties: see `teacher-notes.md` for constructive framing that validates concerns without stoking fear.
- This lesson works without any tech — it's discussion and paper-based. Optional: live demo using teacher's OpenClaw.
