# Companion Textbook — Chapter 1: What Is AI?

**Module:** 1 — What Is AI? — Meet Your Digital Brain  
**Reading Level:** Grades 6–8 (Flesch-Kincaid 6th–7th grade)  
**Length:** ~440 lines (~22KB) — full first draft

## Overview

Chapter 1 of the *AI Explorers* companion textbook. Written in a conversational, engaging tone designed to feel more like a magazine article than a traditional textbook. Opens with a narrative hook ("Maya's Monday Morning") and builds through history, concepts, and hands-on OpenClaw interactions.

## Chapter Structure

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Opening Hook | "Maya's Monday Morning" — a day-in-the-life narrative showing AI everywhere |
| What Is Intelligence? | Philosophical warm-up, accessible for 6th graders |
| What Is AI? | Definition, history timeline (1950s–2020s), key milestones |
| AI in Everyday Life | Relatable examples across 6 categories |
| AI vs. Regular Software | Comparison table with clear distinctions |
| Meet OpenClaw | Introduction to the tool students will use all year |
| Chapter Summary | Key takeaways + vocabulary table |
| OpenClaw Challenges | 3 hands-on exercises students do with OpenClaw |
| Review Questions | 5 comprehension questions |
| Going Further | Enrichment links and extension activities |

## Special Features

- **"AI in the Wild" sidebars** (4×): Turing Test, AlphaGo's Move 37, AI in healthcare, AI as a writing partner
- **"OpenClaw Challenge" boxes** (3×): Hands-on exercises integrated throughout the chapter
