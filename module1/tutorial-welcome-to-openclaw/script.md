# Script: Welcome to OpenClaw
**Module 1 — Online Tutorial**
**Target duration:** ~5 minutes (300 seconds)
**Format:** Screen-recorded narration with on-screen callouts

---

## [00:00–00:30] INTRO — Hook & Welcome

*[Narrator speaks over animated OpenClaw logo / title card]*

> "Hey, welcome. If you've ever wished you had a smart, always-available assistant that could help you write, research, plan, answer questions, or just think through a problem — that's exactly what we're building together here."

> "I'm going to walk you through OpenClaw: what it is, how to use it well, and — just as importantly — how to use it *safely*. This is your starting point. Let's go."

**[On-screen text]:** "Welcome to OpenClaw — Your AI assistant, done right."

---

## [00:30–01:00] WHAT IS OPENCLAW?

*[Screen shows OpenClaw interface with subtle callouts pointing to key areas]*

> "OpenClaw is a personal AI assistant that runs on your own hardware — or a server you control. That's a big deal. Unlike big consumer AI services where your conversations flow through a company's cloud, OpenClaw is yours."

> "Under the hood, it uses powerful language models — the same kind of technology behind tools like ChatGPT or Claude. But the key difference is *who's in control*. You are."

> "It can help with almost anything: answering questions, summarizing articles, writing emails, debugging code, planning your week — you name it."

**[On-screen callout]:** "Runs on your hardware. Your data stays with you."

---

## [01:00–01:30] STARTING A CONVERSATION

*[Screen shows conversation input box, cursor blinking]*

> "Starting a conversation is as simple as typing. Click into the chat box at the bottom of the screen, type what you need, and hit Enter — or tap Send."

> "You can start with something casual: 'What's a good recipe for lemon chicken?' Or something more complex: 'Help me draft a professional email declining a job offer.' There's no special syntax required — just talk to it like you'd talk to a knowledgeable friend."

> "One tip right off the bat: the more context you give, the better the response. Compare 'Write me an email' to 'Write me a short, professional email to my landlord asking for an extension on next month's rent because of unexpected medical bills.' See the difference? Context is king."

**[On-screen callout]:** "More context = better answers."

---

## [01:30–02:00] TYPES OF QUESTIONS TO ASK

*[Screen shows split panel: examples of good prompts on the left, categories on the right]*

> "OpenClaw handles an incredibly wide range of tasks. Let's walk through the main categories."

> "**Research and learning:** 'Explain quantum computing in plain English.' 'What were the main causes of World War I?' 'Summarize this article for me.'"

> "**Writing and editing:** 'Proofread this paragraph.' 'Rewrite this sentence to sound more confident.' 'Draft a thank-you note for a job interview.'"

> "**Planning and productivity:** 'Help me make a weekly meal plan.' 'Break down this project into smaller tasks.' 'What are some strategies for managing work-life balance?'"

> "**Coding and tech:** 'Why is my Python script throwing this error?' 'Explain what this function does.' 'Write a bash command to rename all files in a folder.'"

> "**Creative work:** 'Help me brainstorm names for my podcast.' 'Write a short story about a robot who learns to cook.' 'Give me five tagline ideas for my small business.'"

**[On-screen text]:** "Research · Writing · Planning · Coding · Creative"

---

## [02:00–02:30] PRIVACY BASICS — WHAT NOT TO SHARE

*[Screen shows a friendly "caution" visual — not alarming, just clear]*

> "Now let's talk about something important: privacy. Even though OpenClaw runs locally and is designed with your privacy in mind, there are still things you should *never* type into any AI assistant."

> "**Never share:** Social Security numbers, credit card or bank account numbers, passwords or PINs, government ID numbers, or anyone else's private information without their consent."

> "Think of it like texting a very smart friend — they might keep it confidential, but you wouldn't text your credit card number to *anyone*. Same principle here."

> "Also worth knowing: if your OpenClaw instance is connected to any cloud services or external models, those conversations may be processed off-device. Always check your settings and know your configuration."

**[On-screen callout]:** "🔒 Never share: SSNs · Passwords · Financial details · Others' private info"

---

## [02:30–03:00] PRIVACY BASICS — WHAT'S SAFE (AND SMART)

*[Screen shows examples of good, safe prompts]*

> "Here's what's totally fine and actually great to use AI for: general questions, anonymized scenarios, your own ideas, your own writing for editing, publicly available information, and personal planning where you don't need to include sensitive data."

> "If you're working on something sensitive — say, a work document — you can describe it without pasting in confidential content. 'Help me structure a performance review' is just as useful as pasting in the whole document, and much safer."

> "The golden rule: if you wouldn't put it on a sticky note and leave it on your desk at work, don't put it in the chat."

**[On-screen callout]:** "Share the *shape* of the problem, not the sensitive details."

---

## [03:00–03:45] EVALUATING AI RESPONSES CRITICALLY

*[Screen shows an AI response with light annotation callouts]*

> "This is probably the most important section of this whole tutorial. AI assistants are powerful — but they are not infallible. You need to think of OpenClaw as a brilliant first draft, not a final authority."

> "AI can *hallucinate* — that's the technical term for when it confidently states something that's just... wrong. It might give you a fake citation, a made-up statistic, or a subtly incorrect historical fact — all delivered with total confidence."

> "So here's your critical evaluation checklist:"

> "**One: Does it sound right?** Trust your gut. If something seems off, it probably deserves a second look."

> "**Two: Can you verify it?** For anything factual — especially medical, legal, or financial — cross-check with a reliable source. Don't take the AI's word for it."

> "**Three: Is it relevant to *your* situation?** AI gives general answers. Your life has specific details. Make sure the response actually fits your context."

> "**Four: Did it answer what you actually asked?** Sometimes AI drifts or answers a slightly different question. Re-read your prompt and the response together."

> "**Five: When in doubt, ask a follow-up.** 'Are you sure about that?' or 'Can you cite your source?' often surfaces uncertainty or corrections."

**[On-screen callout]:** "AI = brilliant first draft. You = final authority."

---

## [03:45–04:15] PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER — LIVE DEMO WALKTHROUGH

*[Screen shows live OpenClaw interface — narrator types a real example prompt]*

> "Let's put this all together with a quick real-world example. Say I want to plan a weekend trip."

> *[Types: 'I have a free weekend in late March and I'm based in Los Angeles. I love hiking and good food. Suggest a 2-day trip itinerary.']*

> "Notice I gave it context: my location, my timeframe, my interests. Now watch the response come in…"

> *[Response appears on screen]*

> "Pretty solid starting point! Now I'd apply my critical lens: Does this look right? Are these real places? Does it fit what I actually asked? In this case, yes — but I'd still do a quick Google check on any specifics before booking anything."

> "That's the workflow: give good context, review critically, verify what matters."

**[On-screen callout]:** "Context → Response → Critical review → Verify if needed"

---

## [04:15–04:45] QUICK TIPS FOR GETTING MORE OUT OF OPENCLAW

*[Screen shows a clean "tips" card]*

> "Before we wrap up, here are five quick tips for getting the most out of OpenClaw:"

> "**One: Iterate.** If the first response isn't quite right, refine your prompt. 'Make it shorter.' 'More formal.' 'Focus just on the cost aspect.' Treat it like a conversation."

> "**Two: Use it for brainstorming.** Even if you don't use what it gives you, it's great for generating ideas to react to."

> "**Three: Give it a role.** 'You are a friendly doctor explaining things to a patient' changes the whole tone and depth of a health question."

> "**Four: Ask for formats.** 'Give me this as a bullet list.' 'Summarize in three sentences.' 'Write this as a table.' Outputs are flexible."

> "**Five: Be honest when you're not sure.** If you don't know enough about a topic to evaluate the AI's answer, say so in your prompt — 'I'm a beginner, please keep it simple and flag if you're unsure about anything.'"

**[On-screen text]:** "Iterate · Brainstorm · Set a role · Request format · Be honest"

> "In the next module, we'll go deeper into writing effective prompts. But for now? Go try it. Ask it something you've been curious about. See what happens."
