# Activity — AI or Not AI? Sorting Cards

**Module:** 1 — What Is AI? — Meet Your Digital Brain  
**Used In:** Lesson 1.1 (primary), can also be used as a warm-up in any Module 1 lesson  
**Duration:** 15–20 minutes  
**Format:** Physical card sort or digital drag-and-drop

## Overview

A 30-card sorting activity where students categorize technologies as either AI or not AI. Cards cover familiar everyday technologies — some obvious, some surprising. The activity sparks discussion about what actually makes something "AI" and challenges common misconceptions (e.g., "if it's digital, it must be AI").

## Card Set

**30 cards total:**
- **15 AI examples:** Google Maps routing, Netflix recommendations, spam filter, face unlock, Siri/voice assistants, autocorrect, fraud detection, AlphaFold, self-driving cars, grammar check, image search, translation, ad targeting, medical diagnosis AI, chess engine
- **15 Non-AI examples:** Calculator, basic thermostat, timer-based traffic light, ATM, barcode scanner, smoke alarm, toothbrush timer, vending machine, elevator buttons, digital clock, TV remote, microwave, washing machine cycle, basic GPS turn-by-turn, dictionary spellcheck

Each card includes front (name + emoji + description) and back (✅ AI or ❌ Not AI + explanation).

## Tips

- Run as a **think-pair-share**: students sort individually, then compare with a partner before class debrief
- The most interesting cards are the tricky non-AI ones (thermostat, ATM) — spend extra time on those
- Works great as a **warm-up** or **exit activity** on any Module 1 day
