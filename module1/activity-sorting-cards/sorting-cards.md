# AI or Not AI? — Sorting Cards

**Activity:** Students sort 30 cards into two piles: ✅ AI or ❌ Not AI

---

## How to Use

- Print and cut out each card (or use the digital version)
- **Front** faces up during sorting; **Back** reveals the answer
- Cards are numbered 1–30 (Cards 1–15 = AI; Cards 16–30 = Not AI)

---

## ✅ AI Cards (1–15)

---

### Card 1 — Google Maps Routing

**Front**

🗺️ **Google Maps Routing**
Figures out the fastest route to your destination in real time, adjusting for traffic and road conditions.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
It uses machine learning to predict traffic patterns and continuously optimize routes based on real-world data — not just fixed rules.

---

### Card 2 — Netflix Recommendations

**Front**

🎬 **Netflix Recommendations**
Suggests movies and shows you might like based on what you've watched before.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
A recommendation engine uses machine learning to find patterns in viewing history and match them to other users with similar tastes.

---

### Card 3 — Spam Filter

**Front**

📧 **Email Spam Filter**
Automatically moves suspicious or junk emails to your spam folder before you ever see them.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Modern spam filters use machine learning to recognize patterns in millions of emails and improve over time as new spam tactics emerge.

---

### Card 4 — Face Unlock

**Front**

🔓 **Face Unlock**
Unlocks your phone by recognizing your face — even in different lighting or with glasses on.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Face recognition uses deep learning neural networks trained on thousands of facial images to identify a specific person reliably.

---

### Card 5 — Siri

**Front**

🎙️ **Siri (Voice Assistant)**
Listens to your voice, understands what you're asking, and responds or takes action on your behalf.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Siri combines speech recognition and natural language processing — both AI techniques — to understand and respond to natural speech.

---

### Card 6 — Autocorrect

**Front**

⌨️ **Autocorrect**
Automatically fixes typos and spelling mistakes as you type on your phone or computer.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Modern autocorrect uses language models that learn from context, not just a dictionary — predicting what word you most likely meant to type.

---

### Card 7 — Fraud Detection

**Front**

💳 **Credit Card Fraud Detection**
Flags suspicious purchases on your credit card and alerts you or blocks the transaction automatically.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Banks use machine learning models trained on millions of transactions to spot unusual behavior that differs from your normal spending patterns.

---

### Card 8 — AlphaFold

**Front**

🧬 **AlphaFold (Protein Folding)**
Predicts the 3D shape of proteins from their amino acid sequence — a problem scientists struggled with for 50 years.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
AlphaFold uses deep learning to predict how proteins fold, a task so complex it couldn't be solved with traditional programming. It's one of AI's biggest scientific breakthroughs.

---

### Card 9 — Self-Driving Cars

**Front**

🚗 **Self-Driving Cars**
Navigates roads, avoids obstacles, and drives passengers to destinations without a human driver.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Self-driving systems use computer vision, sensor fusion, and reinforcement learning to perceive the environment and make real-time driving decisions.

---

### Card 10 — Grammar Check

**Front**

✍️ **AI Grammar Check (e.g., Grammarly)**
Goes beyond spell check — suggests rewriting full sentences for clarity, tone, and style.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Advanced grammar tools use large language models to understand meaning and context, not just apply fixed grammatical rules.

---

### Card 11 — Image Search

**Front**

🔍 **Reverse Image Search**
Upload a photo and find similar images, identify objects, or find the original source on the web.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Image search uses convolutional neural networks to extract visual features from images and find matches — far beyond simple pixel comparison.

---

### Card 12 — Translation

**Front**

🌐 **AI Translation (e.g., Google Translate)**
Translates text or speech between languages with natural, context-aware results.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Neural machine translation uses deep learning trained on billions of translated sentences, learning grammar and meaning across languages automatically.

---

### Card 13 — Ad Targeting

**Front**

📱 **Targeted Advertising**
Shows you ads for things you've recently searched for or seem likely to want based on your behavior online.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Ad platforms use machine learning to analyze browsing patterns, demographics, and past purchases to predict which ads are most likely to lead to a click or purchase.

---

### Card 14 — Medical Diagnosis AI

**Front**

🏥 **AI Medical Diagnosis**
Analyzes X-rays, MRI scans, or patient data to help doctors detect diseases like cancer earlier and more accurately.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Medical AI uses deep learning models trained on thousands of labeled scans to identify abnormalities — sometimes matching or exceeding specialist accuracy.

---

### Card 15 — Game AI (Chess Engine)

**Front**

♟️ **Chess Engine (e.g., Stockfish, AlphaZero)**
Plays chess at a superhuman level, evaluating millions of positions and choosing the best move.

**Back**

✅ **AI**
Chess engines — especially AlphaZero — use machine learning (specifically reinforcement learning) to teach themselves chess by playing millions of games, discovering strategies no human programmed.

## ❌ Not AI Cards (16–30)

---

### Card 16 — Calculator

**Front**

🧮 **Calculator**
Takes numbers you enter and performs math operations like addition, multiplication, and division.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A calculator follows fixed arithmetic rules — it doesn't learn, adapt, or make decisions. Every calculation is deterministic and rule-based.

---

### Card 17 — Basic Thermostat

**Front**

🌡️ **Basic Thermostat**
Turns the heat on when the temperature drops below a set point, and turns it off when the temperature is reached.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A basic thermostat uses a simple if/then rule: "if temp < X, turn on heat." It doesn't learn your schedule or adapt to patterns — that's just a conditional.

---

### Card 18 — Timer Traffic Light

**Front**

🚦 **Timer-Based Traffic Light**
Changes between red, yellow, and green on a fixed time schedule, regardless of how many cars are waiting.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A timer traffic light runs on a preset schedule. It has no sensors, makes no decisions, and cannot adapt — just a simple repeating loop.

---

### Card 19 — ATM Cash Dispenser

**Front**

🏧 **ATM Cash Dispenser**
Lets you withdraw cash from your bank account by entering your PIN and selecting an amount.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
An ATM follows strict programmed steps: verify PIN, check balance, dispense cash. It applies fixed rules with no learning or pattern recognition involved.

---

### Card 20 — Barcode Scanner

**Front**

📦 **Barcode Scanner**
Reads the black-and-white lines of a barcode and looks up the matching product in a database.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A barcode scanner uses a laser or light sensor to decode a specific pattern and look up a matching value — it's a deterministic lookup, not pattern recognition or learning.

---

### Card 21 — Smoke Alarm

**Front**

🔥 **Smoke Alarm**
Sounds a loud alarm when it detects smoke particles in the air above a certain threshold.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A smoke alarm uses a simple sensor: "if smoke particles detected above threshold, trigger alarm." No learning, no adaptation — just a threshold trigger.

---

### Card 22 — Electric Toothbrush Timer

**Front**

🪥 **Electric Toothbrush Timer**
Buzzes every 30 seconds to remind you to switch quadrants while brushing your teeth.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
The timer is a simple countdown: buzz at 30s, 60s, 90s, 120s. It has no sensors, no decisions, and no learning — just a fixed interval loop.

---

### Card 23 — Vending Machine

**Front**

🥤 **Vending Machine**
Accepts money, lets you choose a product, and dispenses it when you press a button.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A vending machine follows a fixed sequence: check payment, check stock, dispense item. It doesn't learn preferences or adapt behavior — pure rule execution.

---

### Card 24 — Elevator Buttons

**Front**

🛗 **Elevator Buttons**
Press a floor button, the elevator travels to that floor and opens its doors.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
Basic elevator controls follow simple logic: go to the requested floor, open doors on arrival. No learning or prediction involved — though some modern elevators do use AI for dispatch optimization!

---

### Card 25 — Digital Clock

**Front**

🕐 **Digital Clock**
Displays the current time by counting seconds since it was set.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A digital clock simply counts elapsed time using an oscillating crystal — no data, no decisions, no learning. Pure sequential counting.

---

### Card 26 — TV Remote

**Front**

📺 **TV Remote Control**
Sends an infrared signal to your TV to change channels, adjust volume, or power on/off.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A TV remote sends fixed infrared codes for each button — it's just a signal transmitter. No intelligence, learning, or adaptation involved.

---

### Card 27 — Microwave

**Front**

📡 **Microwave Oven**
Heats food by emitting microwave radiation for a set amount of time you choose.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A microwave runs for the duration you set, then stops. It doesn't learn how long to heat your food or adjust based on results — just a timed power cycle.

---

### Card 28 — Washing Machine Cycle

**Front**

🫧 **Washing Machine Cycle**
Runs through a pre-programmed sequence: fill, wash, rinse, spin — on a set timer.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
A standard washing machine cycle follows a fixed script of steps and timings. It doesn't sense how dirty the clothes are or adapt the wash — that's just a programmed sequence. (Smart washers do use sensors, but basic ones don't.)

---

### Card 29 — Basic GPS Turn-by-Turn

**Front**

📍 **Basic GPS Turn-by-Turn Navigation**
Calculates a route from A to B and gives step-by-step directions using a fixed road map.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
Classic GPS navigation uses a shortest-path algorithm (like Dijkstra's) on a static map. It doesn't learn from traffic patterns or improve over time — that's algorithmic routing, not AI.

---

### Card 30 — Dictionary Spellcheck

**Front**

📖 **Dictionary Spellcheck**
Underlines words that don't match any entry in a built-in word list.

**Back**

❌ **Not AI**
Basic spellcheck is a dictionary lookup: is this word in the list? Yes/no. It doesn't understand context or learn from corrections — that's just pattern matching against a fixed database.
