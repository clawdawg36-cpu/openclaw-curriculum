# Completion Criteria — Your First OpenClaw Conversation

**Module 1 · Tutorial · tutorial-first-conversation**

---

## What "Done" Looks Like

A student has successfully completed this tutorial when they have:

1. **Completed all 6 steps** — sent at least one message to OpenClaw for each step in the walkthrough (introduce yourself, factual question, creative question, help with a task, stump-the-AI, final reflection).

2. **Written 6 reflection responses** — one inline reflection answer per step. Responses do not need to be polished or long, but they must be genuine (not copied from the tutorial examples). Minimum 2–3 sentences per reflection.

3. **Submitted their reflections** — turned in their 6 written reflections through the method their teacher specified (Google Doc, printed worksheet, LMS submission, etc.).

4. **Demonstrated critical thinking in at least 2 reflections** — at least two of the six reflections should show that the student thought about AI's capabilities *and* limitations, not just whether the response was "cool" or "weird."

---

## Minimum Bar vs. Strong Work

| Criteria | Minimum (Passing) | Strong Work |
|---|---|---|
| Steps completed | All 6 attempted | All 6 with genuine follow-up questions |
| Reflection quality | 2–3 sentences, on-topic | Shows curiosity, surprise, or critical thinking |
| Critical thinking | At least 2 reflections mention limits or trust | Multiple reflections explore the "why" behind AI behavior |
| Engagement | Followed sample prompts | Used their own original prompts; pushed past the examples |

## How Teachers Verify Completion

### Option A — Reflection Document Submission (Recommended)
Students export or copy their 6 reflection answers into a shared Google Doc, class LMS assignment, or printed worksheet and submit it. Teacher reviews for:
- Presence of all 6 reflections
- Minimum length (2–3 sentences each)
- Evidence of genuine engagement (not just repeating the sample prompts)

### Option B — Live Discussion / Exit Ticket
Teacher asks students to verbally share one of their reflections (Steps 2, 4, or 6 work well for class discussion). Students who can speak to a specific moment in the tutorial have likely completed it meaningfully.

### Option C — Partner Share-Out
Students pair up and each shares their "Final Reflection" from Step 6 (the fill-in-the-blank). Teacher circulates to spot-check. Works well for younger classes or where written submission isn't practical.

### Option D — Screenshot Portfolio (1:1 or self-paced contexts)
Students take screenshots of 2–3 of their OpenClaw conversations and annotate them with their reflections. Useful for async or at-home completion.

## Common Issues & Teacher Notes

**"The student just used the sample prompts word-for-word."**
Prompt them to go back and try Step 3 or Step 5 with something personal or original. The sample prompts are scaffolds, not scripts. You can ask: *"What's something YOU'RE actually curious about?"*

**"The student says they don't know what to write for reflections."**
Point them to the reflection question for that step. If they're still stuck, try asking: *"What did you expect? What actually happened? Was it different?"*

**"The student says AI got everything right and it was perfect."**
Encourage them to go back to Step 5 and try harder to stump it. The goal isn't admiration — it's investigation.

**"The student is concerned about privacy."**
Validate this. Remind them not to share anything personal (full name, address, passwords). This is also a great teaching moment for digital literacy.
