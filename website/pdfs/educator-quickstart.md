# 🚀 AI Explorers — Educator Quick-Start Guide

> **Goal:** Get you ready to teach Module 1 in under 30 minutes of reading.  
> No CS background needed. Seriously.

---

## What Is AI Explorers?

- **A hands-on AI curriculum** built for middle and high school students — they build, experiment, and reflect, not just read.
- **Powered by OpenClaw**, a real AI agent that students interact with directly, making abstract concepts tangible.
- **Structured in modules**, each ~3 weeks, covering how AI works, how to use it responsibly, and how to make it do things.

## Module 1 at a Glance

**Theme:** *What is AI, and how does it work?*

### Lesson Sequence

| Lesson | Title | Key Activity |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | What Is AI? | Students brainstorm where AI shows up in their daily life |
| 2 | How AI Learns | Visual demo: training data and pattern recognition |
| 3 | Talk to an Agent | First OpenClaw session — students ask it questions |
| 4 | Prompting 101 | Students experiment: how does wording change the answer? |
| 5 | The Lab | Hands-on: build a simple AI-powered task with OpenClaw |
| 6 | Reflect + Share | Groups present what they built; class discussion |

### The Lab (Lesson 5)
The lab is the centerpiece of Module 1. Students pick from 2–3 scaffolded project options and use OpenClaw to complete them. Everything is browser-based. No coding required for the base options; extension challenges exist for students who want to go deeper.

**Lab options include:**
- Build a homework helper prompt
- Create a Q&A bot for a topic they care about
- Design a "responsible AI" policy for a fake school app

## Pacing Options

Pick what fits your schedule. All options cover the same core content.

| Pace | Duration | Sessions/Week | Notes |
|------|----------|--------------|-------|
| **Standard** | 3 weeks | 3–4 | Recommended. Gives time for reflection and discussion. |
| **Sprint** | 1 week | Daily (5 days) | Great for workshops, summer programs, or intensives. Skip some extensions. |
| **Extended** | 4 weeks | 2–3 | Best for classes with less frequent meeting times or more discussion depth. |

**Tips for Sprint mode:** Focus on lessons 1, 3, 4, and 5. Combine lessons 2+6 into a single session. Cut extension challenges.

**Tips for Extended mode:** Add more discussion time to lessons 3 and 4. Let the lab run across two sessions. Incorporate a guest speaker or video.

## Top 5 Teacher Questions

**1. "Do I need to know how to code?"**  
Nope. Zero coding required to teach this curriculum. If students want to go deeper with code, the extension challenges have them covered — but you don't need to lead that.

**2. "What if OpenClaw gives a weird or wrong answer?"**  
That's actually a teaching moment! AI makes mistakes. Part of the curriculum is learning to evaluate AI outputs critically. When it happens, lean into it: "Why do you think it said that? Is that right?"

**3. "How do I manage 30 students all using OpenClaw at once?"**  
Each student has their own session — there's no shared state to worry about. For lab days, consider using pair work (one account per pair) to make discussion richer and reduce the number of simultaneous sessions.

**4. "What if a student goes off-script and asks OpenClaw something inappropriate?"**  
OpenClaw has built-in safety filters. It won't engage with harmful prompts. If something slips through, use it as a teaching moment about responsible AI use — that's literally what this curriculum is about.

**5. "Can I skip a lesson if we're behind?"**  
Yes, with care. Lessons 1, 3, and 5 (the Lab) are the backbone — don't skip those. Lessons 2, 4, and 6 can be shortened or combined if needed. The teacher guide flags which sections are essential vs. enrichment.
