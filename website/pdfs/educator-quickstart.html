AI Explorers

Educator Quick-Start Guide — everything you need to teach Module 1 with confidence.

📚 No CS background required  ·  ✅ Ready in <30 min

🤖 What Is AI Explorers?

  • 1
    A hands-on AI curriculum for middle and high school students — they build, experiment, and reflect, not just read or watch.
  • 2
    Powered by OpenClaw, a real AI agent students interact with directly — turning abstract concepts into something you can actually poke and prod.
  • 3
    Structured in modules (~3 weeks each), covering how AI works, how to use it responsibly, and how to make it actually do things.

🧰 What You Need

Hardware
Just a browser
No installs. No IT ticket. Works on any device with a modern browser.
Accounts
GitHub + OpenClaw
GitHub: free, for lesson materials. OpenClaw: provided by your school or district.
Your Time
~2 hrs before Day 1
Then ~30 min/week prep. 45–50 min class sessions, 3–5x per week.
Per Module
~3 weeks
Standard pace. Sprint and extended options also available (see Pacing).

The Lab (Lesson 5)

The lab is the centerpiece of Module 1. Students choose from scaffolded project options and use OpenClaw to complete them. Browser-based. No coding required for the base options.

Option A: Build a homework helper prompt
Option B: Create a Q&A bot for a topic they care about
Option C: Design a "responsible AI" policy for a fake school app

❓ Top 5 Teacher Questions

"Do I need to know how to code?"
Nope. Zero coding required to teach this curriculum. Extension challenges exist for students who want to go deeper, but you don't need to lead that.
"What if OpenClaw gives a weird or wrong answer?"
That's a teaching moment! AI makes mistakes. Part of the curriculum is learning to evaluate AI outputs critically. When it happens, lean in: "Why do you think it said that? Is that right?"
"How do I manage 30 students all using OpenClaw at once?"
Each student has their own session — no shared state to worry about. For lab days, consider pair work (one account per pair) to make discussion richer and reduce simultaneous sessions.
"What if a student asks OpenClaw something inappropriate?"
OpenClaw has built-in safety filters. If something slips through, use it as a teaching moment about responsible AI — that's literally what this curriculum is about.
"Can I skip a lesson if we're behind?"
Yes, with care. Lessons 1, 3, and 5 (the Lab) are the backbone — don't skip those. Lessons 2, 4, and 6 can be shortened or combined. The teacher guide flags essential vs. enrichment sections.
