# AI Explorers: School Adoption Brief
### *Build Your AI Assistant, Piece by Piece*

**Prepared for:** School Administrators and Board Members  
**Curriculum:** AI Explorers — Middle School AI Literacy Program  
**Grade Band:** 6–8  
**Duration:** One Semester (7 Modules)  
**Licensing Cost:** $0

---

## Executive Summary

AI Explorers is a free, open-source, project-based AI curriculum designed for middle school students in grades 6–8. Students don't just *learn about* artificial intelligence — they *build* one, module by module, culminating in a live AI Expo where they present a working AI assistant to a real audience.

**Why now?** Artificial intelligence is reshaping every profession students will enter. Districts that act in the next 2–3 years will graduate students with a meaningful head start — not just familiarity with AI tools, but the hands-on literacy to understand, evaluate, and responsibly deploy them. AI Explorers is the most cost-effective path to that outcome.

**Who it's for:** Any middle school with internet-connected devices (including Chromebooks). No prior coding experience is required for students or teachers. No budget required.

---

## Program Overview

### The Core Idea

AI Explorers follows a single through-line: students build a personal AI assistant across seven modules, adding one capability per module. By the end, they own a working agent they designed, trained, and governed themselves.

### Module Structure

| Module | Focus | Student Deliverable |
|--------|-------|---------------------|
| 1 | Identity & Deployment | Named, personalized AI agent |
| 2 | Perception (Vision & Audio) | Agent with image/audio capabilities |
| 3 | Knowledge & Reasoning | Custom domain-knowledge skill |
| 4 | Machine Learning | A trained ML model, integrated |
| 5 | Communication Channels | Agent connected to a live channel |
| 6 | Ethics & Guardrails | Written usage policy + bias audit |
| 7 | AI Expo (Synthesis) | Live public demonstration |

### Schedule

- **Duration:** One semester (~18 weeks)
- **Format:** Each module runs 2–4 weeks depending on scope
- **Sprint option:** Each module includes a 1-week condensed version
- **Standalone:** Modules 1–3 function as a self-contained 6-week unit

### Outcome

Every student leaves with: a working AI agent, demonstrated technical literacy, an ethics portfolio, and a public presentation under their belt.

| Requirement | Status |

## Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Pilot (Weeks 1–18, one class section)
- 1–2 teachers complete preparation (~8–12 hours)
- Run one class section through the full 7-module arc
- Collect student work samples and teacher feedback
- Host an AI Expo at semester end

### Phase 2 — Department Expansion (Semester 2)
- Expand to 2–4 teachers across Computer Science, STEM, or elective departments
- Peer co-teaching model: pilot teacher mentors new adopters
- Establish school-wide AI Expo tradition

### Phase 3 — School-Wide (Year 2)
- Integrate into standard elective rotation (grades 6–8)
- Optional: cross-curricular integration (ELA, Science, Social Studies)
- Optional: student AI Mentor program (8th graders support 6th grade cohort)

| Timeline | Milestone |
|----------|-----------|
| Month 1 | Teacher prep, device check, pilot launch |
| Month 4–5 | First AI Expo |
| Month 6 | Department review, expansion plan |
| Month 9 | Full department adoption |
| Year 2 | School-wide integration |

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| **Vendor lock-in** | Open source — no vendor exists to lock you in |
| **Curriculum discontinuation** | MIT-licensed; district can fork and maintain independently |
| **Budget cuts** | $0 cost; immune to budget pressure |
| **Connectivity issues** | Offline fallbacks for 3 of 7 modules |
| **Teacher turnover** | All materials self-contained; new teachers can onboard from curriculum alone |
| **AI content concerns** | Module 6 (Ethics) includes guardrails by design; usage policies are a student deliverable |
| **Technology obsolescence** | Browser-based design tracks with device refresh cycles naturally |

**Open source means free forever.** The curriculum cannot be discontinued, paywalled, or changed without your control.
