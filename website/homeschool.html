The 7-Module Arc

One agent. Seven capability unlocks.
A semester-long story.

Your kid follows a single through-line: they are building their own personal AI assistant. Each module adds one new power. By Module 7, it's real — and they built it.

🐣
Module 1 · 1–2 weeks

What Is AI? — Deploy Your Agent

Your kid gets their first look at AI, explores its history and vocabulary, then deploys their own AI instance. They name their agent, write its personality, and take their first steps as an AI creator.

Capability Unlock: Identity No prior experience needed
👁️
Module 2 · 1–2 weeks

Give Your Agent Vision

How do computers see? Students explore image recognition, train a Teachable Machine classifier (no code!), connect Hugging Face models, and give their agent the ability to perceive the world.

Capability Unlock: Vision & Hearing Runs in any browser
🧠
Module 3 · 2 weeks

Give Your Agent a Knowledge Base

Decision trees, pattern recognition, reasoning. Students build a custom skill for their agent — domain knowledge and logic they write themselves. First real code (guided).

Capability Unlock: Reasoning & Knowledge Guided coding intro
📚
Module 4 · 2 weeks

Give Your Agent the Ability to Learn

What is machine learning, really? Students explore training data, supervised learning, and overfitting — then train a real sentiment classifier and integrate it directly into their agent.

Capability Unlock: Machine Learning Python helpful, not required
🎙️
Module 5 · 2 weeks

Give Your Agent a Voice

LLMs, prompt engineering, and how language models actually work. Students connect their agent to a live channel — Discord, iMessage, or a webhook — so it can speak to the real world.

Capability Unlock: Communication Goes live this week!
⚖️
Module 6 · 1–2 weeks

Make Your Agent Ethical

Students audit the agent they built. What biases crept in? What should it refuse? They write guardrails, conduct a bias testing lab, and create a real usage policy — governing the thing they made.

Capability Unlock: Responsibility Great family discussion starter
🎓
Module 7 · 1–2 weeks

Capstone: The AI Expo

Your kid presents their fully built, named, governed AI agent to a real audience — family, homeschool co-op, community members. Not a test. A demonstration of something real they created.

Capability Unlock: Synthesis & Presentation Portfolio-ready artifact

Flexible pacing: A motivated learner can finish in 8 weeks. A more exploratory pace takes 14–18 weeks. Start anywhere. Stop after any module and your kid still has a working agent they built themselves. No prerequisites. No placement tests. No right speed.

Common Homeschool Concerns

We built answers in, not afterthoughts

These are the questions every homeschool and unschool family asks. We designed for all of them.

📋

Assessment Flexibility

No mandatory grades or standardized tests. Every module produces a tangible artifact — the agent itself, a lab writeup, a design journal entry. You assess what matters for your family, with the rubrics we provide as optional starting points.

✓ Portfolio-based. Use our rubrics or design your own.
🎓

Transcript Credit

Completing all 7 modules represents approximately one high school semester of Computer Science or STEM elective credit. The curriculum maps to AI4K12 and CSTA standards, and the Parent Guide includes sample transcript language for high school documentation.

✓ ~0.5 HS credit equivalent. Sample language included.
🧩

Different Learning Styles

Visual learners: every concept has diagrams and illustrated explainers. Hands-on learners: every module is built around labs. Verbal learners: discussion prompts and reflection questions throughout. Readers: companion text chapters included.

✓ Multiple modalities built into every module.
🌱

Self-Directed Learning

The curriculum is structured enough to give direction without requiring a parent facilitator for every session. Each module has a clear goal, a set of activities, and a deliverable. Your kid can work independently and bring questions to you.

✓ Independent-learner ready. Parent guide for support.
👪

Multiple Kids, Different Ages

The core project scales: a 10-year-old and a 16-year-old can work through the same curriculum at different depths. Younger kids focus on the concepts and build something simpler. Older kids go deeper in the code and design. Same modules, different depth.

✓ Works across ages 10–18 simultaneously.
🔌

Tech Requirements

Any modern browser on any device — laptop, tablet, Chromebook. No installs for Modules 1–3. A free Hugging Face account for Modules 4–5. OpenClaw self-hosted or shared class instance. Student data never leaves your infrastructure.

✓ Browser-only for most modules. Free accounts only.
