Common Teacher Questions

Things teachers always ask
before they say yes.

Do I need a computer science background to teach this?

No. The teacher guide is designed specifically for teachers with no AI background. It explains every concept at the level you need to facilitate discussion — not at a depth that requires expertise. You don't need to be an expert; you need to be a good facilitator. The guide helps you do that.

Math and science teachers will find the structure familiar. Humanities teachers — English, history, library science — have successfully run every unit in this curriculum. We track this because it matters to us. The curriculum is designed to work regardless of your subject background.

How long does each unit take to complete?

Each unit is structured for approximately 3 sessions at 50 minutes each — about 1–1.5 weeks depending on your schedule. The full 7-unit curriculum is designed for one semester (14–16 weeks), with built-in flexibility for discussion extensions and project time.

You don't need to teach the full curriculum. Individual units are designed to stand alone. If you only have 3 weeks, run Units 1 and 5. If you want the full arc with a capstone project, all 7 units build toward that.

What technology do students need? Does it work on Chromebooks?

Yes — Chromebooks are the primary target device. All lab activities run in a browser. There are no downloads, no installs, no student accounts, and no third-party platforms requiring login. Students need a browser and an internet connection. That's it.

Offline fallback materials are included for the discussion and reflection components of each unit, so even connectivity issues don't derail a lesson.

How do I handle students who use AI to do the work for them?

This is addressed directly in the teacher guide, and the curriculum is structured to make it less of a problem than it sounds. The primary deliverables are builds and reflections — training a model, explaining how it works, defending decisions made during the project. These aren't things you can meaningfully outsource to AI without understanding the underlying concepts.

Unit 5 (AI Ethics) also gives you a natural framework to have this conversation directly with students. Many teachers use that unit as the moment to surface the "when is it appropriate to use AI assistance" question explicitly.

What if my administration hasn't approved AI tools yet?

The curriculum doesn't require any AI tools that your district needs to approve. The labs use simple, browser-based machine learning demos — not commercial AI platforms like ChatGPT or Gemini. There's no student data collection, no accounts, and no third-party data processing.

If your district does require approval documentation, the standards mapping and privacy overview (available in the free pack) are designed to support that process. Many teachers have used the standards alignment doc to move through curriculum review without a lengthy approval cycle.

Is there a way to differentiate for advanced or struggling students?

Yes — each unit includes a differentiation guide with concrete suggestions for both ends. Extension activities challenge students who move quickly through the core content. Scaffolded versions of lab activities and simplified discussion prompts support students who need more structure.

The curriculum is also designed so that the core project — what students actually build — can be completed at different depths. A 6th grader and an advanced 8th grader can work through the same unit and produce very different artifacts, both legitimately.

Is there a district or site license available?

There's no license required at all. This curriculum is released under an open Creative Commons license (CC BY-SA 4.0). You can download it, share it with every teacher in your building, adapt it for your district's context, and redistribute it — all for free, no permission needed.

If your district is interested in a more formal adoption — pilot program documentation, PD support, or a briefing for administrators — see the district information page or contact us directly.

Can I adapt or modify the materials for my classroom?

Yes, absolutely — that's the point. The open license (CC BY-SA 4.0) explicitly permits modification and redistribution. Teachers regularly adapt slide decks for their school's branding, modify discussion prompts for their specific student population, and shorten or expand units based on available time.

If you make significant improvements and want to share them back with the community, we'd love to see what you built. The GitHub repository is open for contributions.
