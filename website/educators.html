The Semester Timeline

3 weeks per module. One semester. One complete arc.

Each module runs approximately 3 weeks at 3–4 class periods per week. The full 7-module arc fits a standard semester. Flexible pacing options are included if you need to compress or extend.

M1
Weeks 1–3 · Identity

What Is AI? — Deploy Your Agent

Students explore AI history, vocabulary, and concepts — then deploy their own OpenClaw instance, name their agent, and write its personality. The hook that makes the rest of the arc land.

Lab: First Deploy — students launch a live AI agent
M2
Weeks 4–6 · Perception

Give Your Agent Vision

How do computers see? Students explore image recognition, train a Teachable Machine classifier with their own photos, connect Hugging Face vision models to OpenClaw, and give their agent the ability to perceive the world.

Lab: Teachable Machine — train a custom classifier
M3
Weeks 7–9 · Knowledge

Give Your Agent a Knowledge Base

Students dive into decision trees, pattern recognition, and reasoning. They build their first custom OpenClaw skill — giving their agent domain knowledge and logic they designed themselves.

Lab: Skill Builder — write a real agent skill
M4
Weeks 10–12 · Learning

Give Your Agent the Ability to Learn

What is machine learning, really? Students explore training data, supervised learning, and overfitting — then train a real sentiment classifier and integrate their trained model directly into their agent.

Lab: Train Your Model — real ML on real data
M5
Weeks 13–15 · Voice

Give Your Agent a Voice

LLMs, prompt engineering, and how language models actually work. Students connect their agent to a real communication channel — so it can receive and respond to messages in the real world.

Lab: Channel Connect — agent goes live
M6
Weeks 16–18 · Ethics

Make Your Agent Ethical

Students audit the agent they built: What biases crept in? What should it refuse? They write guardrails, conduct a bias testing lab, and create a real usage policy governing the thing they made. The classroom discussions are unlike any ethics lecture.

Lab: Bias Audit — examine your own creation
M7
Weeks 19–21 · Synthesis

Capstone: The AI Expo

Students present their fully-built, named, governed AI agent to a real audience — peers, teachers, parents, community members. Not a test. A demonstration of something real they built. Expo planning guide and rubrics included.

Culminating Event: AI Expo — real audience, real agents

The moments students don't forget.

These labs are the moments students remember. They're hands-on, browser-based, and designed so that every student in the class succeeds — regardless of technical background. No installs. No accounts. Just something real that they built.

What Happens If...

The "what if OpenClaw goes down?" question.

Every teacher running a live platform worries about this. Here's how AI Explorers is designed so a bad technology day doesn't wreck a unit.

Built for real classroom reality

The curriculum is structured so that deep conceptual learning — the part that matters — happens before and around the live platform moments. Labs have defined offline fallback activities that cover the same learning objectives without the platform.

If OpenClaw is unavailable on lab day, the lesson still runs. Students engage with the concepts, complete the analysis, and do the platform portion when connectivity is restored — typically within one class period.

No unit requires uninterrupted multi-day platform access. Every lesson stands alone.

Offline fallback options, every module

  • Every lab includes a PDF "offline mode" version covering the same concepts
  • Conceptual activities, design challenges, and reflection exercises don't require connectivity
  • Teachable Machine (M2) runs fully in-browser — no backend, no account required
  • All slide decks and lesson plans are downloaded locally — no cloud dependency for instruction
  • Student portfolio work (Agent Design Journal) is paper-friendly and works without technology
  • Teacher guides include explicit "if technology fails" contingency notes for every lesson
  • The AI Expo (M7) is a presentation event — students, not live agents, take center stage
