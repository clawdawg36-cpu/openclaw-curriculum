Built Online-First

Not adapted for online learning.
Designed for it.

Most curricula are written for classrooms and then retrofitted for online use. AI Explorers was built from the ground up for asynchronous, self-directed learners — because that's how real distance education works.

🌐

Everything lives in your browser

Every lesson, every lab, every tool — Teachable Machine, Hugging Face, OpenClaw — runs in a modern web browser. No software to install, no OS requirements, no apps to download. Works on Chromebooks, Windows laptops, Macs, and tablets.

📦

Zero physical materials

There are no textbooks to order, no lab kits to ship, no printed workbooks. All lesson materials, guides, slide decks, and activity sheets are digital — download, open, done. Nothing arrives by mail and nothing runs out.

⏸️

Truly asynchronous

No live sessions required. No scheduled class times. Students work through each module on their own schedule — pause a lab mid-way, come back tomorrow, pick up exactly where they left off. Built for real life, not the bell curve.

🔁

Go at any pace

Each module comes in a one-week sprint version and an extended four-week version. Students moving fast can cover the full curriculum in seven weeks. Students going deep can take a semester. Both paths end with a real AI agent they built themselves.

🤝

Works alongside your online school

AI Explorers is designed to complement, not conflict with, your existing virtual school. Use it as a standalone elective, integrate modules into existing STEM coursework, or run it as an enrichment track — it's flexible enough to fit your program's structure.

📱

No fixed technology requirements

Any browser-capable device works for Modules 1–3. Module 4 recommends a browser with internet access for Google Colab — but it's supported on a basic Chromebook. No specialized hardware, no subscription software, no hidden requirements.

How It Works

Seven modules. One arc.
A student who built something real.

Each module unlocks one new capability for the student's AI agent. By Module 7, they've built a working, named, personalized AI assistant — all from home, all on their own schedule.

1
Module 1 — Identity

Deploy Your Agent

Students explore what AI is, set up their own OpenClaw instance in a browser, name their agent, and write its personality. Zero coding experience required. Complete in a single session or spread over a week — entirely up to you.

Fully async ✓
2
Module 2 — Perception

Give Your Agent Vision

Students train a Teachable Machine image classifier in their browser and connect it to their agent. The training happens locally — no special hardware, no data uploaded anywhere. Works offline once the model is trained.

Browser-only ✓
3
Module 3 — Knowledge

Give Your Agent a Knowledge Base

Students build a custom OpenClaw skill — adding domain knowledge and decision logic to their agent. Guided step-by-step with a downloadable template. Works without any prior programming experience for the core activity.

Self-paced ✓
4
Module 4 — Learning

Train Your Agent to Learn

Students train a real sentiment classifier using Google Colab (free, browser-based). The module includes a browser fallback for every lab — no Python installation needed. Students integrate their trained model directly into their agent.

Colab-based ✓
5
Module 5 — Voice

Give Your Agent a Voice

Students connect their agent to a real communication channel — Discord, a webhook, or a messaging platform — so it can interact with the world. LLMs, prompt engineering, and how language models work, explained without the jargon.

No account required for core ✓
6
Module 6 — Ethics

Audit Your Agent

Students audit the agent they built: What biases crept in? What should it refuse? They write guardrails and a real usage policy — governing the thing they made. One of the most meaningful modules because students confront choices they made themselves.

Reflective + hands-on ✓
7
Module 7 — Synthesis

Present at the AI Expo

Students present their fully built AI agent to an audience — family, co-op group, online school cohort, or recorded video. The module includes a remote/virtual presentation format for distance learners. A real demonstration of something real they built.

Virtual format included ✓

Your Pace

Seven weeks, one semester,
or somewhere in between.

Distance learners don't all move at the same pace — and they shouldn't have to. AI Explorers supports every tempo, from an intensive summer push to a slow-lane deep dive across a full year.

⚡
Sprint

Summer Intensive

7 weeks · 1 module/week

One module per week, 3–4 sessions per week at roughly 45–60 minutes each. Ideal for summer programs, school breaks, or students who want to move fast. All seven modules, full agent, in under two months.

📅
Most Popular

Standard Semester

18–20 weeks · 1 module/2–3 weeks

The natural pace for a school semester. Each module gets room to breathe — time for discussion, extension projects, and revisiting concepts. Works well as a standalone elective in a virtual academy or homeschool program.

🌊
Deep Dive

Full-Year Exploration

Full year · 1 module/month

One module per month, with lots of space for project extension, rabbit holes, and going deep. Perfect for unschoolers and self-directed learners who want to truly master each concept before moving on. No rush. All depth.

Common Questions

Questions from distance learners
and their families.

The most common questions from online school students, virtual academy families, and self-directed learners — answered honestly.

Can my child really do this completely independently, without a teacher?

Yes — that's exactly what it's designed for. Every module includes detailed student-facing instructions that assume no teacher is present. Labs are written step-by-step. Concepts are explained in plain language before students apply them. Anticipated stumbling points are called out in advance with suggestions for getting unstuck.

That said, the parent/facilitator guide is there for when you want to check in or co-explore. Many families find the curriculum doubles as a learning experience for the parent — you'll both come away with a better understanding of AI.

How does it work with our virtual school's existing curriculum and credit requirements?

AI Explorers is flexible by design. It can be used as:

• A standalone elective (technology, computer science, STEM, digital literacy)

• An enrichment track running alongside core coursework

• An independent study unit documented via the Agent Design Journal portfolio

The curriculum includes a standards alignment document mapping each module to CSTA K–12 CS Standards, ISTE Student Standards, and AI4K12 frameworks — which are commonly referenced in virtual school approvals. If your school needs a specific mapping, the GitHub repo includes a blank template.

What happens if my student gets stuck mid-module? There's no teacher to ask.

Every module anticipates this. Each lab includes a "Help! I'm stuck" sidebar that covers the most common failure modes and how to resolve them. The facilitator guide has additional troubleshooting sections for parents who want to dig in together.

Beyond the built-in support, the GitHub Discussions board is active — other families and educators using the curriculum often respond within a day. It's a genuine community, not a support ticket queue.

And if all else fails: it's completely normal to pause a module, sleep on it, and return fresh the next day. The asynchronous nature of the curriculum means there's never a penalty for taking a break.

Does my student need to know how to code to start?

Not at all — Modules 1 through 3 require zero coding. Students deploy their agent, give it vision, and build custom knowledge entirely through guided tools and interfaces. No code is written until the student is ready for it.

Modules 4 and 5 introduce some programming concepts using Python in Google Colab (browser-based, no installation). The labs are fully guided with copy-paste code blocks and step-by-step explanations of what each line does. A motivated student with no coding background can complete these modules successfully.

Module 3 (building a custom skill) is the first place students write any configuration logic — and it uses a simple, readable format with a fully worked example as a template. Most students complete it in one session.

What if our internet connection is slow or unreliable?

We've designed around this. OpenClaw can be configured for local-only operation once it's set up — subsequent sessions don't require a live connection to run the agent. All curriculum PDFs and guides can be downloaded for offline use.

Teachable Machine (Module 2) runs entirely in the browser and processes images locally — no data leaves the device and it functions on slow connections after the initial page loads.

Google Colab (Module 4) does require a stable internet connection for the machine learning lab. If connectivity is a consistent challenge, the module includes an alternative lab using a pre-trained model download, which requires only a brief connection to fetch the initial file.

How do we handle the AI Expo in Module 7 if we're remote?

Module 7 includes a complete virtual presentation format for distance learners. Options include:

• Video presentation: The student records a walkthrough of their agent — the included rubric covers video format and evaluation criteria. Many families share with extended family, grandparents, or their online school cohort.

• Live video call: Present to family, a co-op group, or even a few adults the student respects. The module provides facilitation notes for virtual audiences.

• Written showcase: For students who prefer writing, an alternative showcase format uses the Agent Design Journal as the primary artifact, with a structured presentation document.

The point of the AI Expo is a real audience — family counts entirely. Students rise to whatever audience is present.

Is there any cost? Are there hidden fees or "Pro" tiers?

No — and no. AI Explorers is published under Creative Commons (CC BY-SA 4.0) and is free forever. OpenClaw is MIT-licensed open source software. There is no paid tier, no premium content, no subscription, no upsell.

The only accounts you'll need:

• A free Hugging Face account (used for model access in Modules 2 and 4)

• A free Google account for Google Colab in Module 4 (or skip using the offline alternative)

That's it. No credit card ever required.
