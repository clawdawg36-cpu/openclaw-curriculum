Your Questions, Answered

Every parent asks these.
Here are the honest answers.

We know the questions on your mind. Is this safe? Is it too hard? Will it actually help? We'll give you straight answers — no marketing spin.

🛡️
Is it safe for my child?
Yes — and the setup is specifically designed for school environments. AI Explorers runs on OpenClaw, which is self-hosted by your school. That means your child's work, projects, and data stay entirely on your school's own server — never uploaded to any external cloud, never shared with any third party, never used for ads or training AI models.

There's no open internet chat, no social network component, no exposure to external users. The platform is closer to a school-managed word processor than to TikTok. All curriculum materials are written for ages 11–14 and reviewed for age-appropriateness before publication.
🎒
Is it age-appropriate? Is my kid ready?
It's built specifically for middle schoolers — and no prior coding experience is needed to start. Module 1 assumes nothing. If your child can read a paragraph, follow multi-step directions, and use a web browser, they're ready. Every module includes a pacing option for students who want to go deeper and one for students who need more time.

Many students who thought they "weren't tech people" discover they love building AI projects. The curriculum is designed so that the interesting part isn't typing code — it's making decisions about what your agent does, who it helps, and how it should behave.
🚀
Will this actually help my child's future?
Yes — more than almost anything else they could learn right now. AI will touch every career your child might pursue, from medicine to music to law to logistics. The students entering the workforce in the 2030s and 2040s will be operating in an AI-transformed economy. The ones with a head start aren't the ones who used AI tools — they're the ones who understand how those tools work.

AI Explorers teaches your child to build AI systems, understand their limitations, identify bias, and govern them responsibly. That's not just a tech skill — it's a citizen skill.
💰
What does it cost us?
Nothing. And that's a permanent commitment, not a promotional period. The entire curriculum — all 7 modules, all lesson plans, all teacher guides, all activities, all assessment tools — is published under a Creative Commons open license. It is free for every school, forever. There is no paid tier, no "Pro" version, no subscription, no upsell.

Your school needs a device with a modern web browser and a modest server to run OpenClaw. Many schools run it on a single recycled computer. No student needs a personal account with any paid service.

What Your Child Will Build

One agent. Seven capability unlocks.
A semester to remember.

🤖 They build an AI assistant. From scratch.

Not a pre-made chatbot. Not a simulation. A real, working AI agent that your child names, designs, and trains — one capability at a time over seven modules.

The semester ends with an AI Expo — a public presentation where students demonstrate their agent to parents, teachers, and community members. Not a test. A real audience, and something real to show them.

Parent FAQ

More questions parents ask

The concerns we hear most often — answered as clearly as we can.

My child says they're "not a coding person." Can they still do this?

Yes — this is one of the things we're most deliberate about. Modules 1–3 require zero coding. Students work through structured activities in a web browser, write instructions in plain language, and make decisions about their agent's behavior without writing a single line of code.

Python appears in Module 4 as an optional extension — and even then, there's a browser-based path using Google Colab that requires no setup and very little syntax knowledge. The interesting parts of this curriculum are about decisions, not debugging. Many students who thought they weren't "tech people" discover that building AI is more about judgment than syntax — and they're often the most creative ones in the room.

What kind of AI will my child be interacting with? Is it like ChatGPT?

The AI your child builds and uses is different from consumer chatbots like ChatGPT. OpenClaw agents are structured, school-controlled AI systems — not open-ended chat interfaces connected to the internet.

Your child designs the agent's capabilities and constraints. The agent can only do what your child has explicitly built and enabled. There's no free-form web search, no access to inappropriate content, and no connection to public AI services unless your school specifically configures one (which the curriculum doesn't require).

In Module 5, students do connect their agent to a language model for natural language understanding — but this is done through your school's controlled infrastructure, not through a public API. Your school controls the configuration.

What happens to my child's project after the semester ends?

That depends on your school's policy — but the project belongs to your child. Because OpenClaw is self-hosted by the school, the agent configuration, design journal, and all project files are stored on the school's server. Your school can export and return those files to students at the end of the semester.

We encourage schools to give students a copy of their Agent Design Journal as a portfolio artifact — it's a documented record of every decision and capability unlock across all 7 modules. It's excellent material for high school applications, portfolios, or just a record of something remarkable they accomplished.

I'm worried about AI replacing teachers. Does this curriculum contribute to that?

No — and in fact the curriculum explicitly teaches students why AI can't replace teachers (or many other human roles).

Module 6 (Ethics) has students audit their own AI agent for limitations, failures, and gaps. A central theme of the curriculum is that understanding what AI can't do is just as important as understanding what it can. Students leave with a much more nuanced, realistic view of AI — including a clear-eyed understanding of what requires human judgment, creativity, and care.

The curriculum is also designed to make your child's teacher more effective, not less necessary. AI Explorers gives teachers structured materials and support — it doesn't replace the human relationship, facilitation, and mentorship that make learning stick.

My child's school doesn't offer this yet. What can I do?

The best thing you can do is forward this page to your child's teacher. Teachers — not administrators — are usually the ones who bring new curricula into schools. A single interested teacher can start with one class, one semester, and build from there.

The curriculum includes everything a teacher needs to get started: lesson plans, slide decks, setup guides, teacher PD materials, and technical documentation for IT. A non-CS teacher with no prior coding background has successfully taught this curriculum — you don't need to find a specialist.

If your child's teacher is interested, the adoption brief for administrators is also available — it's a two-page summary of everything a school board needs to know, including cost ($0), privacy posture, and standards alignment.

Is this appropriate for kids who are already into coding? Will they be bored?

Kids with coding experience often go deepest. Every module includes extension challenges designed for students who want to push further. Module 3 lets students write real JavaScript/Python skills. Module 4 involves training a classifier using a real machine learning library. Module 5 allows students to build a custom communication channel.

Many technically advanced students say the most challenging part wasn't the code — it was Module 6 (Ethics), where they had to confront the biases and limitations in the agent they built and write principled guardrails for it. That challenge doesn't have a syntax. It requires judgment, and it's hard for everyone.
