## State Education Departments with AI Curriculum Interest

These states have either passed AI education legislation, issued guidance, or have active state-level AI in education initiatives. Prioritize outreach here.

| State | Contact Point | AI Education Activity | Approach |
|---|---|---|---|

Contact CDE's Computer Science team: csforall@cde.ca.gov. Include CSTA and AI4K12 alignment documentation. |
| **Texas** | TEA — Technology Applications TEKS team (tea.texas.gov) | Updated Technology Applications TEKS include CS and AI components | Contact TEA curriculum team. Texas has a formal instructional materials adoption process — position as a supplementary open resource pending formal review. tea.publiccomments@tea.texas.gov |
| **Virginia** | VDOE — CS & Technology team (doe.virginia.gov) | VA is a national leader in CS ed policy; CS for VA initiative | Virginia has CS education liaisons in every district. Email the state CS coordinator and offer to demo. Contact: csforva@doe.virginia.gov |
| **Illinois** | ISBE — Learning and Teaching division (isbe.net) | IL Digital Equity and AI Literacy legislation pending | ISBE has been active in digital literacy standards. Send policy brief + adoption brief. isbe.assistance@illinois.gov |
| **Washington** | OSPI — CS program (k12.wa.us) | WA state CS graduation requirement; tech-forward policy environment | Contact OSPI's CS education team. WA districts are actively seeking AI-specific resources. cse@k12.wa.us |
| **Massachusetts** | DESE — Curriculum & Instruction (doe.mass.edu) | Boston-area districts piloting AI literacy; DESE updating digital literacy standards | Submit to DESE's curriculum resource hub. Contact: curriculum@doe.mass.edu. Include research citations and AI4K12 alignment. |
| **Colorado** | CDE — Teaching & Learning (cde.state.co.us) | CO has equity-focused digital literacy framework; active AI policy conversations | Colorado values equity framing. Lead with the "free, no per-seat cost, open source" angle in all outreach. cde_information@cde.state.co.us |
| **North Carolina** | NCDPI — Digital Learning team (dpi.nc.gov) | NC has a Digital Learning Plan; active district-level AI pilots | NCDPI has curriculum review processes for supplementary materials. Submit to their resource library. Contact: digital.learning@dpi.nc.gov |
| **Georgia** | GaDOE — STEM program (gadoe.org) | Georgia has a statewide STEM certification program; AI is a growing focus | Contact GaDOE STEM coordinator. Position as a STEM elective or enrichment resource. stem@gadoe.org |
| **New York** | NYSED — Office of Curriculum & Instruction (nysed.gov) | NYC DOE has AI literacy pilots; NYS updating CS/digital literacy standards | Two-track approach: (1) NYSED statewide standards submission, (2) NYC DOE direct outreach. NYC: schooldevelopment@schools.nyc.gov |

**General approach for state departments:**
1. Send the policy brief first — 2 pages, research-backed, equity-forward.
2. Follow up with the adoption brief if they respond.
3. Offer a 30-minute virtual briefing with the curriculum team.
4. Reference ESSA Title IV funding as a mechanism for adoption.
5. Provide alignment documentation to state standards (not just AI4K12 and CSTA — map to their specific state standards if possible).
