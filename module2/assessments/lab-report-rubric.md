# Image Classifier Lab Report Rubric

**Module 2 — Lab Report Assessment**
**Project:** Train an image classifier using Teachable Machine and document your process.

**Total Points:** 16 (4 categories × 4 points each)

---

## Scoring Guide

### 4 — Exceeds Expectations
Work goes beyond the requirements. Shows deep understanding, strong connections, and independent thinking.

### 3 — Meets Expectations
Work fully addresses the requirements with clear understanding. Minor gaps don't affect the overall quality.

### 2 — Approaching Expectations
Work partially addresses the requirements. Some understanding present but key gaps or errors remain.

### 1 — Beginning
Work is incomplete or shows significant misunderstanding. Major gaps in content or execution.

---

## Category 1: Data Collection & Training Process

| Score | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **4** | Describes all steps of the training process with specific detail: how many images were collected per class, what variety was included (angles, lighting, backgrounds), and why those choices matter. Explains the connection between data quality and model performance. |
| **3** | Describes the training process clearly with adequate detail. Notes the number of classes and approximate image counts. Shows understanding that more/better data generally helps. |
| **2** | Describes basic steps (took photos, trained model) but lacks specific detail. Vague about image counts or variety. Limited connection between data choices and outcomes. |
| **1** | Training process description is incomplete, missing, or inaccurate. Does not explain how images were collected or why. |

---

## Category 2: Results & Accuracy Analysis

| Score | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **4** | Reports accuracy for each class with specific numbers. Analyzes *why* some classes performed better than others (e.g., more images, better contrast, consistent background). Identifies at least one surprising result and offers a hypothesis. |
| **3** | Reports overall accuracy and notes which classes worked better or worse. Provides a reasonable explanation for differences in performance. Results are presented clearly. |
| **2** | Reports some accuracy data but incompletely. Analysis is surface-level ("it worked pretty well" or "it was wrong sometimes") without connecting to specific causes. |
| **1** | Accuracy data is missing or results are not analyzed. No comparison across classes. |

---

## Category 3: Reflection on Errors & Improvements

| Score | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **4** | Identifies specific failure cases (e.g., "it confused wave and thumbs up when my hand was sideways"). Proposes concrete, testable improvements (e.g., "I would add 20 more photos of edge cases"). Connects errors back to the concept of overfitting or data gaps. |
| **3** | Identifies at least one type of error the model made and suggests a reasonable improvement. Shows understanding that errors are informative, not just failures. |
| **2** | Acknowledges the model made mistakes but is vague about what went wrong or how to fix it. Improvement suggestions are generic ("train more"). |
| **1** | Does not reflect on errors or improvement. No attempt to explain why the model failed in certain cases. |

## Lab Report Prompt (for students)

You trained an image classifier using Teachable Machine. Now write a lab report (1–2 pages) that answers:

1. **Setup:** What classes did you create? How many images per class? What did you do to vary your training images?
2. **Results:** How accurate was your model? Which class worked best? Which worked worst?
3. **Analysis:** Why do you think some classes worked better than others?
4. **Errors:** Describe a specific mistake your model made. What caused it?
5. **Improvements:** What would you change if you trained it again?
6. **Vocabulary:** Use at least 4 terms from the Module 2 vocabulary list in your report.

*Include at least one screenshot of your model in action (correct prediction AND incorrect prediction if possible).*
