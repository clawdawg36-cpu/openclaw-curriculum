# Module 2 Exit Tickets

Five quick formative checks — one per lesson. Each ticket takes about 3–5 minutes to complete. Students write, sketch, or respond on paper or a shared doc.

---

## Lesson 2.1 Exit Ticket — Pixels & RGB

**Big Idea:** Images are made of tiny colored squares. Computers see numbers, not pictures.

**Question 1 (Circle one):**
A pixel is…
- A) A type of paintbrush
- B) The smallest unit of a digital image
- C) A computer program
- D) A sound file

**Question 2 (Fill in the blank):**
RGB stands for __________, __________, and __________.

**Question 3 (Short answer):**
Why does a computer represent a red pixel as `(255, 0, 0)` instead of just the word "red"? Write 1–2 sentences.

**Bonus (Draw it):**
Sketch a 3×3 pixel grid. Color one square red, one green, one blue. Then write the RGB value for each colored square next to it.

## Lesson 2.3 Exit Ticket — Teachable Machine vs. Pre-Trained Models

**Big Idea:** Sometimes you train your own model. Sometimes you use one that's already trained. Both have tradeoffs.

**Question 1 (Pick the best tool):**
For each scenario, circle whether you'd use **Teachable Machine** (custom training) or a **Pre-Trained Model**:

a) You want your agent to tell the difference between YOUR thumbs-up and YOUR wave gesture.
→ Teachable Machine / Pre-Trained Model

b) You want your agent to recognize cats vs. dogs from the internet without any training.
→ Teachable Machine / Pre-Trained Model

c) You want your agent to recognize your pet rock vs. your water bottle.
→ Teachable Machine / Pre-Trained Model

**Question 2 (Short answer):**
Name one advantage of using a pre-trained model and one advantage of training your own with Teachable Machine.

- Pre-trained advantage: ___________________________________________________
- Teachable Machine advantage: ______________________________________________

**Question 3 (Reflect):**
What's one risk of training your own model with only a small number of photos? (Hint: think about what could go wrong.)
