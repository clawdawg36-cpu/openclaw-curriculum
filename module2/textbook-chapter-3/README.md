# Companion Textbook — Chapter 3: How Computers See — Pixels, Patterns, Perception

**Module:** 2 — How AI Perceives the World
**Reading Level:** Grades 6–8 (Flesch-Kincaid 6th–7th grade)
**Length:** ~600 lines (~33KB) — full first draft

## Overview

Chapter 3 of the *AI Explorers* companion textbook. Covers how AI processes visual information — from raw pixel data through feature detection, classification, object detection, and image captioning. Includes a major section on training data and Hugging Face, and closes with a focused section on bias in computer vision (Gender Shades study, wrongful arrests).

**Note:** This chapter was previously the first half of `module2/textbook-chapter-2/chapter-2.md` (the combined "How AI Sees and Hears" chapter). It was split into Chapter 3 (vision) and Chapter 4 (audio/speech) per the canonical chapter numbering plan.

## Connection to Chapter 4

Chapter 3 ends with a preview of Chapter 4 (hearing). The vision pipeline established here mirrors the audio pipeline in Chapter 4. Students who read both will see the same underlying pattern — perception as numbers, models as pattern-finders — applied to two different senses.
