# 🏆 Teachable Machine Challenge
## *Build the Most Creative AND Accurate 3-Class Classifier*

**Module 2 | Competition Activity | ~60 minutes**

---

## Overview

You've learned how machine learning works under the hood. Now it's time to compete.

Your mission: build a **3-class image classifier** using [Google Teachable Machine](https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/) that is *both* creative **and** accurate. The winning team doesn't just score the highest — they impress the judges with something unexpected.

---

## The Rules

### 1. Tool
Use **Google Teachable Machine** (Image Project).  
👉 [teachablemachine.withgoogle.com](https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/)

### 2. Classes
Your classifier must have **exactly 3 classes** — no more, no less.

### 3. Data Collection
- You may use your **webcam**, upload images, or both.
- Each class needs **at least 30 training samples**.
- You may not download pre-labeled datasets from the internet.
- All samples must be collected **during this session**.

### 4. Team Size
Teams of **2–3 students**.

### 5. Time Limits
| Phase | Time |
|-------|------|
| Ideation (pick your concept) | 5 min |
| Data collection + training | 25 min |
| Testing + refinement | 10 min |
| Presentation prep | 10 min |
| Presentations | 3 min per team |

## Presentation Format

Each team gets **3 minutes** to present. Hit these beats:

1. **What it does** (10 sec) — State your 3 classes clearly.
2. **Why you chose it** (30 sec) — What made this interesting or useful?
3. **Live demo** (90 sec) — Show it working in real time. Judges will test it too.
4. **What surprised you** (20 sec) — One thing ML did that you didn't expect.
5. **Agent usefulness reflection** (30 sec) — Would you add this to an AI agent? Why or why not?

> ⏱️ Strict 3-minute limit. Practice beforehand!

---

## Live Demo Protocol

During judging, the **head judge** will test your classifier with:
- 3 items/poses/gestures **you did NOT show them in advance**
- They'll run each sample 3 times

Your live accuracy score = correct predictions ÷ 9 attempts × 40 points.

## What Makes a Great Entry?

The best classifiers aren't the obvious ones ("cat vs. dog vs. bird"). They're the ones that make you think:

> *"I never would have thought to teach a machine that — but it totally works."*

Creative categories that are **still accurate** win. See `inspiration-ideas.md` for 30 ideas to get you started.

## Tips for Winning

- **Consistency beats quantity** — 40 good samples > 100 sloppy ones.
- **Control your background** — Use a consistent backdrop per class.
- **Think about edge cases** — If your 3 classes look similar, add variety to training data.
- **Weird categories can win** — A classifier that detects "focused vs. distracted vs. asleep" is both useful *and* impressive.
- **Name your classes clearly** — "Class 1" tells judges nothing. "Happy / Neutral / Annoyed" tells a story.

## Quick Start

1. Go to [teachablemachine.withgoogle.com](https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/)
2. Click **Get Started** → **Image Project** → **Standard image model**
3. Rename your 3 classes
4. Click **Webcam** under each class and hold the record button to capture samples
5. Click **Train Model**
6. Test in the **Preview** panel on the right
7. Iterate — collect more samples for any class that underperforms
