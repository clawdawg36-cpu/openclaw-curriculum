# Quiz Guide: Module 2 — Perception Concepts

**For:** Instructors and Curriculum Designers  
**Quiz file:** `quiz.md`

---

## Overview

This 5-question quiz assesses student understanding of the core concepts covered in Module 2: how AI systems perceive and interpret the world through pixels, audio signals, and learned pattern recognition.

### Topics Covered

| Q# | Topic | Format |
|----|-------|--------|
| 1 | Pixels and RGB — how images are numerically represented | Multiple choice |
| 2 | How classifiers learn (pattern-based, not rule-based) | True/False |
| 3 | Training data requirements and bias | Multiple choice |
| 4 | Speech recognition challenges vs. text AI | Multiple choice |
| 5 | Difference between text-based and vision AI (applied scenario) | Scenario / Multiple choice |

---

## Passing Recommendation

**Recommended passing score: 4 out of 5 (80%)**

- Students scoring **5/5** demonstrate strong conceptual grasp of perception AI — ready to advance to Module 3.
- Students scoring **4/5** have solid understanding with one minor gap — review the explanation for the missed question before proceeding.
- Students scoring **3/5** should revisit the full Module 2 lesson, particularly the sections on training data and the differences between AI modalities.
- Students scoring **2/5 or below** should retake the module before progressing.

---

## Formative Usage Notes

### ✅ Use this quiz as a formative check-in, not a high-stakes assessment

This quiz is designed to help students reflect on what they've learned, not to rank or grade them formally. Instructors are encouraged to:

- **Debrief as a group** — Go through each answer and explanation together after students submit. The explanations are written for learners, not just validators.
- **Use it mid-module** — The quiz can be placed mid-lesson (after the "pixels and classifiers" section) rather than only at the end. This helps students consolidate before tackling the more complex modality comparison content.
- **Allow retakes** — Since this is formative, students should be allowed to retry after reviewing the explanations. The goal is learning, not gatekeeping.
- **Discussion anchor** — Question 5 (scenario) is a good discussion prompt even for students who answered correctly. Ask: *What are the ethical implications of Tool B? Who should decide whether facial recognition is appropriate at school events?*

### 📊 Auto-Grading Setup

This quiz is designed for platforms that support multiple-choice auto-grading (e.g., Google Forms, Canvas, Schoology, Kahoot). When configuring:

- All questions have exactly one correct answer
- Distractors are plausible but clearly wrong upon reflection — avoid changing them without review
- Point value: 1 point per question (total: 5 points)
