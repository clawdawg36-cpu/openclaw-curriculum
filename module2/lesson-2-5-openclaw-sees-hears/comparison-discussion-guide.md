# Comparison Discussion Guide: Text AI vs. Vision AI
## Lesson 2.5 | Teacher Reference — Socratic Prompts

---

## 📌 Purpose

This guide supports the 8-minute structured discussion in Part 3 of Lesson 2.5. Use Socratic questioning to help students arrive at their own understanding of the difference between text AI and vision AI — rather than lecturing.

The goal isn't just technical knowledge. It's *intuition* about where AI modalities break down and why the gap matters for their personal agent.

---

## 🎭 Facilitation Approach

- **Ask, don't tell.** When students get close to a good answer, push further: "Why do you think that?"
- **Embrace wrong answers.** Use them: "Interesting — what would have to be true for that to work?"
- **Return to their agent.** Keep pulling examples back to their specific agent concepts.
- **Name the gap explicitly** at least once: "This is why Lab 2.4 exists."

---

## 🧩 Discussion Block 1: What Text AI Does Well (2 min)

**Opening prompt:**
> "We've been using OpenClaw all module. What kinds of things does it do really well?"

*Expected answers:* Answering questions, writing things, explaining concepts, summarizing, planning.

**Follow-up:**
> "What do all of those things have in common?"

*Guide toward:* They all live in the world of language. Words in, words out.

**Push deeper:**
> "If everything you want OpenClaw to help with can be *described in words* — do you even need vision AI?"

*This is a real question. Let them wrestle. Some valid answers: no, sometimes. That's fine.*

---

## 🧩 Discussion Block 2: Where Text AI Breaks Down (3 min)

**Prompt:**
> "Think about something you saw today — a person's face, a food you ate, the street outside. Could you describe it well enough for OpenClaw to fully understand it?"

*Let a few students try to describe something complex (a texture, a face, a cluttered room).*

**Follow-up:**
> "What got lost?"

*Expected: nuance, specificity, things that are obvious visually but hard to put into words.*

**Key question:**
> "If a friend sent you a photo and said 'what do you think?', could you relay that photo to OpenClaw right now without your friend losing anything important?"

*Most will say no. Ask why not.*

**Bridge:**
> "So what tasks are basically *impossible* for OpenClaw to do without vision?"

*Build a class list on the board. Examples: identifying a plant, checking if a door is locked from a photo, reading a handwritten note, spotting a rash in a medical image, navigating a physical space.*

---

## 🧩 Discussion Block 3: What Vision AI Adds (2 min)

**Prompt:**
> "Imagine OpenClaw could see everything you see right now. Not just read your description — actually *see* it. What changes?"

*Students may say: it doesn't need you to describe things. It can react to the physical world. It notices things you didn't mention.*

**Harder follow-up:**
> "Does that make text AI obsolete?"

*Expected no — vision AI still uses language to respond. They work together. Push:*

> "So what's the relationship between vision and language in an AI system?"

*Guide toward: vision is input; language is still the main way it communicates back. They're not competing — they're layered.*

---

## 🧩 Optional Extension: The Translation Tax (bonus if time allows)

**Prompt:**
> "Every time you described an image to OpenClaw today, something was lost in translation. What would you call that loss?"

*Listen for intuitions about information degradation, bottlenecks, subjectivity.*

**Follow-up:**
> "Who pays for that loss right now — you, or your agent?"

*Answer: you. You're doing cognitive work to translate vision into language before handing it to AI.*

**Landing the point:**
> "What would it mean to 'remove yourself from that loop'?"

*This is exactly what Lab 2.4 does. Let them arrive at it.*

---

## 🚫 Common Misconceptions to Watch For

| Misconception | What to Say |
|---------------|-------------|
| "Vision AI just looks at images" | "It's doing the same thing we did in 20 Questions — finding patterns, labels, relationships. It's turning visual data into structured information." |
| "Text AI can already understand images if you describe them" | "Partially — but who's doing the describing? That's the question." |
| "Vision AI is always better" | "Better at what? Text AI doesn't need a camera. If I ask you to solve a math problem, a camera doesn't help." |
| "AI can see like humans see" | "What's different about how you and OpenClaw experience an image? You experience it; OpenClaw processes it." |

---

## 🔗 Through-Line Moment (End of Discussion)

Close with this or a variation:

> "The frustration you felt today — having to describe images in words, losing detail, watching OpenClaw almost-get-it — that's not a bug in OpenClaw. That's the current state of your agent. It's text-first. After Lab 2.4, it becomes vision-capable. Same agent, new sense."

*Then pivot directly into the Agent Preview Activity.*

## 📝 Discussion Participation Rubric

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Insightful** | Contributes a novel idea, extends a peer's point, or makes a connection to their personal agent concept |
| **3 — On-Point** | Responds accurately to a prompt, engages with the question asked |
| **2 — Participating** | Makes an attempt, even if partially off-base — shows engagement |
| **1 — Present** | Listens but doesn't contribute verbally |
| **0** | Off-task |
