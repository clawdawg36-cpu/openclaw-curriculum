## Slide 3 — Setting the Stage
**Welcome to the city**

[Simple city map graphic: downtown grid, highlighted "flagged zones," camera icons]

- AI cameras: 847 installed city-wide
- Predictive system: reviews movement data, social graphs, purchase patterns
- Dashboard: analysts review 50–100 alerts per day
- Stakes: resource allocation, police dispatch, immigration reporting

*Not a dystopia. A real city, modeled on documented systems.*

---

## Slide 4 — Role A: The Surveilled Citizen
**Meet Alex.**

[Character card graphic]

- 17 years old
- Lives in a "flagged zone"
- Takes the same route to work every day
- No criminal record
- Risk score: 73/100

Alex doesn't know any of this.

*You are Alex. You don't know you're being watched. You just know your life.*

---

## Slide 5 — Role B: The Analyst
**Your dashboard**

[Simulated dashboard graphic: list of names, risk scores, frequency flags]

- 47 alerts today
- Alex appears 3 times this week
- Score: 73/100
- "Connections to high-risk individuals" flag

Decision: Escalate to police? Flag for review? Do nothing?

*You have 12 other alerts waiting.*

---

## Slide 6 — Role C: The City Administrator
**You signed the contract.**

- System has been running 18 months
- Reported crime: down 12% in surveilled areas
- Cost: $4.2M/year
- New complaint: ACLU says 78% of flagged individuals are Black or Latino in a city that's 43% Black/Latino

*What do you say?*

## Slide 7 — In-Role: Decision Time

## Slide 8 — The Revelation
**What the characters didn't know**

1. Alex is flagged partly because of *who Alex follows on social media* — friends who were arrested (not convicted)

2. The analyst's dashboard shows the score — **not why the score was generated**

3. The "12% reduction in reported incidents" includes incidents people stopped reporting because they distrusted the system

*60 seconds of silence. Take that in.*

---

## Slide 9 — Cross-Role Discussion
**Rearrange: One A, One B, One C per group**

- What did your character know that others didn't?
- Could any single person in this system have stopped what happened to Alex?
- Is anyone in this simulation a villain?

*Listen for this: harm without malice.*

## Slide 13 — Real-World Anchors
**This isn't hypothetical**

- **Palantir** — surveillance contracts with ICE, NYPD, Chicago PD
- **China's Social Credit System** — population-scale behavior scoring
- **Chicago's Strategic Subject List** — predictive "heat list"
- **Amazon Ring + police** — private surveillance shared with law enforcement

*Which of these feels most like the simulation? Most different? Why?*

---

## Slide 14 — Structural Interventions
**Three levels of change**

| Level | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **Technical** | Explainability requirements; bias audits |
| **Organizational** | Human review before action; data type bans |
| **Legal/Policy** | City council approval; bans on predictive policing |

Your group: propose ONE intervention. Defend it.

*Why not just "fix the algorithm"?*
