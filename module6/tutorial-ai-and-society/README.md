# Online Tutorial — AI and Society

**Module:** 6 — AI and Society: Who Benefits, Who Gets Left Behind  
**Format:** Video tutorial (~8 minutes)  
**Used In:** Lesson 6.1 (assign as pre-work or in-class discussion starter)  
**Audience:** Students with basic AI familiarity; no technical background required

## Overview

A video tutorial exploring how AI systems affect real people and communities. Uses a single hiring algorithm case study as a through-line to examine: who builds AI, who it affects, how bias enters systems, why AI decisions are hard to challenge, and what accountability can look like.

## Content Sections (~60 sec each)

1. Hook — a story about a job application that never got a response
2. Who builds AI — teams, incentives, blind spots
3. Who AI affects — the hiring case study, Part 1: the job seeker
4. How bias enters systems — data, proxies, feedback loops
5. Why AI decisions are hard to challenge — opacity and scale
6. What accountability looks like — audits, laws, community advocacy
7. The bigger picture — systemic vs. individual framing
8. Wrap-up and what you can do
