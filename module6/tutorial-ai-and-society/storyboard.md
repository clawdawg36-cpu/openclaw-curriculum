# Storyboard: AI and Society
**Module 6 — Online Tutorial**
**Format:** ~12 scenes across ~8 minutes
**Layout:** Each scene includes timestamp, narration excerpt, visual description, on-screen text/callouts, and production cues.

---

## Scene 01 — [00:00–00:20] Opening Hook: Jordan's World

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Meet Jordan. Jordan just graduated from college with a degree in marketing, a solid GPA, and two internships on their résumé."

**Visual description:**
Illustrated portrait of Jordan (gender-neutral, early 20s) at a desk. Laptop open. Stack of résumé printouts nearby. Warm apartment lighting — hopeful, not bleak. Subtle animation: fingers on keyboard.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Character label fades in: "Jordan — Recent Graduate"
- Ambient text: "47 Applications. 3 Weeks."

**Production cues:**
- Illustration style: flat design with muted warm palette
- No dialogue — narrator only
- Soft ambient sound: keyboard typing, city background

**Transition:** Slow zoom out to show the laptop screen — résumé visible

---

## Scene 02 — [00:20–00:45] The Silence

**Narration excerpt:**
> "For 31 of those jobs, they never heard back. Not a rejection email. Not a phone call. Just silence."

**Visual description:**
Split-screen: left shows Jordan checking email inbox (all empty). Right shows a growing tally counter: "Applications sent: 47 / Responses received: 16." Counter animates slowly. The 31 missing responses visualized as grey, hollow circles.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Counter animation: "47 sent → 16 responses → 31 silent"
- Subtle callout: "What happened to the other 31?"

**Production cues:**
- Sound design: notification sound for each "response received"; silence for each grey circle
- Pacing: slow, deliberate — let the silence land

**Transition:** Grey circles morph into a stylized algorithm flowchart

---

## Scene 03 — [00:45–01:45] Who Builds AI?

**Narration excerpt:**
> "The teams that design AI tools are overwhelmingly concentrated in a small number of large tech companies… The communities most affected by high-stakes AI are drastically underrepresented among the people building it."

**Visual description:**
Stylized graphic: a globe with bright dots over San Francisco, Seattle, New York, London, Beijing. Then zoom into a stylized "AI team" office. Animated demographic bar chart appears — showing gender, education, and geographic skew in AI workforces. Not accusatory — informational.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Map label: "Most AI R&D happens here →" (arrows to the five cities)
- Bar chart title: "Who Works in AI?"
  - Gender: ~80% male (approximate industry average)
  - Education: ~75% advanced degree
  - First-generation college students: underrepresented
- Key callout: "Who builds a system shapes what it sees as normal."

**Production cues:**
- Bar chart should animate in segment by segment as narrated
- Palette: neutral informational — avoid making this feel like an indictment
- Source note (small, bottom corner): "Approx. figures based on published AI workforce surveys"

**Transition:** Wipe to résumé → algorithm pipeline

---

## Scene 04 — [01:45–02:45] Jordan's Application: The ATS Pipeline

**Narration excerpt:**
> "The ATS does several things: it parses your résumé, compares it to the job description, and scores each applicant. The top scorers move forward."

**Visual description:**
Animated flowchart — clear, step-by-step:
1. Jordan submits résumé (paper icon enters pipeline)
2. ATS parses résumé (magnifying glass over document)
3. Keywords extracted (highlighted text: "marketing," "campaign," "analytics")
4. Score assigned (0–100 gauge animates to 34)
5. Score compared to threshold (red line at 60)
6. ❌ Filtered out — résumé falls into "rejected" pile

Second pass: another résumé (different candidate) goes through same pipeline, scores 72, gets a green ✅ → moves to "human review."

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Step labels: "Submit → Parse → Score → Filter → (Maybe) Human Review"
- Callout on Jordan's score: "Score: 34 / 100 — Why?"
- Callout: "98% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS systems"

**Production cues:**
- Animation should feel mechanical — conveyor belt metaphor works well
- Color Jordan's résumé icon in a distinct color so it's trackable through the pipeline
- Use simple iconography: no real résumé data visible

**Transition:** Freeze on the "Score: 34" and zoom in

---

## Scene 05 — [02:45–03:20] Bias Entry Point 1: Historical Data

**Narration excerpt:**
> "AI systems learn from data — and data reflects the world as it was, not as it should be."

**Visual description:**
Two-panel visual:
- Left panel: "Training Data" — illustrated stack of past résumés/hiring records with a calendar label "2005–2020"
- Right panel: "What the model learned" — animated arrows showing correlations the model identified (e.g., "School X → hired," "Neighborhood A → not hired")

Overlay text animates: "The model faithfully learned the wrong lesson."

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Left panel label: "Historical hiring data"
- Right panel label: "Patterns the model extracted"
- Key callout: "Biased past → biased future"
- Example: "Historical hires: mostly from 3 universities → Model rates other schools lower"

**Production cues:**
- Keep this visual clear and schematic — no real company/school names
- Animation: arrows connecting "historical pattern" to "model output"

**Transition:** Slide right to next bias entry point

---

## Scene 06 — [03:20–03:45] Bias Entry Point 2 & 3: Proxies and Feedback Loops

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Sometimes the algorithm doesn't use a sensitive attribute directly — but uses something that correlates with it… Amazon famously had to scrap an AI hiring tool because it downgraded résumés that included the word 'women's.'"

**Visual description:**
Three-panel infographic:
1. **Proxy variables:** Illustrated Venn diagram — "zip code" overlapping with "race," "college name" overlapping with "class"
2. **Amazon example:** Simple text card with Amazon logo (or neutral "Company A") and the résumé-screening story
3. **Feedback loop:** Circular arrow diagram: biased decision → no hire → no success data → model reinforces bias

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Panel 1 callout: "Proxy = a variable that stands in for something you didn't mean to measure"
- Panel 2 callout: "Real case: Amazon AI screener, 2018 — downgraded 'women's' keywords"
- Panel 3 label: "Feedback loop: bias feeds itself"

**Production cues:**
- Amazon panel: cite publicly reported story; keep neutral tone
- Feedback loop: use circular arrow with clock icon to suggest ongoing cycle

**Transition:** Cut back to Jordan, now trying to get answers

---

## Scene 07 — [03:45–04:45] Why It's Hard to Challenge

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Jordan wants to know why they weren't selected. They call HR. They're told: 'We receive a high volume of applications and aren't able to provide individual feedback.'"

**Visual description:**
Animated sequence: Jordan picks up phone → phone rings → automated message visual → Jordan sends email → bounces back with canned "high volume" reply. Frustrated expression.

Then: a "black box" graphic fades in — a literal dark cube labeled "AI Model" with inputs going in (résumés) and outputs coming out (scores), but the inside is invisible.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Phone/email panels: "High volume. Can't provide feedback."
- Black box label: "Proprietary algorithm — no transparency required"
- Callout list (appears one by one):
  - ❌ No disclosure that AI was used
  - ❌ No criteria shared
  - ❌ No appeal process
  - ❌ No legal requirement to explain

**Production cues:**
- Phone/email sequence should feel frustrating — slow, bureaucratic
- Black box graphic: clean, symbolic — not technically complex
- Consider ambient sound: hold music for the phone sequence

**Transition:** Dissolve to accountability split-screen

---

## Scene 08 — [04:45–05:45] What Accountability Looks Like

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Accountability is possible — and it's being built, slowly, in several ways."

**Visual description:**
Four-quadrant grid, each quadrant animating in as narrated:
1. **Audits** — magnifying glass over code/model
2. **Regulation** — gavel + EU flag / NYC skyline
3. **Litigation** — scales of justice
4. **Advocacy** — illustrated group of diverse people with megaphone

Each quadrant has a short real-world example label.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Grid title: "Paths to Accountability"
- Quadrant 1: "Algorithmic Audits — NYC Local Law 144 (2023): employers must audit and disclose AI hiring tool bias results"
- Quadrant 2: "Regulation — EU AI Act (2024): employment AI classified as 'high risk'"
- Quadrant 3: "Litigation — Class actions filed in hiring, facial recognition, credit lending"
- Quadrant 4: "Advocacy — Algorithmic Justice League, AI Now Institute, community organizing"
- Bottom callout: "Accountability requires all of these."

**Production cues:**
- Quadrants should feel hopeful — not triumphant, but forward-moving
- Use real organization names (they're public-facing)
- Keep each quadrant label brief — narration carries the detail

**Transition:** Zoom out to wide-angle city shot

---

## Scene 09 — [05:45–06:20] The Bigger Picture

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Jordan's experience isn't a bug in the system. In many ways, it's the system working exactly as designed — optimizing for efficiency and pattern-matching, without any mechanism to check whether those patterns encode historical injustice."

**Visual description:**
Wide establishing shot: Jordan's desk zooms out to show an entire neighborhood, then a city, then a network diagram of AI decision points overlaid on the city. Each node labeled: "Hiring," "Lending," "Healthcare," "Education," "Criminal Justice." Animated lines connect them — showing how AI touches every major life domain.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Network node labels: Hiring · Lending · Housing · Healthcare · Education · Criminal Justice
- Callout: "One biased model × thousands of employers = millions of people affected"
- Key line: "AI doesn't erase historical injustice. It automates it."

**Production cues:**
- This is the emotional high point of the video — let the visual breathe
- Network diagram should feel interconnected, not chaotic
- Music: slight swell here, then settle for the next section

**Transition:** Cut back to Jordan — now active, looking things up

---

## Scene 10 — [06:20–07:00] Jordan's Next Move

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Jordan, in our story, eventually found a job — through a referral, from a friend, which bypassed the algorithm entirely. That's meaningful. But it also illustrates the problem."

**Visual description:**
Return to Jordan, but now in a different setting — at a library or coffee shop, reading. Screen shows a website about employment rights. Another person is with them — a friend, making the referral call. Warm, realistic illustration.

Subtle parallel: on-screen browser shows "Know Your Rights: AI in Hiring" — acknowledging that Jordan had to seek this information out, and not everyone knows it exists.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Jordan's browser: "AI in Hiring: Know Your Rights"
- Callout: "The referral worked. The system still didn't."
- Reflection text: "Who gets the referral? Who already has the network?"

**Production cues:**
- Tone: bittersweet, not triumphant — Jordan got through, but the system isn't fixed
- Show Jordan as resourceful and informed, not just lucky

**Transition:** Soft wipe to "What You Can Do" card

---

## Scene 11 — [07:00–07:45] What You Can Do

**Narration excerpt:**
> "What can you do? Learn the landscape. Ask questions. Support accountability. Build differently. Stay curious."

**Visual description:**
Clean card layout — five action items appear one by one as narrator speaks. Each has an icon and a short label. Background is calm, slightly warm — approachable, not lecture-y.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Card title: "What YOU Can Do"
- Items (with icons):
  1. 🧠 **Learn the landscape** — AI is making decisions about you. Know where.
  2. ❓ **Ask questions** — Was AI involved? What are your rights?
  3. 📢 **Support accountability** — Back transparency laws, support advocacy orgs.
  4. 🔧 **Build differently** — If you work in tech: audit, explain, include affected communities.
  5. 🔍 **Stay curious** — This field moves fast. Informed citizens are the check.
- Footer: "You don't have to be an engineer to demand better."

**Production cues:**
- Pacing: give each item 5–7 seconds — don't rush
- Icons: simple, accessible line-art style
- Color: each item could have a distinct color accent — still accessible (see accessibility-notes.md)

**Transition:** Fade to closing title card

---
