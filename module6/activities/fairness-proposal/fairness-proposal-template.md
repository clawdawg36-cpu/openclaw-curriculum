## 📄 Your Fairness Proposal

A fairness proposal is a formal written document that:
1. Identifies a specific AI system causing harm
2. Makes a clear argument for why change is needed
3. Proposes concrete, actionable changes
4. Addresses likely objections
5. Calls for a specific action

This is a **1-page written proposal**. Every section counts. Be concise and precise.

---

## Instructions

- Proposal length: **1 page** (approximately 400–500 words when complete)
- Use the structure below. Each section is short — be specific, not vague.
- Your proposal should be something you could actually send to a company, a school board, or a policymaker.
- You'll be assessed on: clarity of argument, specificity of evidence, quality of proposed solution, and how well you address the "so what."

## Self-Assessment Checklist

Before submitting, check:

☐ My problem statement is specific (names the system, not vague)  
☐ I identified who is harmed — not just "some people"  
☐ My evidence goes beyond my own opinion  
☐ My recommendations are actionable (someone could actually do them)  
☐ I addressed an objection, not just dismissed it  
☐ My ask is clear and specific  
☐ My proposal is approximately 1 page  
☐ I would be comfortable sending this to the actual organization

## Proposal Rubric

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeds | 3 — Meets | 2 — Approaching | 1 — Beginning |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Problem Statement** | Precise, specific, no ambiguity | Clear and accurate | Somewhat vague | Missing or unclear |
| **Evidence** | Specific data/examples, well-sourced | Relevant examples used | General claims, little support | Opinion only |
| **Recommendations** | Specific, actionable, feasible | Clear and reasonable | Vague or impractical | Missing |
| **Addresses Objection** | Engages strongest counterargument | Notes objection, partially responds | Acknowledges but dismisses | Missing |
| **Clarity & Concision** | Tight, purposeful, persuasive | Clear, mostly focused | Rambling or off-topic at times | Hard to follow |
| **The Ask** | Specific, time-bound, realistic | Clear ask present | Vague | Missing |

## Example Strong Proposal Opening

*(This is an example — write your own, don't copy this)*

> **Regarding:** Automated essay scoring used for 8th-grade English assessments  
> **Addressed to:** Riverside Unified School District Board of Education  
>
> **The Problem:** Riverside USD currently uses the EssayBot AI system to score student writing in grades 6–8. EssayBot assigns grades based on vocabulary complexity and sentence length — but research shows this penalizes students whose first language is not English, even when the ideas in their writing are sophisticated. This is not a grading edge case: it is the primary evaluation tool for one-third of the ELA grade.
