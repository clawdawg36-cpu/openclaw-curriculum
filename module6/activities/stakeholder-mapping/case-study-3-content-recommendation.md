## 📋 The Scenario

**FeedFlow** is the recommendation algorithm used by a major social media platform with 2 billion users. Every time you open the app, FeedFlow decides what you see — which posts, videos, ads, and accounts appear in your feed and in what order.

The algorithm's core goal: **maximize time on platform**. It has learned that content triggering strong emotional reactions (anger, fear, outrage, excitement) keeps people scrolling longer.

**What researchers and whistleblowers have found:**
- The algorithm amplifies emotionally charged content, including misinformation, because it drives engagement
- Teenage girls who interact with diet content are increasingly shown eating disorder content
- In multiple countries, FeedFlow has been linked to the spread of political violence and ethnic hatred — because outrage content spread faster
- The algorithm personalizes so aggressively that users in different "bubbles" see almost completely different versions of reality
- Internal research showed the platform increases teen depression and anxiety, but this was not made public

**Arguments from the platform:**
- The algorithm shows people what they *want* to see (revealed preference)
- Billions of people use the platform voluntarily
- The company employs thousands of content moderators
- Removing the algorithm would make the platform less useful

---

## Key Facts to Consider

- **The company** earns $120B/year, mostly from targeted ads
- **Advertisers** pay more when their ads are shown to engaged users
- **The platform is free** — users pay with their data and attention
- **Users** largely don't know how recommendations work
- **Researchers** who want to study the algorithm are denied access
- **Teenagers** spend an average of 5 hours/day on the platform
- **Governments** in multiple countries are attempting to regulate the algorithm
