## 📋 The Scenario

**TalentSort** is an AI tool used by large companies to screen job applicants. Before a human recruiter ever reads a resume, TalentSort scans it and assigns a score from 1–100. Applicants below a certain score are automatically rejected. The system was trained on 10 years of hiring data from the company — meaning it learned from decisions made by humans who may have had their own biases.

**Discovered problems:**
- The system downgraded resumes that mentioned all-women's colleges
- It rated candidates from certain zip codes lower
- Applicants who included words like "chess club" scored higher than those who listed community organizations
- The company's existing workforce is 78% male and majority white

The company uses TalentSort because it's faster and cheaper than having humans screen thousands of applications.

---

## Key Facts to Consider

- **Applicants never know** their score or that AI screened them
- **The company** saves roughly $2M/year using this tool
- **Recruiters** now only see pre-filtered candidates
- **The vendor** (TalentSort) keeps the scoring algorithm secret ("proprietary")
- **Job seekers from underrepresented groups** have lower pass-through rates
- **Government regulators** are just starting to look at AI hiring tools
