## 📋 The Scenario

**PredictPatrol** is an AI system used by a city police department to predict where crimes are likely to happen and which individuals are at elevated risk of committing crimes in the next 6 months. Officers are assigned patrol routes based on the AI's predictions. Individuals flagged as "high risk" may receive extra check-ins from officers.

The system is trained on historical arrest and crime report data going back 20 years.

**Known issues with the system:**
- Neighborhoods with historically higher police presence have more recorded crimes → more police → more recorded crimes (a feedback loop)
- "Risk scores" for individuals are based partly on who they associate with
- The system has a 30% false positive rate — meaning 3 in 10 people flagged as "high risk" have no future contact with police
- People flagged as high risk are not told about their score
- Two independent studies found the system flags Black and Latino residents at significantly higher rates than white residents with similar histories

**Arguments in favor:**
- Crime has dropped 12% in areas where it's deployed
- It's more "objective" than individual officer intuition
- Allows limited police resources to be deployed efficiently

---

## Key Facts to Consider

- **The city** signed a 5-year, $3.4M contract with PredictPatrol
- **Officers** are expected to follow routing suggestions but technically have discretion
- **Flagged residents** don't know they're on a list
- **The vendor** argues their algorithm is protected intellectual property
- **Civil liberties groups** have sued to make the system's methodology public
- **Crime stats** show a drop — but researchers disagree on whether AI caused it
