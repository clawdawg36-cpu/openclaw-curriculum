## 🎤 What Is a Structured Debate?

A structured debate is a formal argument where two sides make claims, support them with evidence, and respond to each other's points. Unlike a regular argument, there are rules:

- You must support your claims with **evidence or reasoning** — not just opinions
- You must listen to and **directly respond** to the other side
- You earn points for logic, evidence, and respectful engagement — not volume

In this debate, you may be assigned a position you don't personally agree with. That's the point. Understanding the strongest version of both sides makes you a better thinker.

## 🧠 Debate Tips

- **Cite specifics.** "Research shows..." is weaker than "A 2023 MIT study found..."
- **Acknowledge nuance.** "This is true, but it doesn't account for..."
- **Attack the argument, not the person.** "That reasoning has a flaw..." not "You're wrong because..."
- **Ask clarifying questions.** If you don't understand their point, ask before you rebut.
- **The strongest move:** Acknowledge a good point from the other side, then explain why your position still wins.
