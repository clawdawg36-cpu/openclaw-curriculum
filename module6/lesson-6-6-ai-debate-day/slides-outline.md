## Slide 2 — Welcome to the Ring
**Why debate?**

Because after 5 lessons of investigation, you need to argue.

- Not just feel
- Not just analyze
- **Argue** — with evidence, against the strongest opposition

*The best way to understand a position is to defend it and be challenged.*

---

## Slide 3 — Debate Format
**The structure**

| Phase | Time | Who |
|-------|------|-----|
| Opening statement | 2 min | Each side |
| Rebuttal | 1.5 min | Each side |
| Cross-examination | 2 min | Both sides |
| Closing | 30 sec | Each side |
| Audience Q&A | 2 min | Class → teams |

*Total per debate: ~10 minutes*

---

## Slide 4 — The Four Topics

**1.** Schools should be prohibited from using facial recognition.

**2.** Platforms should use AI to auto-remove misinformation without human review.

**3.** Courts should be prohibited from using AI risk scores in sentencing.

**4.** AI-generated art and writing should be protected by copyright law.

*Your side is assigned. You argue it.*

---

## Slide 5 — Judging Criteria
**What wins a debate**

- ✅ **Clarity** — We understand your position
- ✅ **Evidence** — You support claims with facts/examples
- ✅ **Counterargument** — You engage the other side
- ✅ **Persuasion** — Did you move anyone?

*Note: "I believe this strongly" is not evidence. What do you know?*

## Slide 7 — Research Phase
**30 minutes. Use them.**

Step 1 (10 min): Your position + 3 arguments + 2 counterarguments
Step 2 (15 min): OpenClaw queries — find evidence, cases, data
Step 3 (5 min): Practice your 2-minute opening with a partner

*Hard stop at time — you'll build under pressure in the debates too.*

---

## Slide 8 — Debate 1
**Facial Recognition in Schools**

> *"School districts should be prohibited from using AI facial recognition systems on school property."*

FOR: Privacy rights, chilling effect, disparate impact
AGAINST: Security, consent frameworks, missing persons

---

## Slide 9 — Debate 2
**Algorithmic Content Moderation**

> *"Social media platforms should use AI to automatically remove misinformation without human review."*

FOR: Scale problem, human reviewers too slow, clear harm
AGAINST: Free speech, who defines misinformation, over-removal risk

---

## Slide 10 — Debate 3
**AI in Criminal Justice**

> *"Courts should be prohibited from using AI risk scores in sentencing and bail decisions."*

FOR: Documented racial disparities, due process, opacity
AGAINST: Human judges also biased, consistency, risk management

---

## Slide 11 — Debate 4
**AI Creative Rights**

> *"AI-generated art and writing should be protected by copyright law."*

FOR: Economic protection, creative investment
AGAINST: AI can't author, trained on unconsented work, chills human creativity

---

## Slide 12 — Debrief
**What did you learn?**

1. Did arguing a position you don't hold change your thinking?
2. What was the strongest argument you heard on any side?
3. Did your OpenClaw agent give you anything you couldn't verify?
4. Using AI to argue about AI — does that feel like a problem?

## Slide 13 — The Meta-Lesson

> *"AI can help you construct an argument.*
> *It cannot decide what you believe.*
> *It cannot evaluate evidence for you.*
> *Those are human jobs.*
>
> *The better the AI assistant, the more important it is to maintain that distinction."*
