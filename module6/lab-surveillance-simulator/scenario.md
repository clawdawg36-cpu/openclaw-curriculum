# The Scenario: Project SAFE

**Share this document with students at the start of Period 1.**

---

## Background

Westview Unified School District is considering a new AI system called **Project SAFE** (Student Activity and Facility Enhancement). The vendor, DataGuard Inc., has presented the system to the school board as a way to improve campus safety and academic outcomes.

---

## What Project SAFE Would Do

If approved, Project SAFE would:

1. **Monitor physical movement** — cameras with AI face recognition track which students are where on campus throughout the day. The system flags "anomalous" patterns (e.g., a student spending long periods in a bathroom, loitering near the parking lot).

2. **Scan social media activity** — the system scans public social media posts by students and flags anything it considers a potential threat indicator (certain keywords, emojis, or posting patterns).

3. **Analyze in-class behavior** — cameras in classrooms use AI to estimate student engagement levels (looking at faces, posture, and movement). This data is reported to teachers weekly.

4. **Generate risk scores** — the system combines all of the above into a daily "student risk score" for each student. School counselors are alerted when a student's score exceeds a threshold.

5. **Store data for 5 years** — all data is stored on DataGuard Inc.'s servers for five years. The district has a contract that allows DataGuard to use "anonymized" data for research and product improvement.

---

## The Decision

The school board has called a **Community Input Meeting** before voting on whether to approve Project SAFE. Five groups have been invited to speak:

- **School Administration** (represented by the Principal and Vice Principal)
- **Students** (represented by the Student Council)
- **Parents** (represented by the Parent-Teacher Association)
- **DataGuard Inc.** (the tech vendor)
- **Civil Liberties Advocates** (representatives from a digital rights organization)

Each group has 5 minutes to present their position. Then there will be an open discussion, and the board will ask questions.

---

## The Question Before the Board

> **Should Westview Unified School District deploy Project SAFE?**
> If yes — under what conditions, with what limits?
> If no — what alternatives, if any, should be considered?

---

## Your Job

You have been assigned to represent one of the five stakeholder groups. Your job is to:

1. **Understand your group's interests** — What matters most to you? What are you afraid of? What do you want?
2. **Prepare your group's statement** — What is your position? What evidence or arguments support it?
3. **Ask hard questions** — What would you want to know from the other groups?
4. **Negotiate if needed** — Are there conditions under which you'd change your position?

Remember: real people make these decisions. Your role is to think like them — even if you personally disagree with that stakeholder's position.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Surveillance** | Systematic monitoring of people's behavior, activities, or information |
| **Risk score** | A numerical value generated by an algorithm to predict future behavior |
| **Algorithmic bias** | When an AI system produces systematically unfair results for certain groups |
| **FERPA** | Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act — federal law protecting student education records |
| **Fourth Amendment** | Constitutional protection against unreasonable searches and seizures |
| **Chilling effect** | When surveillance causes people to change their behavior out of fear, even if they've done nothing wrong |
| **Data retention** | How long organizations keep data they've collected about people |
