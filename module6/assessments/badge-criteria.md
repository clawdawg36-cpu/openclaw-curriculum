# Module 6 Badge Criteria — Ethics Advocate 🛡️

**Module:** Module 6: AI Ethics, Bias & Fairness  
**Badge Name:** Ethics Advocate  
**Badge Tagline:** *"I don't just use AI — I hold it accountable."*

---

## What Is the Ethics Advocate Badge?

The Ethics Advocate badge recognizes students who have demonstrated both the knowledge and the practical skills to critically examine AI systems, identify fairness issues, and take concrete steps toward more responsible AI design and use.

Earning this badge means you didn't just learn *about* AI ethics — you **did** the work.

---

## Badge Requirements

To earn the Ethics Advocate badge, you must complete **all three** of the following:

---

### Requirement 1: Complete the Bias Audit ✅

**What:** A full, documented bias audit of an AI system, submitted as part of the KEY LAB.

**Evidence required:**
- Completed Bias Testing Workshop Documentation Template (see `bias-testing-workshop-template.md`)
- Evidence of multiple tests with documented inputs and outputs
- Written analysis identifying bias type(s) and likely root cause(s)
- At least two proposed remedies

**Rubric:** Scored using the KEY LAB rubric (`rubric-key-lab.md`), Dimension 1 — Bias Audit Quality  
**Minimum score to count toward badge:** 3/4

---

### Requirement 2: Write a Usage Policy ✅

**What:** A complete usage policy for the AI system you audited (or a comparable AI tool).

**Evidence required:**
- Written usage policy document (minimum 200 words)
- Must address: permitted uses, prohibited uses, data privacy, stakeholder responsibilities, consequences for misuse

**Rubric:** Scored using the KEY LAB rubric (`rubric-key-lab.md`), Dimension 2 — Usage Policy Completeness  
**Minimum score to count toward badge:** 3/4

---

### Requirement 3: Pass the Ethics Scenarios Quiz ✅

**What:** A short quiz presenting 5–8 real-world AI ethics scenarios. Students must correctly identify the ethical issue, name the relevant concept(s), and briefly explain what should be done.

**Format:** Written responses (not multiple choice). Responses are scored on accuracy and reasoning.

**Concepts covered:** All 13 vocabulary terms from `vocabulary.md` — with emphasis on: algorithmic bias, feedback loops, stakeholder analysis, fairness, transparency, accountability, responsible disclosure.

**Minimum score to pass:** 70% (or teacher-set threshold)

**Note:** The Ethics Scenarios Quiz is administered by the teacher. Students may retake once after a review session.

---

## How to Claim Your Badge

1. Confirm all three requirements are complete (use the self-assessment form: `self-assessment.md`)
2. Let your teacher know you're ready
3. Teacher verifies your KEY LAB score and quiz result
4. Badge is issued in the learning management system (or via physical card/sticker, per teacher's discretion)

---

## Badge Display

Once earned, the Ethics Advocate badge may be:
- Added to your digital portfolio
- Displayed on your class profile page
- Referenced in your portfolio reflection (see `portfolio-prompt.md`)

---

## A Note on What This Badge Means

Earning the Ethics Advocate badge doesn't mean you have all the answers about AI ethics — no one does. It means you've built the **habits of mind** to ask better questions: *Who benefits? Who's harmed? What could go wrong? What should we do about it?*

Those questions matter. Keep asking them.
