# Portfolio Prompt — Module 6 Artifact

**Module:** Module 6: AI Ethics, Bias & Fairness  
**Type:** Portfolio / Culminating Artifact  
**Student Choice:** Option A or Option B (see below)

---

## Overview

At the end of Module 6, you will select **one artifact** to represent your learning and add it to your course portfolio. This is your chance to show what you've built, analyzed, and argued — and to reflect on what it means.

You have two options. Choose the one that best represents your strongest work.

---

## Option A: Fairness Proposal

**What you submit:**
- Your completed **Fairness Proposal** (400–800 words)
- A **short portfolio reflection** (100–150 words) answering: *Why did you choose this artifact? What does it show about your learning? What would you change or improve if you had more time?*

**What it demonstrates:**
Your ability to identify a real fairness problem in AI, analyze stakeholders, and propose a concrete, evidence-backed solution.

**Scoring:** Your proposal will be evaluated using the Fairness Proposal rubric (`rubric-fairness-proposal.md`). The portfolio reflection is not separately scored but should be substantive.

---

## Option B: Usage Policy + Bias Audit Report

**What you submit:**
- Your completed **Usage Policy** (minimum 200 words) from the KEY LAB
- Your completed **Bias Audit Report** (Bias Testing Workshop Documentation Template, see `bias-testing-workshop-template.md`)
- A **short portfolio reflection** (100–150 words) answering: *Why did you choose this artifact? What does it show about your learning? What would you change or improve if you had more time?*

**What it demonstrates:**
Your ability to investigate a real AI system for bias, document your findings rigorously, and create a practical governance document (usage policy) to address misuse.

**Scoring:** The Usage Policy and Bias Audit are evaluated using the KEY LAB rubric (`rubric-key-lab.md`). The portfolio reflection is not separately scored but should be substantive.

---

## Reflection Prompts (both options)

Your portfolio reflection should address at least **two** of the following:

- Why did you choose this artifact over the alternative?
- What was the hardest part to figure out, and how did you work through it?
- What feedback did you receive during the process, and how did you respond to it?
- If you could revise this artifact, what would you change and why?
- What does this artifact say about who you are as a thinker about AI?

---

## Submission

Submit your portfolio artifact (proposal or lab documents) along with your reflection to the portfolio section of the LMS by the date your teacher specifies.

**Checklist before submitting:**

- [ ] I have chosen Option A or Option B
- [ ] My main artifact is complete and meets the minimum requirements
- [ ] My portfolio reflection is 100–150 words and addresses at least two prompts
- [ ] I have reviewed my work against the appropriate rubric
- [ ] I am proud to have this in my portfolio

---

## Teacher Notes

- Portfolio artifacts may be shared in a class gallery walk (with student permission) at the end of the unit.
- Students who complete the Bias Audit and Usage Policy (Option B) are also meeting two of the three requirements for the **Ethics Advocate** badge. See `badge-criteria.md`.
- Encourage students to reuse and revise work from their labs and drafts — the portfolio artifact should be polished, not first-draft.
- The portfolio reflection is a low-stakes metacognitive exercise; keep it ungraded or give simple completion credit.
