# Module 6 — Daily Exit Ticket Templates

**Module:** Module 6: AI Ethics, Bias & Fairness  
**Purpose:** Formative assessment — capture student thinking at the close of each lesson  
**Format:** 3–5 minutes, 2–3 questions per ticket  
**Submission:** Index card, shared doc, or LMS exit ticket tool

---

## Lesson 6.1 Exit Ticket — What Is Algorithmic Bias?

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. In your own words, define **algorithmic bias**. Give one example from today's lesson or your own life.

2. What is a **proxy variable**? Why might one accidentally introduce bias into an AI system?

3. Agree or disagree (and briefly explain): *"If an algorithm uses objective data, it can't be biased."*

---

## Lesson 6.2 Exit Ticket — Where Does Bias Come From?

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. List **two sources of bias** that can enter an AI system. For each, describe how you would try to catch it before deployment.

2. What is a **feedback loop**? Draw or describe a simple example of a feedback loop making a biased system worse over time.

3. What is one question you still have about bias in AI data or training?

---

## Lesson 6.3 Exit Ticket — Bias Simulation & Role-Play

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. What **role did you play** in today's simulation? (e.g., data collector, algorithm designer, affected community member)

2. What would you do **differently** if you were actually designing the system? Be specific — name one concrete change.

3. Whose perspective was hardest to represent in the simulation? Why does that matter when building real AI systems?

## Lesson 6.4 Exit Ticket — Surveillance, Deepfakes & Privacy

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. What is one way **AI-powered surveillance** can be used for public safety, and one way it can be misused?

2. Define **deepfake** in one sentence. Name one potential harm and one potential beneficial use.

3. Where do you personally draw the line on AI surveillance? What would need to be true for you to feel it was acceptable?

---

## Lesson 6.5 Exit Ticket — Stakeholders & Fairness Frameworks

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. Pick an AI system (real or hypothetical). List **three stakeholders** affected by it. Who benefits most? Who carries the most risk?

2. What is the difference between **fairness as equal treatment** and **fairness as equal outcomes**? Can both be achieved at once?

3. Name one thing a designer could do to make an AI system more **transparent** and one thing to make it more **accountable**.

---

## Lesson 6.6 Exit Ticket — Usage Policies & Guardrails

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. What is a **usage policy** for an AI system? What should one include?

2. What is a **guardrail** in the context of AI? Give a specific example.

3. Draft one rule you would put in a usage policy for a hypothetical AI tutoring assistant used in this class.

---

## Lesson 6.7 Exit Ticket — Responsible Disclosure & Ethics Advocacy

**Name:** _______________________ **Date:** _______ **Period:** _______

1. What does **responsible disclosure** mean? Why is it important when someone discovers a flaw or bias in an AI system?

2. What is **alignment** in AI? What happens when an AI system is *misaligned*?

3. Reflect on Module 6 as a whole: What is the most important thing you're taking away? What will you do differently as someone who uses — or might one day build — AI systems?
