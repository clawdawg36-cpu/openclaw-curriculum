# KEY LAB — Summative Rubric

**Module:** Module 6: AI Ethics, Bias & Fairness  
**Assessment Type:** Summative — Laboratory Assessment  
**Lab Title:** Bias Audit + Usage Policy Lab  
**Total Points:** 12 (3 dimensions × 4-point scale)  
**Cross-reference:** See *Module 6 Lab Guide* for full procedural instructions and `bias-testing-workshop-template.md` for the documentation template used during the lab.

---

## Overview

The KEY LAB asks students to:
1. Conduct a structured **bias audit** of a real or provided AI system
2. Write a **usage policy** for that system (or a related AI tool)
3. Implement or design a working **guardrail** — a rule, filter, or check that prevents a specific misuse or harmful output

This rubric assesses the quality of the final lab submission. The Bias Testing Workshop documentation template is a required artifact.

---

## Scoring Rubric

### Dimension 1: Bias Audit Quality

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Audit is thorough and methodologically sound. Student tested the system with multiple varied inputs, documented results systematically (using the workshop template), correctly identified the type(s) of bias present (or provided a well-reasoned conclusion that none was detected), traced the likely root cause, and proposed specific, feasible remedies. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Audit is complete and generally well-executed. Tests are varied; results are documented. Bias identification is reasonable, though root cause analysis or proposed remedies may lack depth. |
| **2 — Developing** | Audit is present but incomplete. Tests are limited in scope or variety; documentation is partial. Bias identification may be surface-level (naming the bias without evidence) or conclusions are unsupported by the tests run. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Audit is missing, or consists of one or two informal observations without structured testing or documentation. No meaningful bias analysis present. |

---

### Dimension 2: Usage Policy Completeness

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Usage policy is comprehensive and well-organized. Includes: intended use cases, prohibited uses, data privacy considerations, stakeholder responsibilities, consequences for misuse, and a process for updating the policy. Language is clear enough for the intended audience (e.g., students, users, administrators). |
| **3 — Proficient** | Usage policy covers most required elements. At least four of the six key components are addressed clearly. Minor gaps in one area (e.g., update process or stakeholder responsibilities) do not significantly undermine the policy's usefulness. |
| **2 — Developing** | Usage policy is present but incomplete. Covers only the most obvious elements (allowed/prohibited uses) without addressing data privacy, consequences, or governance. May read as a list of rules rather than a coherent policy. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Usage policy is missing or is a single sentence/short paragraph that does not constitute a real policy. |

---

### Dimension 3: Working Guardrail

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Guardrail is clearly defined and demonstrably functional (tested and shown to work). Directly addresses a specific harm or misuse identified in the bias audit or usage policy. Student explains *how* the guardrail works (the logic or mechanism), *why* this particular harm was chosen, and what the guardrail's limitations are. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Guardrail is functional and addresses a relevant harm. Explanation of how it works is present; may not fully articulate limitations or the connection to the bias audit findings. |
| **2 — Developing** | Guardrail is described but may not be demonstrated as working, may be too vague to implement, or may address a harm not clearly connected to the system studied. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Guardrail is missing, is a theoretical statement without implementation, or does not constitute a real constraint on system behavior. |

## Required Artifacts

Students must submit all of the following for a complete lab assessment:

- [ ] Completed **Bias Testing Workshop Documentation Template** (`bias-testing-workshop-template.md`)

- [ ] **Guardrail** — either: working code, a structured prompt filter, a decision tree, or a detailed design specification with test cases
