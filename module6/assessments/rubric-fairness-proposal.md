# Fairness Proposal — Summative Rubric

**Module:** Module 6: AI Ethics, Bias & Fairness  
**Assessment Type:** Summative — Written Proposal  
**Total Points:** 16 (4 dimensions × 4-point scale)

---

## Overview

Students submit a **Fairness Proposal** identifying a real or realistic AI system with a documented or potential fairness problem, analyzing stakeholders, and proposing a concrete, evidence-backed solution.

---

## Scoring Rubric

### Dimension 1: Problem Statement Clarity

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Problem statement is specific, focused, and unambiguous. The AI system is clearly named/described, the fairness issue is precisely articulated, and the affected population is explicitly identified. Reader immediately understands what the problem is and why it matters. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Problem statement is clear and addresses most key elements. The system and issue are identifiable, though one element (scope, population, or stakes) may be slightly vague. |
| **2 — Developing** | Problem statement is present but lacks specificity. The AI system may be described too broadly, the issue is partially defined, or the affected group is assumed rather than stated. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Problem statement is missing, too vague to evaluate, or describes a non-fairness issue. Reader cannot determine what system or problem is being addressed. |

---

### Dimension 2: Stakeholder Analysis Depth

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Identifies three or more distinct stakeholders with nuanced analysis of how each is affected (benefits, harms, power dynamics). Considers both direct and indirect stakeholders. Demonstrates understanding that stakeholder interests may conflict. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Identifies at least two stakeholders with reasonable analysis of impact. Most relevant groups are represented; analysis may miss one indirect stakeholder or overlook a key tension. |
| **2 — Developing** | Identifies stakeholders but analysis is surface-level. May list groups without explaining how they are specifically affected, or may focus only on the most obvious party. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Stakeholder analysis is absent or identifies only one group without meaningful analysis. |

---

### Dimension 3: Fairness Solution Feasibility

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Proposed solution is specific, actionable, and realistic given known technical and social constraints. Addresses root cause (not just symptoms). Anticipates potential trade-offs or unintended consequences and discusses how to manage them. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Proposed solution is reasonable and generally feasible. Addresses the core problem, though it may not fully account for implementation challenges or trade-offs. |
| **2 — Developing** | Proposed solution is present but vague, overly broad ("just make it fair"), or technically implausible without acknowledgment. Does not address root cause or implementation path. |
| **1 — Beginning** | No solution is proposed, or the solution is irrelevant to the identified problem. |

---

### Dimension 4: Evidence Use

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Integrates multiple forms of evidence (data, examples, research, course concepts) to support both the problem identification and the proposed solution. Evidence is accurately cited and clearly connected to the argument. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Uses relevant evidence to support key claims. At least one piece of evidence is well-integrated; minor gaps may exist in how evidence connects to the solution. |
| **2 — Developing** | Some evidence is present but may be loosely connected, unverified, or used only for the problem (not the solution) or vice versa. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Little to no evidence. Claims are asserted without support, or evidence cited is irrelevant or inaccurate. |

## Teacher Notes

- This rubric is designed for proposals of 400–800 words (adjust length expectation as needed).
- Students may choose any AI system for their proposal; encourage specificity over abstraction.
- Consider pairing this with the portfolio prompt (see `portfolio-prompt.md`) for students who choose the Fairness Proposal as their Module 6 portfolio artifact.
