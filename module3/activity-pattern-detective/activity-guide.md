# 🔍 Pattern Detective — Activity Guide

**Module 3 | Pattern Recognition Warm-Up**
**Time:** 10–15 min (warm-up) or 20–30 min (full activity)
**Format:** Individual or pairs

---

## Your Mission

You are a Pattern Detective. Your job: look at data, find the hidden rule, and explain why that rule matters.

Every card in `pattern-cards.md` has a sequence, table, or dataset with a hidden rule — just like the patterns an AI learns from training data. Your job is to find it.

> **Why this matters:** AI doesn't memorize answers. It finds rules — patterns in data that let it predict what comes next. If it finds the right rule, it makes good predictions. If it finds the wrong rule, it makes confident mistakes. You're about to see both.

## The AI Connection

Every sequence on your cards is a tiny version of what AI does with language, images, and decisions.

When an AI reads the text: *"The capital of France is ___"* — it's not looking up an answer in a database. It learned that "Paris" follows that pattern from millions of examples of that sentence (or sentences like it) in its training data.

**That's pattern recognition.** And it's why:

| Situation | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Pattern appears in training data | AI answers correctly and confidently |
| Pattern is rare in training data | AI may guess or hallucinate |
| Pattern was learned wrong (noisy data) | AI gives confident wrong answers |
| Pattern never appeared in training data | AI has no basis for a response |

> **Your custom skill is a way of giving the AI a new pattern it never learned:** your specific data, rules, or context. That's how you make it smarter for your use case.

## Bonus Challenges

### 🧩 Rule Depth Challenge
Can you find a pattern that requires 4+ values before the rule becomes clear? How would an AI trained only on the first 3 values get it wrong?

### 🌐 Real-World Pattern Hunt
Find a real dataset (weather data, sports scores, stock prices) and look for a pattern. What rule fits? Is it always consistent? What breaks the pattern?

### 🤖 Ask OpenClaw
Give OpenClaw one of your sequences and ask it to find the rule. Does it get it right? What happens when you give it a trick card? Does it notice the error or confidently continue the broken pattern?
