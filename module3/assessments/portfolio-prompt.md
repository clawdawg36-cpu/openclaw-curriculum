# Module 3 Portfolio Prompt
## Artifact: Pet Advisor Decision Tree + Agent Skill Reflection

> **Portfolio Section:** Module 3 — How AI Thinks
>
> **Time to complete:** ~30–45 minutes (may be done in class or at home)
>
> **Format:** 2-part submission — one visual artifact + one written reflection

---

## What You're Submitting

Your Module 3 portfolio entry has two pieces:

### Part A: Your Pet Advisor Decision Tree (Visual Artifact)

Include your **completed Pet Advisor decision tree** from the Lesson 3.2 Design Challenge.

This can be:
- A photo of your hand-drawn tree
- A digital version created in a drawing tool, flowchart maker, or Scratch
- A printed copy of a digital file

**Your tree must show:**
- [ ] At least 3 levels of decision nodes
- [ ] Clear Yes/No labels on all branches
- [ ] At least 3 different pet recommendations as leaves
- [ ] Your name and date somewhere on the artifact

---

### Part B: Written Reflection — What Can Your Agent Do? What Can't It?

Write **1–2 paragraphs** (approximately 150–250 words) responding to this prompt:

> *Your Pet Advisor decision tree is now a skill your agent could use to help people find the right pet.*
>
> **What can your agent do well with this new skill?** Describe at least one thing it handles effectively and explain why.
>
> **What can your agent NOT do — or might it get wrong?** Describe at least one real limitation of your decision tree, and connect it to something you learned this module about how AI thinks (features, patterns, decision trees, hallucination, reasoning, knowledge base).

**You should use at least 2 vocabulary words from Module 3 in your reflection.**

---

## Example Sentence Starters

Struggling to start? Try one of these:

- *"My Pet Advisor tree does a good job of… because it uses the feature of… to…"*
- *"One limitation of my tree is that it can't handle… This is similar to how AI hallucinates when…"*
- *"If someone gives my agent unexpected input — like [example] — it would probably… because my decision tree doesn't have a node for…"*
- *"After this module, I understand that AI reasons well when… but struggles when…"*

---

## Scoring

Your portfolio entry will be reviewed (not formally graded) using these criteria:

| What we're looking for | Strong | Developing |
|---|---|---|
| **Artifact is complete** | Tree has 3+ levels, labeled branches, 3+ leaf outcomes | Tree is partial or unlabeled |
| **Reflection answers both questions** | Clearly identifies one strength AND one limitation | Only addresses one, or answers are vague |
| **Uses module vocabulary** | 2+ terms used correctly in context | Terms absent or used incorrectly |
| **Specific and honest** | Reflection shows real thinking about the limits of *your* tree | Generic ("AI can be wrong sometimes") |

---

## A Note on Honesty

The best reflections are the honest ones. You don't have to pretend your decision tree is perfect — it isn't! That's not the point. The point is to show that you understand *why* it has limits and what those limits tell us about how AI works.

An agent that only knows what you built into its tree is a powerful image of AI in general: it can only reason about what it was designed to handle. The skill you just built, and its gaps, are a miniature version of every AI system ever deployed.
