# Skill Showcase Guide
## How to Demo Your Skill to the Class

**Format:** 2-minute demo  
**Audience:** Your classmates + teacher  
**Goal:** Show what you built and explain why it's cool

---

## 🎯 What Makes a Great Demo?

A great demo is **clear**, **specific**, and **surprising**. You want people to think: *"Whoa, I didn't know an AI could do that!"*

The worst demo just shows something working without explaining *why* it matters. Don't do that.

---

### **[0:00 – 0:20] Hook — Start with a question**

Open with a question that makes people curious. Don't say "Hi, I'm going to show you my skill." Instead, try:

- *"Quick question — does anyone know what the weather is in Tokyo right now?"* (pause)
- *"What's the funniest type of joke? Knock-knock? Dad jokes? Let me show you something..."*
- *"What if you could have a personal tutor who never gets tired of quizzing you?"*
- *"What do wolves, octopuses, and axolotls have in common with my AI agent?"*

---

## 💡 Tips for Nervousness

- **Everyone wants you to succeed.** Your classmates are curious, not critical.
- **If something breaks:** Say "Interesting — let me try this instead" and move on.
- **Speak slower than you think you need to.** Nerves make us talk fast.
- **Look up sometimes.** It's okay to glance at your notes, but make eye contact when you say your closing line.

---

## 🏆 What Makes a Demo Stand Out?

| Good Demo | Great Demo |
|-----------|-----------|
| Shows the skill working | Shows it AND explains why it's different from before |
| Reads from notes | Knows it well enough to improvise |
| Finishes on time | Has a strong closing that makes people think |
| Shows one example | Shows a surprising or funny example |

---

**One last thing:** Be proud. You actually built something. Most people who use AI have never done what you just did. 🎉
