# 🎤 Stump the AI — Class Showcase

**Module 3 | Optional Sharing Format**
**Time:** 10–20 minutes (depending on group size)
**Format:** Whole class or small groups

---

## Overview

After completing the activity, students share their best "stump" with the group. This is a low-stakes, high-energy share-out that celebrates curiosity and critical thinking.

The goal is **not** to show off who's "smarter than the AI" — it's to collectively map the landscape of AI failure and start asking: *what would it take to fix this?*

---

## Format Options

### Option A: Lightning Round (5–10 min)
Each student/pair shares in 60 seconds or less:
1. Read the question
2. Say what OpenClaw got wrong
3. Name the failure category
4. Done!

Fast-paced, high energy. Great for larger groups.

---

### Option B: Deep Dive (10–20 min)
Pick 3–5 of the most interesting stumps and spend 2–3 minutes on each:
1. Presenter reads the question aloud
2. Class guesses what OpenClaw would say
3. Presenter reveals the actual response
4. Group discusses: *why* did it fail?
5. Quick vote: 🔴 Hard fix / 🟡 Medium fix / 🟢 Easy fix with a skill

More discussion, more insight. Great for smaller groups or when a few stumps are especially rich.

---

### Option C: Gallery Walk (15–20 min)
Students post their Failure Report Cards around the room (digital or physical). Everyone walks around and adds sticky-note reactions:
- ⭐ "Most surprising failure"
- 💡 "Best fix idea"
- 🤔 "I want to understand this better"

At the end, debrief by collecting the starred and "fix idea" cards.

---

## Showcase Presenter Template

Use this structure if presenting to the class:

---

**My Question:**
> *(read it out loud)*

**What I Expected:**
> *(one sentence — what should the AI have said?)*

**What OpenClaw Actually Said:**
> *(read the response — or summarize if it's long)*

**The Failure:**
> *(where exactly did it go wrong?)*

**My Failure Category:**
> *(from the reference sheet)*

**My Fix Idea:**
> *(what would a skill need to fix this?)*

---

## Audience Participation Cards

Print or project these prompts for the audience to respond after each presentation:

| Card | Prompt |
|---|---|
| 🙋 **Me too!** | I found a similar failure |
| 😲 **Whoa** | I didn't expect that failure type |
| 🔧 **Fix it!** | Here's how a skill could fix this: ___ |
| ❓ **Question** | What happens if you rephrase it as: ___ |

---

## Group Awards (Optional & Fun)

At the end of the showcase, take a class vote on:

🏆 **Best Stump** — Most creative question that exposed a real failure

🔬 **Most Instructive** — The failure that taught everyone the most

🛠️ **Most Fixable** — The stump most likely to be fixed with a skill someone could write today

🤯 **Most Surprising** — Nobody saw that failure coming

> Awards can be emoji reactions, sticky dots on a wall, or just a show of hands. Keep it light.

---

## Debrief Discussion

After the showcase, bring the group together for 5 minutes of discussion:

**Question 1:** What failure category showed up the most?
*(Tally on a whiteboard — any patterns?)*

**Question 2:** Which failures could OpenClaw never fix on its own, and which ones just need the right skill?
*(Map onto the 🔴/🟡/🟢 fixability scale)*

**Question 3:** If you had to write one skill to fix one failure type, which would you choose — and what would the skill need to know or do?
*(This plants the seed for the skill-writing module)*

---

## Connection to Skill Writing

Close the showcase with this framing:

> "Every 🟢 failure you found today is basically a skill spec. You just described what the skill needs to know. In the next activity, you'll actually write one."

This is the payoff of the whole exercise — students have now *diagnosed* a real AI limitation and can see exactly how a skill would patch it.

---

*Optional: Collect all Failure Report Cards into a class "AI Failure Map" — a running document of discovered failures that students can add to throughout the module.*
