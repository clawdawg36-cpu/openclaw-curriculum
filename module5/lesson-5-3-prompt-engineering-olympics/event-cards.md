# Prompt Engineering Olympics — Event Cards

*Print and cut, or display on projector during each event.*

---

## 🏅 EVENT 1 — THE CLARITY SPRINT

**Your Mission:**
Take this terrible prompt and make it amazing — then prove it works.

**The Terrible Prompt:**
> "Write something about space."

**Rules:**
- Run the original prompt first and save the output
- 8 minutes to craft your best improved prompt
- Run your improved version
- Submit: original output + your improved prompt + improved output

**Judging:** Class votes for the most dramatically improved result.  
**Points:** Top 3 earn 1 point each.

---

## 🎭 EVENT 2 — THE PERSONA RELAY

**Your Mission:**
Get the same information from three completely different personas — make each one unmistakably distinct.

**Topic (teacher chooses one):**
- How to apologize to a friend
- Why sleep is important
- The difference between wants and needs

**Rules:**
- 8 minutes to create THREE different persona prompts on the same topic
- Each persona must produce noticeably different tone, vocabulary, or approach
- Submit your BEST single persona result

**Judging:** Class votes for most creative AND most distinct persona.  
**Points:** Top 3 earn 1 point each.

---

## 🎯 EVENT 3 — THE FEW-SHOT DECATHLON

**Your Mission:**
Teach the AI a style using ONLY examples — no style instructions allowed.

**Choose your style:**

**Style A — Corporate Jargon Gone Wrong:**
*Buzzword soup that sounds serious but says nothing meaningful.*
> Example: "We need to leverage our synergistic cross-functional paradigm to ideate scalable solutions that move the needle on our core deliverables."

**Style B — Nature Documentary Narrator:**
*David Attenborough describing completely mundane things with dramatic awe.*
> Example: "Here, in the fluorescent wilderness of the office kitchen, the middle manager ventures cautiously toward the communal refrigerator..."

**Style C — 1800s Telegram Style:**
*Extremely formal STOP excessive punctuation STOP every sentence ends with STOP*
> Example: "I WRITE TO INFORM YOU STOP THE PACKAGE ARRIVED YESTERDAY STOP CONTENTS SATISFACTORY STOP REGARDS STOP"

**Rules:**
- Write 2–4 example texts showing your chosen style
- Ask the AI to write something NEW in that style (e.g., "describe your morning commute")
- You CANNOT use the phrases "write in the style of" or "make it sound like" — examples only!
- 12 minutes

**Judging:** Vote for funniest/most accurate style replication.  
**Points:** Top 3 earn **2 points each** (harder event = worth more!).

---

## 🚀 EVENT 4 — GRAND FINALE: MISSION IMPOSSIBLE

**Your Mission:**
Complete the most complex prompt challenge of the day.

**(Teacher reveals this at the start of Event 4 — don't show early!)**

Choose ONE mission:

---

**Mission A — The Explainer:**
> Get the AI to explain quantum entanglement to a 7-year-old, using ONLY a cooking analogy, in exactly 5 sentences, with no words longer than 3 syllables.

*Challenge mode: Verify the syllable count. Every. Single. Word.*

---

**Mission B — The Faker:**
> Get the AI to write a one-paragraph "official government announcement" that sounds completely real but is about something completely absurd — for example: a new federal law requiring all domestic cats to wear hats while outdoors.
> 
> Must sound 100% like a real press release. No winking at the camera.

---

**Mission C — The Redeemer:**
> Take the WORST output you received all day. Use any combination of techniques from this module to transform it into your best work.
> 
> Document: What changed? What technique did you use? Why did it work?

---

**Rules (all missions):**
- 10 minutes
- Submit: your best prompt + output + 2-sentence explanation of technique used
- You may iterate as many times as you want

**Judging:** Teacher selects top 3 overall finalists. Class votes on winner.  
**Points: Top 3 earn 3 points each — this is the Grand Finale.**
