# Slides Outline — Lesson 5.3: Prompt Engineering Olympics

**Module:** 5 — Give Your Agent a Voice  
**Lesson:** 5.3 — Prompt Engineering Lab: The Olympics  
**Format:** ~12 slides across 2 periods

---

## Slide 1 — Title / Opening Ceremony

**Headline:** 🏅 Prompt Engineering Olympics  
**Subhead:** Two periods. Four events. One champion.  
**Visual:** Olympic-style rings made from chat bubbles / prompt text  
**Note:** Enter with energy. Announce teams. Post scoreboard.

---

## Slide 2 — The Rulebook

**Headline:** How it works  
**Content:**
- 4 events, each with its own task and judging criteria
- Submit your best prompt + output to the class doc after each event
- Events 1 & 2: 1 point each (first two podium slots)
- Events 3 & 4: 2–3 points (harder = worth more)
- Judging: class vote + teacher tiebreaker

**Visual:** Trophy icons, scoreboard template  
**Note:** Pause here. Make sure every student has the shared doc open and AI access working.

---

## Slide 3 — Event 1: The Clarity Sprint ⚡

**Headline:** Event 1 — The Clarity Sprint  
**Subhead:** Turn this terrible prompt into an amazing one  
**Content:**
```
The terrible prompt:
"Write something about space."
```
**Rules:**
- Run the original prompt first (save it)
- 8 minutes to craft the best improved version
- Run your improved version
- Submit both outputs to the shared doc

**Visual:** Countdown timer (8:00)  
**Note:** Start timer immediately. Circulate.

---

## Slide 4 — Event 1: Judging

**Headline:** Event 1 — Let's see your work  
**Content:**
- 2–3 volunteers share their improved prompt + result
- Class vote: thumbs up for most improved
- Top 3 earn a point

**Judging question:** Which component of a specific prompt made the biggest difference?

**Note:** Keep judging to 5 min. Announce points on the scoreboard.

---

## Slide 5 — Event 2: The Persona Relay 🎭

**Headline:** Event 2 — The Persona Relay  
**Subhead:** Same topic. Three completely different voices.  
**Content:**

**Topic:** [Teacher chooses one]
- How to apologize to a friend
- Why sleep is important  
- The difference between wants and needs

**Rules:**
- 8 minutes to get responses from THREE different personas
- All on the same topic
- Submit your BEST single persona result

**Visual:** Three distinct character icons (scientist, chef, coach)  
**Note:** Timer starts now.

---

## Slide 6 — Event 2: Judging

**Headline:** Event 2 — Personality contest  
**Content:**
- Share your best persona prompt + output
- Class votes: Most creative AND most distinct persona
- Top 3 earn a point

**Debrief:** Why does assigning a role change the output? (connect back to Lesson 5.1)

---

## Slide 7 — Halftime Scoreboard 📊

**Headline:** Halftime — Where do we stand?  
**Content:** [Live scoreboard — teacher fills in]  
**Visual:** Podium with team names  
**Note:** Announce current standings. Build hype for Period 2. "Events 3 and 4 are worth MORE."

---

## Slide 8 — Event 3: The Few-Shot Decathlon 🎯

**Headline:** Event 3 — The Few-Shot Decathlon  
**Subhead:** Teach the AI a style using ONLY examples — no instructions allowed  
**Content:**

Choose your style:
- **A:** Corporate jargon gone wrong
- **B:** David Attenborough narrating mundane things
- **C:** 1800s telegram style STOP

**Rules:**
- Write 2–4 example texts showing the style
- Ask the AI to write something NEW in that style
- Cannot use "write in the style of" — examples only!
- 12 minutes

**Visual:** Example text blocks in each style  
**Note:** Read one example from each style aloud so students understand the options.

---

## Slide 9 — Event 3: Judging

**Headline:** Event 3 — Style check  
**Content:**
- Top 3 results shared
- Vote: Did it nail the style? How many examples did it take?
- Top 3 earn **2 points** (this one's harder!)

**Debrief:** How many examples before the AI got it? What does that tell us about few-shot learning?

---

## Slide 10 — Event 4: Mission Impossible 🚀

**Headline:** 🚨 GRAND FINALE — Mission Impossible  
**Subhead:** Choose your mission. Use everything you know.  
**Content:**

**Mission A — The Explainer:**
> Explain quantum entanglement to a 7-year-old using only a cooking analogy. Exactly 5 sentences. No words longer than 3 syllables.

**Mission B — The Faker:**
> Write an "official government announcement" about a new law requiring all cats to wear hats. Must sound 100% real.

**Mission C — The Redeemer:**
> Take your worst output of the day. Use any technique to turn it into your best. Document what you changed.

**Rules:** 10 minutes. Submit: prompt + output + 2-sentence technique explanation.  
**Points: 3 points to top 3 — worth the most!**

**Note:** REVEAL this slide at the start of Period 2, Event 4 — don't show early.

---

## Slide 11 — Award Ceremony 🏆

**Headline:** 🥇 And the winner is…  
**Content:** [Live — announce final tallies and winners]  
**Visual:** Podium with confetti graphic  
**Note:** Celebrate loudly. Give prizes if you have them. Then 2 min reflection.

**Class reflection:**
- "What technique was most powerful today?"
- "What would you do differently?"

---

## Slide 12 — Exit Ticket

**Headline:** Before you go — reflect  
**Content:**
> In your journal: Name one technique you used today. What were you trying to achieve with it? Did it work?

**Visual:** Timer (2 minutes)  
**Note:** Collect journals or scan as students leave.

---

## Teacher Notes — Slide Deck Setup

- Put Event slides (3, 5, 8, 10) on separate slides with built-in timers — use https://www.online-stopwatch.com or embed a GIF countdown
- Slide 10 (Mission Impossible): KEEP THIS HIDDEN until Period 2, Event 4. Students should not see their mission in advance.
- Scoreboard: simplest approach is a whiteboard column — or use a Google Sheet projected live
- Shared doc: create a simple table with columns (Team / Event / Prompt / Output) — students paste as they go
