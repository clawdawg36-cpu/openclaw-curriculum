## 🧱 THE 5 ELEMENTS OF A GREAT PROMPT

| Element | What It Means | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Role** | Tell the AI who to BE | *"You are a friendly tutor for 7th graders..."* |
| **Context** | Give background info | *"I'm writing a persuasive essay for English class..."* |
| **Task** | Say exactly what you want | *"Write a 3-paragraph argument that..."* |
| **Format** | Specify structure/length | *"Use bullet points, max 200 words"* |
| **Constraints** | Set limits or rules | *"No jargon, must be school-appropriate"* |

---

## 🔧 KEY TECHNIQUES

### 1. Role Prompting
Tell the AI to take on a persona or expertise level.
> *"Act as a marine biologist explaining tides to a middle schooler."*

### 2. Few-Shot Examples
Show the AI what you want by giving 1–3 examples first.
> *"Here's the format I want: Q: [question] → A: [short answer]. Now do this for 5 questions about photosynthesis."*

### 3. Chain of Thought
Ask the AI to show its work / think step by step.
> *"Solve this math problem and explain each step."*
> *"Think through this carefully before answering."*

### 4. Constrain the Output
Specify exactly what you want (and don't want).
> *"Give me 5 ideas, each in one sentence, no repeating concepts."*

### 5. Audience Specification
Name who the response is for.
> *"Explain this for a 10-year-old." / "Write this for a college professor."*

### 6. Tone Control
Name the vibe or feeling you want.
> *"Write this in a friendly, casual tone." / "Be formal and professional."*

### 7. Iteration / Refinement
Ask the AI to improve its own answer.
> *"That's good, but make it funnier." / "Shorter please — 3 sentences max."*

---

## ✅ DO THIS

- ✅ Be **specific** — vague prompts get vague answers
- ✅ Set the **audience** — who is this for?
- ✅ Ask for a **format** — list, paragraph, table, steps?
- ✅ Give **context** — why do you need this?
- ✅ **Iterate** — treat it like a conversation, not a one-shot
- ✅ **Verify** — always check important facts yourself
- ✅ **Experiment** — small changes can make a huge difference

---

## ❌ DON'T DO THIS

- ❌ Don't be vague: *"Help me"* → tells the AI nothing
- ❌ Don't assume it knows your context — spell it out
- ❌ Don't accept the first response blindly — refine it
- ❌ Don't trust AI for facts without checking a real source
- ❌ Don't forget the audience — "explain" means different things for different people
- ❌ Don't write one giant sentence — structure your prompt clearly
- ❌ Don't share personal info just to give context

## 🆚 WEAK vs. STRONG — Side by Side

| Weak Prompt | Strong Prompt |
|-------------|---------------|
| *"Write a story"* | *"Write a 150-word funny story about a shy robot who discovers it loves karaoke. Audience: 7th graders."* |
| *"Explain photosynthesis"* | *"Explain photosynthesis in 5 bullet points for a 6th grader who's never heard the word before. Use a food analogy."* |
| *"Help with my essay"* | *"I'm writing a persuasive essay arguing that school should start later. Give me 3 strong arguments with one supporting fact each."* |
| *"What's good about AI?"* | *"List 3 benefits AND 3 risks of AI in healthcare. Be balanced. Use plain language. Max 2 sentences per point."* |
| *"Make a study guide"* | *"Create a study guide for the American Revolution covering: causes, key events, and outcome. Use headers, bullet points, and bold key terms. Grade 8 level."* |

## 🗂️ QUICK TEMPLATES

**For Explanations:**
```
Explain [TOPIC] to [AUDIENCE] using [FORMAT].
Keep it [LENGTH]. Use [ANALOGY/EXAMPLE] if helpful.
```

**For Creative Writing:**
```
Write a [LENGTH] [TYPE] about [TOPIC].
Tone: [TONE]. Include: [ELEMENTS]. Audience: [WHO].
```

**For Research / Info:**
```
I'm [CONTEXT]. List [NUMBER] [THINGS] about [TOPIC].
Format: [FORMAT]. Focus on [SPECIFIC ASPECT].
```

**For Feedback:**
```
Here is my [WRITING/CODE/PLAN]: [PASTE HERE]
Give me [NUMBER] specific suggestions to improve [ASPECT].
Be [DIRECT/GENTLE]. Max [LENGTH].
```

## 🚨 REMEMBER: AI IS NOT A SEARCH ENGINE

| AI Is Good At | AI Struggles With |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Explaining concepts | Real-time facts |
| Brainstorming ideas | Knowing what happened yesterday |
| Editing/rewriting | Perfect accuracy on specific numbers |
| Generating examples | Citing real sources reliably |
| Changing format/tone | Understanding your personal situation |

**Always verify important facts from a real source!**
