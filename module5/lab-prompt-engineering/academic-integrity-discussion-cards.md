## How to Use These Cards

1. **Small groups** (3–4 students) receive one card
2. **Read** the scenario together
3. **Discuss** the questions for 3–5 minutes
4. **Vote** as a group: Acceptable, Not Acceptable, or It Depends
5. **Present** your verdict and reasoning to the class

**There are no trick questions — some of these are genuinely hard!**

**Scenario:**
Sam has a research paper due. She asks the AI to explain three key concepts in the topic. The AI gives clear explanations — but also mentions three specific statistics. Sam includes those statistics in her paper without checking if they're real. One of them turns out to be completely made up. The teacher marks the paper as having false information.

**Discussion Questions:**
1. Who is responsible for the false information — Sam or the AI?
2. Should Sam have verified the statistics? Why didn't she?
3. What's the difference between using AI as a learning tool vs. a source of facts?
4. How do you think about AI's reliability when it comes to specific facts and numbers?

## 📌 Key Vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Academic Integrity** | Being honest about your own work — not misrepresenting who did it |
| **Plagiarism** | Presenting someone (or something) else's words or ideas as your own |
| **Disclosure** | Telling your teacher/reader what tools or help you used |
| **AI Hallucination** | When an AI confidently states something that isn't true |
| **Verification** | Checking that information is accurate using a real, reliable source |
