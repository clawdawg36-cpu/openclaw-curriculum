## Slide 3 — Why Vague Fails

**Headline:** Vague prompt → vague output  
**Content:** Side-by-side comparison:

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Tell me about dogs." | "You are a vet advising a first-time owner of a rescue greyhound. Give 3 bullet-point tips for the first week." |

**Visual:** Two chat windows, one with a generic dog paragraph, one with bullet points  
**Note:** Live demo on projector first — advance to this slide to make the comparison visual.

---

## Slide 4 — The Six Components of a Specific Prompt

**Headline:** Make your prompt do more work  
**Content (animated reveal, one at a time):**

1. **Task** — What do you want the AI to do?
2. **Role** — Who is the AI being?
3. **Audience** — Who is this for?
4. **Format** — How should it look?
5. **Length/Scope** — How much?
6. **Constraints** — What to include or exclude?

**Visual:** A prompt broken into labeled color-coded sections  
**Note:** Ask students to identify which components the demo prompt used.

---

## Slide 5 — Role-Playing Technique

**Headline:** Give the AI a persona — watch the output shift  
**Content:**
- Role-playing = assigning the AI a character or expertise
- The model draws on text associated with that role
- Different role → different vocabulary, tone, recommendations

**Visual:** Three speech bubbles labeled: "Nutritionist," "Food Blogger," "Coach" — same question, different answers  
**Note:** Run the live demo with the "what to eat before a test" example.

---

## Slide 6 — Try It: Three Roles, One Topic

**Headline:** Your turn — three roles, one question  
**Content:**
- Pick a question you actually want answered
- Write THREE version of the prompt, each with a different role
- Which role gave the most useful answer?

**Visual:** Worksheet preview (prompt-comparison-worksheet.md)  
**Note:** Give 3–4 minutes. Circulate. Ask students what they discovered.

---

## Slide 7 — Few-Shot Prompting

**Headline:** Show, don't tell  
**Content:**
- Few-shot = giving the AI 2–3 examples of the pattern you want
- Why it works: examples go in the context window → model predicts the pattern

**Zero-shot vs. Few-shot demo:**
```
Zero-shot: "Write a product review for headphones."

Few-shot: [examples] "Now write one for headphones."
```

**Visual:** Side-by-side output comparison — generic vs. personality-matched  
**Note:** Read the example reviews aloud with some performance — make them funny.

## Slide 8 — Haiku Challenge: Rules

**Headline:** 🏆 The World's Greatest AI Haiku  
**Content:**
- 5 minutes to get your best haiku from the AI
- Must use at least TWO of today's techniques
- Must be a real haiku (5-7-5 syllables — check it!)
- Save your best prompt + best result
- We vote at the end

**Categories:**
- 🌸 Most Beautiful
- 😂 Most Funny
- 🤯 Most Surprising
- 🏅 Best Prompt (technique award)

**Visual:** Olympic rings but made of prompt text / speech bubbles  
**Note:** Set a visible timer. Encourage iteration — first attempt is never best.
