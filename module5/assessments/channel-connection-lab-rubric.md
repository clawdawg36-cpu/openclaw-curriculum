# Channel Connection Lab Assessment Rubric
## Module 5 — Lesson 5-6 / Lab: Channel Connection

**Total Points: 9** (1 pass/fail + 4-point test plan + 4-point reflection)

---

## Part 1: Working Connection Demonstrated — Pass / Fail

**Pass:** Student can demonstrate that their channel connection is functional. At minimum:
- OpenClaw receives a test message from the connected channel, **OR**
- OpenClaw successfully sends a message to the connected channel
- Student can show this live or via screenshot with timestamp

**Fail:** No working connection demonstrated. Connection attempted but could not be verified.

> **Note:** A "fail" here doesn't tank the grade — the test plan and reflection can still earn full marks. Struggling and documenting it honestly is a valuable learning outcome.

**Pass = 1 point | Fail = 0 points**

---

## Part 2: Test Plan Quality (4 points)

Students submit a written test plan documenting their testing approach.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Excellent** | Test plan includes at least 3 test cases. Each case has: a clearly described input, a specific expected output, and the honest actual output. Student tested both success and failure scenarios. |
| **3 — Good** | Test plan includes at least 2 complete test cases. Each has input, expected output, and actual output. May be missing one failure/edge case. |
| **2 — Developing** | Test plan exists but test cases are incomplete. May be missing expected output, or actual output is not filled in honestly. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Test plan is very sparse — only 1 test case, or cases are missing key components. |

---

## Part 3: Reflection Quality (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Excellent** | Reflection explains what worked, what didn't, what they troubleshot, and what they'd do differently. Shows genuine engagement with the technical challenge. If the connection failed, reflection explains where they got stuck and what they tried. |
| **3 — Good** | Reflection covers what worked and what they'd improve. Some engagement with challenges but could go deeper. |
| **2 — Developing** | Reflection is brief. Mostly describes what happened without analysis. ("I connected it and it worked" / "It didn't work.") |
| **1 — Beginning** | Reflection is missing or just one sentence with no substance. |

---

## Scoring Summary

| Part | Points |
|------|--------|
| Working connection (pass/fail) | 0 or 1 |
| Test plan quality | 1–4 |
| Reflection quality | 1–4 |
| **Total** | **2–9** |

| Total | Grade Equivalent |
|-------|-----------------|
| 8–9 | A |
| 6–7 | B |
| 4–5 | C |
| 2–3 | D |

---

## Teacher Notes
- Students who got a working connection but wrote a poor reflection score lower than students who *failed* but documented everything carefully. Make this explicit.
- The lab is about process, not just outcome. A thoughtful failure teaches more than a lucky success.
- Consider a "share-out" where 2-3 students explain their troubleshooting process to the class.
