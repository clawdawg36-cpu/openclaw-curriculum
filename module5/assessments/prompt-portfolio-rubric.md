# Module 5 Prompt Portfolio Rubric

## Overview
Students submit their **3 best prompts** from Module 5 along with a written reflection on their effectiveness.

**Total Points: 16**

---

## Submission Requirements
- 3 prompts (with the actual AI outputs included)
- One effectiveness reflection per prompt (or a combined reflection at the end)
- Chosen from any Module 5 activity

---

## Rubric

### Criterion 1: Specificity (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exceptional** | All 3 prompts include clear context, constraints, audience, and format. No ambiguity. Prompts are precise enough that another student would get a similar output. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Most prompts include specific context and constraints. Minor vagueness in one area. |
| **2 — Developing** | Some specificity present, but prompts are often too broad or missing key context. Output quality clearly suffers from vagueness. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Prompts are vague or one-word. Little evidence of intentional structure. |

---

### Criterion 2: Technique Use (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exceptional** | At least 2 named techniques applied correctly (e.g., few-shot, role assignment, chain-of-thought, format specification). Techniques are used intentionally, not accidentally. |
| **3 — Proficient** | At least 1 technique clearly applied. Student can identify what they used. |
| **2 — Developing** | Some technique-like patterns present but student may not have used them intentionally or can't name them. |
| **1 — Beginning** | No evidence of technique use. Prompts are unstructured requests. |

---

### Criterion 3: Output Quality (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exceptional** | AI outputs are accurate, useful, and clearly shaped by the prompt. Student chose prompts that actually worked well. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Outputs are mostly useful. Minor issues with accuracy or relevance. |
| **2 — Developing** | Outputs are partially useful but contain errors, off-topic content, or needed significant editing. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Outputs are unhelpful, hallucinated, or irrelevant to the stated goal. |

---

### Criterion 4: Reflection Depth (4 points)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4 — Exceptional** | Reflection clearly explains *why* the prompt worked, what technique was used, what the student would change, and what they learned about prompting from this experience. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Reflection explains why the prompt worked and identifies at least one specific improvement. |
| **2 — Developing** | Reflection is surface-level ("it worked" / "it gave a good answer") without analysis. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Reflection is missing, very brief, or just describes what happened without any analysis. |

---

## Scoring Guide

| Total | Grade Equivalent |
|-------|-----------------|
| 14–16 | A |
| 11–13 | B |
| 8–10 | C |
| 5–7 | D |
| 0–4 | Incomplete |

---

## Teacher Notes
- Encourage students to pick prompts they're *proud* of, not just ones that got long outputs.
- A short, precise prompt that got exactly the right answer is better than a long rambling one.
- Reflection quality matters more than prompt volume.
