# AI Expo Cheat Sheet
## Top 30 Terms You Need to Know

**OpenClaw AI Curriculum | Print-Friendly Reference Card**

> Cut this out. Tape it to your desk. Memorize it. Then explain these to someone at the Expo.

---

### FOUNDATIONS (Module 1)

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
Computers programmed to do things that normally require human thinking.

**Machine Learning**
AI that learns from examples — the more it sees, the better it gets.

**Algorithm**
A step-by-step set of instructions a computer follows to solve a problem.

**Training Data**
The examples a machine learning model learns from. Garbage in = garbage out.

**Neural Network**
AI inspired by the human brain — layers of connected nodes that find patterns.

**Model**
The "brain" a computer builds after training — used to make predictions.

**Bias (in AI)**
Unfair results caused by unbalanced or unrepresentative training data.

---

### HOW AI SEES & THINKS (Modules 2–3)

**Pixel**
The smallest unit of a digital image. AI sees images as grids of numbers.

**Classifier**
An AI model that sorts things into categories (spam/not spam, cat/dog).

**Accuracy**
% of predictions the model gets right. Training accuracy ≠ real-world accuracy.

**Feature**
A measurable property (color, shape, weight) that AI uses to make decisions.

**Overfitting**
When AI memorizes training examples but fails on new ones. Too specific.

**Decision Tree**
A series of yes/no questions that lead to a conclusion. Like a flowchart.

**Hallucination**
When AI confidently makes something up. Sounds right — but isn't.

**Representation**
How real-world info gets turned into numbers a computer can process.

---

### HOW AI LEARNS (Module 4)

**Training Loop**
The ML cycle: Collect Data → Label → Train → Evaluate → Iterate.

**Label**
The correct answer attached to a training example — tells the model the right output.

**Weights**
Adjustable numbers inside a model that encode what it has learned.

---

### TALKING TO AI (Module 5)

**Token**
The basic unit of text AI processes — roughly one word or word-part.

**Context Window**
How much text an AI can "see" and remember at once (in tokens).

**Prompt Engineering**
Carefully crafting your AI input to get better, more reliable outputs.

**Chain-of-Thought**
Asking AI to show its reasoning step by step — improves accuracy on hard problems.

**Few-Shot Prompting**
Teaching AI your format by including 2–5 examples in your prompt.

**API**
The rules for how two software programs talk to each other.

---

### AI ETHICS (Module 6)

**Algorithmic Bias**
Systematic unfairness in AI outputs that affects certain groups.

**Fairness**
Equitable treatment of all people by an AI system. Harder to define than it sounds.

**Transparency**
How visible and understandable an AI system's decisions are.

**Accountability**
Who is responsible when an AI system causes harm?

**Alignment**
Making sure AI goals actually match human values — not just what it was optimized for.

**Guardrail**
A technical or procedural constraint that prevents AI from producing harmful outputs.

---

## 3 Things Every Expo Visitor Will Ask

> Practice these answers out loud before the Expo.

**"What does your AI do?"**
*Use: Model, Classifier or Training Loop*

**"How did you teach it?"**
*Use: Training Data, Label, Accuracy*

**"Could it ever be wrong or unfair?"**
*Use: Hallucination, Bias (in AI), Overfitting, or Fairness*
