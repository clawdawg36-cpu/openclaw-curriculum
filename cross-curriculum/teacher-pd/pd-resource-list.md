# Teacher PD Resource List
## OpenClaw Curriculum — Curated Reading, Tool Links, and Community Resources

> **How to use this list:** Organized by category and session relevance. Resources marked **⭐ Essential** are highest priority before you start teaching. Resources marked **📚 Deep Dive** are for extending your own knowledge beyond what's needed to teach.

---

## Section 1: Core AI Literacy

### ⭐ Essential Reading — Before You Start Teaching

**AI4K12 Big Ideas in Artificial Intelligence**  
`https://ai4k12.org/resources/big-ideas-poster/`  
The five conceptual pillars that anchor the entire curriculum: Perception, Representation & Reasoning, Learning, Natural Interaction, Societal Impact. Read the poster; download the PDF. You'll reference this in almost every module.

**Machine Learning for Kids — Teacher Guide**  
`https://machinelearningforkids.co.uk/#!/teachers`  
Practical explanations of ML concepts written for non-CS teachers. Directly relevant to M2 and M4.

**How does machine learning work? (Plain English)**  
`https://vas3k.com/blog/machine_learning/`  
One of the best plain-English explanations of ML. Read Sections 1–3 before teaching M4. Long but worth it.

**Day of AI — Educator Background Reading**  
`https://dayofai.org/educators`  
MIT Media Lab's educator primers. The "What is AI?" and "Supervised Learning" primers are essential before M1 and M4.

---

### 📚 Deep Dive — If You Want to Go Further

**Crash Course AI (YouTube playlist)**  
`https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtO65LeD2p4_Sb5XQ51par_b`  
17 videos, ~10 minutes each. Excellent for building your own conceptual foundations. Watch before each module, not all at once.

**Elements of AI (Free online course)**  
`https://www.elementsofai.com/`  
Free, self-paced course from the University of Helsinki. 6 hours total. Not required — but excellent for teachers who want to understand the full picture. Start here if you're new to AI.

**3Blue1Brown — Neural Networks (YouTube)**  
`https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi`  
4-video series on how neural networks actually work. Watch before teaching M4. Visual, intuitive, no math background required.

## Section 2: AI Ethics and Equity

### ⭐ Essential Reading — Before Teaching M6

**ProPublica: Machine Bias (2016)**  
`https://www.propublica.org/article/machine-bias-risk-assessments-in-criminal-sentencing`  
The COMPAS investigation. This is the case study at the center of M6 Lesson 6.2. Read it in full before teaching that lesson. Understand the methodology — not just the headline — because students will ask.

**Gender Shades (Joy Buolamwini / Timnit Gebru, 2018)**  
`https://proceedings.mlr.press/v81/buolamwini18a.html`  
Or the accessible summary: `https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/gender-shades/overview/`  
The facial recognition accuracy disparity research. Used in M6 Lesson 6.2. The MIT Media Lab page is the accessible version; link the paper for advanced students.

**Algorithms of Oppression (Safiya Umoja Noble)**  
ISBN: 978-1479837243  
Deep examination of racial bias in search algorithms. M6 foundation reading. If you only read one book before teaching M6, read this one.

**Weapons of Math Destruction (Cathy O'Neil)**  
ISBN: 978-0553418811  
How algorithmic decision-making causes harm in education, employment, and criminal justice. Excellent companion to M6 content. Accessible for non-technical readers.

---

### ⭐ Essential — For Ethics Facilitation

**Teaching Controversial Topics: A Framework for Educators**  
`https://www.facinghistory.org/resource-library/teaching-controversial-topics`  
Facing History & Ourselves — excellent practical frameworks for facilitating hard conversations. Directly applicable to M6 facilitation.

**Restorative Circles for Classrooms**  
`https://www.iirp.edu/news/using-circles-in-schools`  
The closing ritual protocol referenced in the M6 surveillance simulator lesson. Understand the structure before you facilitate.

---

### 📚 Deep Dive

**The Alignment Problem (Brian Christian)**  
ISBN: 978-0393635881  
The most accessible book on why making AI behave the way we want is genuinely hard. Excellent preparation for M6 and M7's builder responsibility themes.

**Data Feminism (Catherine D'Ignazio and Lauren Klein)**  
`https://data-feminism.mitpress.mit.edu/` (open access)  
Power, privilege, and data. Connects M6 bias content to a broader framework of who benefits and who is harmed.

**AI Now Institute — Annual Reports**  
`https://ainowinstitute.org/reports`  
Annual research reports on AI's impact on society. Use for staying current on real-world cases between cohorts.

## Section 3: Curriculum and Pedagogy

### ⭐ Essential — For Understanding the Framework

**AI4K12 Progression Documents**  
`https://ai4k12.org/resources/`  
Grade-band progressions for all five Big Ideas. Essential for understanding where your course fits in a student's broader AI education.

**CSTA K-12 CS Standards**  
`https://www.csteachers.org/page/standards`  
The CS standards referenced in each module's teacher guide. Bookmark the searchable version; you'll use it when explaining standards alignment to administrators.

**Day of AI — Full Curriculum**  
`https://dayofai.org/curriculum`  
MIT Media Lab's supplemental AI curriculum. Use as a fallback when OpenClaw is unavailable. Excellent standalone lessons for M2 (perception), M4 (learning), and M6 (ethics).

---

### ⭐ Essential — For Differentiation

**UDL (Universal Design for Learning) Guidelines**  
`https://udlguidelines.cast.org/`  
The differentiation framework underlying the accessibility guidance in each module's teacher guide. Understand the three principles: representation, action/expression, engagement.

**Teaching AI Ethics to K-12 Students: A Framework**  
`https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3430895.3460144`  
Research-backed framework for age-appropriate AI ethics instruction. Particularly useful for calibrating M6 content for your specific grade level.

## Section 4: Tool Documentation

### OpenClaw

**OpenClaw Official Docs**  
`https://github.com/openclaw-ai/openclaw` *(or your institution's deployment URL)*  
Primary reference for all technical setup. Read the "Getting Started" and "Skills" sections before the PD. Know where the config file lives and what a SKILL.md looks like.

**SKILL.md Format Reference**  
Available in your OpenClaw installation: `docs/skill-format.md`  
Full documentation of what goes in a SKILL.md. Keep this open during Exercise 1.3 and M3.

### Teachable Machine

**Teachable Machine — Official Site**  
`https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/`

**Teachable Machine — FAQ and Troubleshooting**  
`https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/faq`  
Read before teaching M2. Especially: "Why is my model inaccurate?" and "Can I use Teachable Machine offline?"

**Teachable Machine — YouTube Channel (Google)**  
`https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCnUYZLuoy1rq1aVMwx4aTzw`  
Official tutorials. 3–5 minutes each. Watch "Train your first model" and "Export your model" before the PD.

### ML4Kids

**Machine Learning for Kids**  
`https://machinelearningforkids.co.uk/`

**ML4Kids Teacher Guide**  
`https://machinelearningforkids.co.uk/#!/teacher`  
How to create class accounts, manage students, and troubleshoot. Read this before teaching M4.

**ML4Kids Worksheets**  
`https://machinelearningforkids.co.uk/#!/worksheets`  
Pre-built activity worksheets. Use as offline fallbacks or differentiation materials.

### Hugging Face

**Hugging Face — What is Hugging Face?**  
`https://huggingface.co/docs/hub/index`  
Overview of the platform, how Spaces work, and how to find relevant models.

**Hugging Face Spaces — Browse**  
`https://huggingface.co/spaces`  
For teacher exploration in Exercise 2.3. Pre-browse before the PD to find 2–3 Spaces you might recommend to your participants.

**Hugging Face — Course (Free)**  
`https://huggingface.co/learn/nlp-course/chapter1/1`  
Optional deep dive. Chapter 1 only — the rest is more technical than you'll need.

### Day of AI

**Day of AI — Main Site**  
`https://dayofai.org/`  
MIT Media Lab's AI curriculum. Free, downloadable, designed for K-12.

**Day of AI — Educator Hub**  
`https://dayofai.org/educators`  
Teacher registration, lesson plans, implementation guides.

## Section 5: Professional Community

### ⭐ Join These Before Your First Cohort

**AI4K12 Teacher Community**  
`https://ai4k12.org/community/`  
The most active community of K-12 AI educators. Discussion forums, lesson plan sharing, regional meetups. The best place to ask "has anyone tried X?" questions.

**CSTA (Computer Science Teachers Association)**  
`https://www.csteachers.org/`  
CS education professional organization. If you're teaching this course as your first CS course, membership is worth it — access to standards documents, professional development, and local chapters.

**CS for All Teachers Community**  
`https://csforall.org/community/`  
Community for teachers from all backgrounds teaching CS. Good if you're an ELA or social studies teacher expanding into CS.

---

### Online Communities

**Reddit: r/CSEducation**  
`https://reddit.com/r/CSEducation`  
Active community of CS teachers K-12 and higher ed. Good for quick questions and lesson feedback.

**Twitter/X: #AI4K12 and #CSEd**  
Search these hashtags for active conversations. Most major AI education researchers and practitioners post here.

**AI in Education (LinkedIn Group)**  
Search "AI in Education" on LinkedIn — several active groups for EdTech and AI educators.

---

### Conferences and Events

**ISTE (International Society for Technology in Education) Annual Conference**  
`https://iste.org/conference`  
Largest ed-tech conference. AI in Education content has grown significantly since 2022.

**CSTA Annual Conference**  
`https://www.csteachers.org/page/annual-conference`  
CS-education-specific conference. More technically focused than ISTE; excellent for deepening content knowledge.

**AI4K12 Annual Symposium**  
`https://ai4k12.org/symposium/`  
K-12 AI education focused. Smaller, but highly targeted to this audience.

## Section 6: Research and Current Events

### For Staying Current (Subscribe)

**MIT Technology Review**  
`https://technologyreview.com`  
Accessible but rigorous AI journalism. Free tier has enough for most needs. Good for real-world examples to bring into M6.

**The Gradient**  
`https://thegradient.pub/`  
More technical ML research summaries. Best for teachers who want to go deeper; not required.

**Brookings Institution — AI and Tech Policy**  
`https://www.brookings.edu/topic/artificial-intelligence/`  
Policy-focused AI analysis. Excellent source for M6 governance discussions and M7 real-world context.

**AI Now Institute Blog**  
`https://ainowinstitute.org/`  
Research blog focused on AI's social implications. Directly relevant to M6 content.

---

### For Real-World Examples (Use in Class)

**Algorithm Watch**  
`https://algorithmwatch.org/`  
Catalog of real-world algorithmic systems and their documented impacts. Excellent for M6 case studies beyond COMPAS and Amazon.

**Distributed AI Research Institute (DAIR)**  
`https://www.dair-institute.org/`  
Timnit Gebru's organization. Research on AI harm, with emphasis on marginalized communities. Use for current case studies.

**Partnership on AI**  
`https://partnershiponai.org/`  
Industry + civil society coalition working on responsible AI. Their case studies on AI harm are balanced and well-sourced.
