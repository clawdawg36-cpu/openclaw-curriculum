## Overview & Methodology

This document covers prioritized keyword targets across all audience segments for the multi-page marketing site (`/`, `/educators`, `/homeschool`, `/school-boards`, `/policy`). Research is informed by:

- Phase 1 personas, sitemap, message matrix, and brand guidelines
- Competitive search landscape (MIT, AI4K12, Code.org, Create & Learn, CSTA)
- Search intent analysis per audience
- Long-tail opportunity mapping for a new/low-DA site

**Priority tiers:**

| Tier | Label | Strategy |
|------|-------|----------|
| P1 | **Primary** | Target in title, H1, meta description. Own this phrase. |
| P2 | **Secondary** | Use in body copy, subheadings, image alt text. |
| P3 | **Supporting** | Sprinkle throughout — FAQs, blog, schema. Builds topical authority. |

**Difficulty estimates** (without paid tool access — relative to a new domain going up against MIT, AI4K12, Code.org):

- 🟢 Low — niche long-tail; achievable within 3–6 months with quality content
- 🟡 Medium — competitive but winnable with strong content and links
- 🔴 High — dominated by .edu / .org; target only if page is exceptional

## Negative Keywords (Avoid Accidentally Targeting)

These could attract irrelevant traffic or wrong-fit audiences. Don't use as primary targets; avoid in meta copy unless clearly contextualized.

|---------|-----------|
| ai tutoring software | Product category we're not — implies AI teaches; we're human-led |

| chatgpt for students | Different product entirely; high volume, wrong intent |

## Schema / Structured Data Recommendations

To strengthen search visibility and potentially earn rich results:

- **`Course` schema** on `/curriculum` — title, description, provider, hasCourseInstance
- **`FAQPage` schema** on `/educators`, `/homeschool`, `/school-boards` — answers common objections in search snippets
- **`Organization` schema** on homepage — name, url, logo, sameAs (GitHub, social)
- **`BreadcrumbList`** on all inner pages
- **`Article` schema** on all blog posts with datePublished, author, keywords

## Competitive Notes

Sites currently ranking for these clusters:

| Competitor | Strengths | Gap We Can Exploit |
|---|---|---|
| MIT Media Lab | Domain authority, academic credibility | No practical teacher/homeschool UX; not designed for adoption |
| AI4K12 | Standards ownership | Not a curriculum — just a framework; no lesson materials |
| Code.org | Brand, volume, teacher community | AI is one small corner; not deep or project-based |
| Create & Learn | Homeschool + paid programs SEO | Paid platform; we're free with no account required |
| CSTA | Policy authority | No curriculum materials, just standards guidance |

**Our moat:** Free + open license + real project output + multi-audience landing pages + no account required. No competitor combines all four. Lean into it in every title and meta.
