## Before Expo Day

### 🔧 The Night Before Checklist

Complete all of these before you sleep:

**Your agent:**
- [ ] Agent is running and responding correctly
- [ ] You've tested the exact inputs you plan to use in your demo
- [ ] Demo inputs are written down (you won't have to remember on the spot)
- [ ] You know what your agent does when it gets a question it can't answer

**Your backup plan:**
- [ ] You have screenshots of your agent working, saved locally (not just in the cloud)
- [ ] You have a short video (~1-2 min) of your agent in action, downloaded to your device
- [ ] You know how to show the backup within 30 seconds if needed

**Your materials:**
- [ ] Capability Inventory Worksheet filled out and with you
- [ ] Demo Script Outline reviewed (you don't need it in front of you, just in your head)
- [ ] Agent Design Journal ready if your teacher wants it
- [ ] Optional: slide deck open and ready on a separate tab

**Logistics:**
- [ ] You know what time setup starts
- [ ] You know where your station is (if assigned in advance)
- [ ] You have a charger for your device
- [ ] You know the WiFi network name and password

## Expo Day: Setting Up Your Station

### 🕐 Arrive Early

Arrive at least **15-20 minutes before the Expo begins.** You need time to:
1. Find your station
2. Get your agent running
3. Do a quick test of your demo inputs
4. Breathe

### 📋 Station Setup Checklist

**Physical setup:**
- [ ] Device plugged in (or battery > 80%)
- [ ] Screen visible to people standing in front of you
- [ ] Brightness turned up (rooms can be bright)
- [ ] Notifications silenced / Do Not Disturb on
- [ ] Nothing embarrassing visible on screen or in other tabs

**Tech setup:**
- [ ] Agent is running and accessible
- [ ] All tabs/windows you need are open
- [ ] Backup screenshots are accessible (desktop or a specific folder)
- [ ] Backup video is downloaded and playable offline

**Your materials:**
- [ ] Demo inputs written on a notecard or sticky note in front of you
- [ ] Any props, printouts, or visuals set out
- [ ] Optional: sign or label with your agent's name (makes your station easier to find)

---

## What to Have Open and Ready

Before the first visitor arrives, you should have exactly these things ready:

### Tab / Window 1 — Your Agent (Main Demo)
Your agent interface, logged in, loaded, and ready to receive input. If your agent needs to "warm up" (e.g., first response is slow), do a test message now.

**What should be on screen:** A clean, empty input ready to go. Not a previous conversation.

### Tab / Window 2 — Your Backup (Fallback)
Screenshots or video of your agent working. This tab should be immediately accessible — don't bury it.

**What should be on screen:** Your most impressive capability screenshot, or the video paused at the beginning.

### Tab / Window 3 — Optional: Slide Deck
If you made a slide deck, have it open on Slide 1, ready to present.

**Keyboard shortcut to remember:** Know how to switch between windows/tabs instantly. Practice it.

## During the Expo

### 🔄 Station Format Options

Your teacher will tell you which format you're using:

**Sequential (one at a time):**  
You present to the full group when called. Stay on time. When done, return to your station for informal Q&A.

**Rotating stations (science fair style):**  
Visitors come to you. You give your demo repeatedly — sometimes to one person, sometimes to a group of five. Keep each run to ~5 minutes.

**Ask your teacher:** _Which format are we using?_ ___________________________

### 🔄 If You're on Rotating Stations: Pacing Tips

- Give a **full 5-minute demo** when a judge or teacher arrives
- Give a **2-3 minute highlight version** for casual visitors between formal evaluations
- It's okay to say "Want the full demo or the quick version?"
- Stay energized — repeat demos can feel tiring, but each visitor is seeing it fresh

### 🤝 Engaging Visitors

When someone walks up to your station:
1. Make eye contact, smile, say "Hi — want to see what I built?"
2. Jump into the hook — don't explain first, show first
3. After the hook, ask: "Want me to walk you through everything, or just show you a specific thing?"
4. Invite them to give you an input: "You can try it — what do you want to ask it?"

## Tech Backup Plan

### Scenario 1: WiFi goes down

> **Do:** Switch to your screenshot/video backup immediately. Say: *"WiFi is down so I'll show you a recording, but I can also explain how it works..."*  
> **Don't:** Spend 5 minutes trying to fix the WiFi while your visitor stands there.

### Scenario 2: Agent is down / not responding

> **Do:** Show backup materials. Explain verbally what the agent would do. Offer to follow up after the Expo.  
> **Don't:** Refresh the page 15 times while apologizing.

### Scenario 3: Agent gives a bad or weird response live

> **Do:** Stay calm. Say: *"That's actually a good example of where it doesn't always work right. Here's what it usually does..."* Then show backup or try once more.  
> **Don't:** Pretend it didn't happen. Lean into it — it's a learning moment.

### Scenario 4: You go blank on what to say

> **Do:** Look at your notecard with demo inputs. Say the first line of your hook. The rest will follow.  
> **Don't:** Stand silently for 10 seconds. Just start talking.

### Scenario 5: Visitor asks something you don't know

> **Do:** Say *"That's a great question — I'd have to look into that."* or *"I'm not sure yet, but my guess is..."*  
> **Don't:** Make something up or get flustered.
