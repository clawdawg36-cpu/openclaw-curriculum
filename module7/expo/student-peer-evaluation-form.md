## Notes on Giving Good Feedback

**Warm ≠ compliment.** A Warm is specific and true, not generic praise. "Great demo" is not Warm. "I loved that you showed what happened when the agent got it wrong — that was brave and it made the whole demo more believable" is Warm.

**Cool ≠ criticism.** A Cool is a genuine question or tension. It comes from curiosity, not from wanting to tear something down. "Cool: I wondered if the guardrails you built would actually catch misuse, or if someone clever could get around them" is a great Cool.

**Wonder ≠ another question.** A Wonder opens something up rather than asking for a specific answer. "Wonder: What would this agent become if you kept working on it for a year?" is a Wonder. "Why didn't you add X?" is just a question.
