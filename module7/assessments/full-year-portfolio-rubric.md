## Overview

The Full-Year Portfolio evaluates the student's **complete Agent Design Journal** — all 7 module journal entries plus the final capstone reflection written during Lesson 7.7. This rubric assesses the collection as a whole, not individual entries in isolation.

**What to collect:**
- Journal entries from Modules 1–7 (7 total)
- Final capstone reflection (Lesson 7.7 prompts)

---

## Scoring Criteria

Each criterion is scored 1–4. Criteria are weighted equally (25% each).

---

### 1. Completeness — 25%

*Are all required journal entries and the final reflection present?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4** | All 7 module journal entries are present AND the final capstone reflection is complete. Each entry is substantive (not just a sentence or two). |
| **3** | 6–7 entries present; capstone reflection present. Minor gaps in substance but overall complete. |
| **2** | 4–5 entries present, or several entries are too thin to demonstrate engagement. |
| **1** | Fewer than 4 entries present, or the portfolio is largely incomplete. |

### 2. Depth of Growth — 25%

*Does the portfolio show genuine intellectual development across the year?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4** | Clear arc of growth — early entries show beginner thinking; later entries show more sophisticated questions, richer understanding of how AI works, and evidence of learning from mistakes. The student is visibly a different thinker by Module 7. |
| **3** | Some evidence of growth — student shows development in most modules, though the arc isn't always explicit. Later entries are meaningfully richer than early ones. |
| **2** | Limited arc — entries feel similar throughout; little evidence that earlier learning is building toward later entries. |
| **1** | No visible growth — entries could have been written in any order; the portfolio doesn't tell a learning story. |

### 3. Honest Self-Assessment — 25%

*Does the student reflect honestly on what they found hard, what went wrong, and what they'd do differently?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4** | Multiple entries (not just the capstone) show genuine honesty about difficulty, confusion, failure, or design regret. Student names specific things they'd change and why. Capstone reflection is deeply honest and personal. |
| **3** | Honest moments appear in several entries; student acknowledges challenges without always going deep. Capstone reflection shows real self-awareness. |
| **2** | Honesty is present but mostly in the capstone; most module entries stay positive or vague about difficulty. |
| **1** | Portfolio reads as uniformly positive or performative; student doesn't meaningfully engage with struggle, failure, or what they'd do differently. |

**Score (circle):** &nbsp;&nbsp; 1 &nbsp;&nbsp; 2 &nbsp;&nbsp; 3 &nbsp;&nbsp; 4

_______________________________________________

### 4. Connection to the Big Picture — 25%

*Does the student connect individual module work to larger ideas about AI, ethics, and their own future?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **4** | Student regularly connects their specific agent work to bigger questions: What does it mean to build AI? What are the stakes? Who is affected? Capstone reflection articulates a personal vision for their future relationship with AI. |
| **3** | Student makes meaningful connections in several entries and especially in the capstone. Not every entry goes this deep, but the thread is there. |
| **2** | Some big-picture thinking, mostly in the capstone or in M6 (ethics module). Earlier entries tend to stay task-level. |
| **1** | Portfolio stays almost entirely at the task level — "I did this, it worked/didn't work" — without zooming out to larger meaning. |
