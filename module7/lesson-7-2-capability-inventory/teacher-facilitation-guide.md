# Teacher Facilitation Guide — Lesson 7.2: Know Your Agent — Capability Inventory

---

## Before Class

### Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Print or set up digital Capability Inventory Template for each student
- [ ] Prepare a completed example inventory for a fictional agent (for modeling)
- [ ] Check that all students have working devices and agent access
- [ ] Review which students have the most/least complete agents — know your outliers
- [ ] Have 3 colored highlighters or colored pens available per student (or colored text in digital doc)

### What to Expect
This session is mostly student work time. Your job is to circulate, probe, and redirect. The most common issues:
1. Students skipping capabilities they know are broken (avoidance)
2. Students claiming a capability is "Solid" without actually testing it
3. Students spending too long on M1–M2 and running out of time for M4–M6

Watch for these patterns and intervene early.

---

## Facilitation Notes

### Warm-Up (5 min)
The "gut answer" exercise is important because it creates a hypothesis to test. When students discover their inventory matches (or doesn't match) their gut answer, it creates a learning moment either way.

If a student says they're most proud of something that turns out to be broken, that's a valuable realization — handle it with care and curiosity, not judgment.

### Work Time Circulation Strategy
Move systematically through the room. Suggested focus per rotation:
- **First 10 min:** Check that everyone is actually testing (not just remembering or guessing). Push: "Show me that working right now."
- **Middle 10 min:** Check M3/M4 progress — these tend to be the most variable. Students with strong M3 domain knowledge often don't realize how impressive it is.
- **Last 10 min:** Push to M5/M6. Some students deprioritize the live channel and ethics sections — remind them these are Expo-ready capabilities.

### Difficult Conversations
**Student with mostly broken agent:**
Keep it private, matter-of-fact, and forward-looking. "Okay, so M4 and M5 aren't working reliably. You've got solid M1, M2, and M3. That's a real agent. Let's build your demo around those three." Don't dwell on what's missing.

**Student who rated everything "Solid" without testing:**
Ask them to test live in front of you for two capabilities. Usually one reveals something shakier than expected. Prompt: "What happens if I give it [unusual input]? Try it."

**Student who wants to fix everything before the demo:**
Gently redirect: "We have limited time. Let's identify your top 3 solid things and make those perfect, rather than trying to fix everything. What would you sacrifice to make your Hook amazing?"

## Common Questions from Students

| Question | Response |
|----------|----------|
| "Do I have to demo M5 if the channel is slow?" | "Shaky is fine if you acknowledge it. Don't demo it as Solid if it's Shaky." |
| "Can I demo something I built outside the modules?" | "Absolutely — add it to 'Other' in the inventory." |
| "What if I can't remember what I did in M4?" | "Open your journal — or just test it live. Let the agent tell you what it knows." |
| "My agent does the same thing different ways — is that one capability or two?" | "Your call. If you'd demo it differently, it's two. If it's the same input/output, it's one." |
| "What if my limitation is that the whole thing barely works?" | "That's honest, and honesty is respected. We'll work on framing it in 7.3." |
