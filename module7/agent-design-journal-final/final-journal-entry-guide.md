# Agent Design Journal — Module 7: Final Entry Guide

> **The most important entry you'll write.**
> You've kept this journal since Module 1. This is where you look back at everything — your thinking, your building, your struggles, and your growth. Take your time. Be honest. There are no wrong answers here.

---

## Before You Begin

Find your **Module 1 journal entry** and read it all the way through. Let it sit for a moment. Then open this guide and write.

Suggested time: **45–60 minutes**. Don't rush it.

---

## Prompt 1 — Then and Now

**Look back at your M1 entry: what did you think AI was then? How has that changed?**

In Module 1, you wrote about what you thought AI was. Pull out a specific thing you said — a belief, an assumption, maybe something that sounds a little embarrassing now.

Write about:
- What you thought AI was (quote yourself if you can)
- What you know now that you didn't then
- The single biggest thing that changed your thinking — was it a lesson, a moment of the build, a bug you couldn't fix, or something that actually worked?

> *"I used to think AI was ___. Now I think ___."*

Don't just say you "know more now." Get specific. What exactly changed?

---

## Prompt 2 — Your Agent's Full Capability Map

**List every capability your agent has. Which one are you most proud of, and why?**

Start by making the list. Every single thing your agent can do — big, small, obvious, surprising. Don't skip anything.

Then pick one. The one you're most proud of. This doesn't have to be the most impressive thing. It might be the hardest thing to build. It might be the smallest thing that took the longest. It might be the thing that feels most *you*.

Write about:
- The full list (use bullet points — be thorough)
- Which capability you chose and why
- What it took to build it — what you tried, what failed, what finally worked
- What it felt like when it worked

---

## Prompt 3 — The Hardest Moment

**What was the hardest moment in the build? What did you do?**

Every builder has one. The moment where something completely broke, or nothing made sense, or you wanted to quit. Maybe it was a bug you spent three days on. Maybe it was realizing your original idea wouldn't work. Maybe it was something personal — a hard week, a presentation that didn't land.

Write about:
- The specific moment (be as concrete as possible — what was happening, what went wrong)
- How you felt
- What you actually did — did you push through? Ask for help? Restart? Walk away and come back?
- What you learned from it

> *Note: "I just figured it out" is not a complete answer. What did figuring it out actually look like?*

---

## Prompt 4 — What Your Agent Still Gets Wrong

**What does your agent still get wrong? What would you fix first?**

Every agent has limits. Even the best ones. Acknowledging them isn't weakness — it's the mark of someone who actually understands what they built.

Write about:
- Two or three things your agent still gets wrong, misses, or fails at
- Why those failures happen (what's the root cause, as best you understand it?)
- If you had one more week to work on it, what would you fix first and why?

Be specific. "It's not perfect" doesn't count. Name the actual failure mode.

---

## Prompt 5 — Two Sentences

**In one sentence: what is your agent? In another: what does it mean that YOU built it?**

This is the hardest prompt. Take it seriously.

**Sentence 1:** Describe your agent in one sentence. Not a feature list. Not a sales pitch. One sentence that captures what it *is* — what it does, who it's for, why it exists.

**Sentence 2:** What does it mean that *you* built it? Not someone else. Not a team. You. What does this say about you, or what does it mean to you personally?

Write both sentences. Then write a paragraph explaining what went into each one — why you chose those words, what you almost wrote instead.

---

## Prompt 6 — What's Next

**What do you want to learn next?**

You're not done learning. You're done with this curriculum — but that's not the same thing.

Write about:
- One technical thing you want to learn next (be specific — not "more AI," but what specifically)
- One non-technical thing you want to get better at (communication, design, persistence, presenting — anything)
- Where you think AI is going, and where you want to be in that story

This is your last journal entry for this course. Make it count. Future-you will read it someday.

---

## Final Note — For the Journal as a Whole

You don't have to turn this in perfectly polished. But you do have to write it honestly.

Seven modules. Hundreds of hours. One agent.

Write like that matters — because it does.
