## Tips for Each Field

**Agent Name**
Use the name you gave your agent during the build. If it changed across modules, use the final name — and consider noting in your portfolio when/why it changed.

**Agent Avatar**
This can be:
- An image you generated during the curriculum (Module 2 or 3)
- A drawing, sketch, or doodle you made
- A logo or icon you created
- Anything that visually represents your agent

If you're submitting digitally, embed the image file. If printing, tape or glue it in.

**Student Name**
Your name. First and last.

**Year**
The school year (e.g., *2025–2026*) or just the graduation year if you prefer.

**Tagline**
This is the most important line on the page. One sentence that captures what your agent is, what it does, or what it means to you. Don't use:
- Generic phrases ("My agent is smart and helpful")
- Feature lists ("It can answer questions and do tasks")

Do try:
- Something specific to your agent's purpose
- Something with your voice in it
- Something that would make a stranger curious

> *Examples of strong taglines:*
> - *"Built to help my grandma navigate her day without needing to ask anyone for help."*
> - *"An agent that reads the news so my little brother can actually understand it."*
> - *"I made something that can do one thing really well — and that thing matters to me."*

## Digital Version Note

If submitting digitally (PDF, Google Doc, Notion page, etc.):
- Replace the ASCII box above with a clean formatted layout
- Use a large font for the agent name (recommend 36pt+)
- Embed the avatar as an actual image
- The tagline should be visually prominent — consider bold or italic
