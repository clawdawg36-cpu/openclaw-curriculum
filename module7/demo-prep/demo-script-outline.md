## Before You Start

**Target time:** 5 minutes total  
**Setup:** Agent open and ready, backup screenshots accessible, notes visible to you (not the audience)

## Section 1 — The Hook
### ⏱ Target: 30 seconds

> **Goal:** Surprise them before you introduce yourself. Start with something your agent *does*, not what it *is*.

**Your opening line (complete this):**

> "Watch this."  
> *(show the demo input)*  
> _______________________________________________________________

**After the reaction, introduce yourself:**

> "My name is _______________ and I built an AI agent called _______________.  
> Over the last [6 weeks / semester / school year], I built it from scratch, one capability at a time.  
> Here's what that looks like."

**Prompts:**
- What's the most surprising or satisfying thing your agent can do?
- Can you get a reaction from the audience in 10 seconds or less?
- Don't explain yet. Show first, then tell.

## Section 2 — The Build
### ⏱ Target: 2 minutes

> **Goal:** Tell the origin story of your agent. Walk through the modules briefly — what you added each time, and how they fit together.

**Suggested structure (adapt to what you actually built):**

> "I started in Module 1 by giving my agent a name and a personality. My agent is called _______________ and it's designed to _______________.

> In Module 2, I gave it the ability to [see / hear / both]. Now it can _______________.

> Module 3 was about knowledge. I taught it about _______________ — things a regular AI wouldn't know.

> [In Module 4, I trained it on data. Specifically, I _______________.] *(skip if not built)*

> In Module 5, I connected it to the real world. You can talk to it on _______________.

> And in Module 6, I gave it ethics. I wrote a usage policy and built guardrails so it won't _______________.

> Together, these six layers make up the agent you're about to see."

- Which module are you most proud of? Give it a few extra seconds.

## Section 3 — The Live Demo
### ⏱ Target: 2 minutes

> **Goal:** Show at least 3 capabilities, live. Narrate what's happening so the audience can follow.

**Demo narration structure (for each capability):**

> "One thing I want to show you is [CAPABILITY].  
> I'm going to [do X] and you'll see [what happens].  
> *(do the thing)*  
> See how it [observation]? That's because [brief explanation]."

**Capability 1:**

> I'll demonstrate: _______________________________________________  
> My exact input: _______________________________________________  
> Narration: _______________________________________________

**Capability 2:**

> I'll demonstrate: _______________________________________________  
> My exact input: _______________________________________________  
> Narration: _______________________________________________

**Capability 3:**

> I'll demonstrate: _______________________________________________  
> My exact input: _______________________________________________  
> Narration: _______________________________________________

**If something breaks mid-demo:**

> "This one's a little unpredictable. Let me show you what it looks like when it works..."  
> *(switch to backup screenshot or video)*

**Prompts:**
- Use real inputs — type something new, don't just show a pre-saved response.
- Talk through what you're doing while you do it.
- Slow down. The audience can't follow as fast as you think they can.
- Pick capabilities that *contrast* — something visual, something smart, something funny.

## Section 4 — The Reflection
### ⏱ Target: 30 seconds

> **Goal:** End with honesty and vision. Don't oversell. The most powerful finish is a real, specific reflection — not "it was great."

**Structure:**

> "I'm really proud of _______________.  
> But I want to be honest — my agent still gets _______________ wrong.  
> If I had more time, I would _______________.  
> This project taught me _______________, and I think it matters because _______________."

**Closing line (optional):**

> "I'm happy to take questions."  
> *(or)*  
> "Thanks for watching — feel free to try it yourself."

**Prompts:**
- What's one genuine failure or limitation you'd be comfortable sharing?
- What would you do differently if you started over?
- What did you learn that surprised you?
- Where do you want to take this next?
