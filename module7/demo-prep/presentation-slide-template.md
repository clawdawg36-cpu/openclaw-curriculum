## Slide 4 — Honest Limitations

> **What goes here:** What your agent gets wrong, can't do, or does inconsistently. This is where your credibility goes up — if you're honest about limits, the audience trusts everything else you say.

**Slide title:**

> "What It Doesn't Do (Yet)"  
> *(or)*  
> "Where It Falls Short — Honestly"

**Content:**

| It struggles with... | Why | What I'd fix |
|---------------------|-----|-------------|
| _____________________ | ___ | ___________ |
| _____________________ | ___ | ___________ |

**Speaker notes:**
> "I want to be real with you about what this agent gets wrong..."  
> _______________________________________________________________

**Design tips:**
- Don't skip this slide. Hiding limitations makes the whole demo feel less trustworthy.
- 2-3 honest limitations is ideal. Not 10 (too defensive), not 0 (not believable).
- Frame it as "here's what I'd work on next" — growth mindset, not shame.
