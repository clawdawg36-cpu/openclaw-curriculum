# Workshop 7.4 — Rehearsal Day

**Module:** 7 — Capstone: AI Expo  
**Grade Level:** 6–10 (adaptable)  
**Duration:** 50–60 minutes  
**Session Type:** Active Rehearsal Workshop  
**Standards Alignment:** AI4K12 Five Big Ideas; CSTA K-12 CS Standards; ELA Speaking & Listening Standards

---

## Overview

Rehearsal Day is the most active session in Module 7. Students deliver timed 5-minute run-throughs in pairs, receive structured feedback on clarity, pacing, and presence, and then practice adversarial Q&A — where partners try to ask the toughest possible questions. The goal is to make the Expo itself feel easy by comparison. Everyone rehearses at least twice.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, students will be able to:

1. Deliver a complete 5-minute demo run-through (timed) with their approved script.
2. Give structured feedback on clarity, pacing, and handling of unexpected moments.
3. Answer at least 5 adversarial Q&A questions from a partner — with honest, confident responses.
4. Identify and commit to 2–3 specific improvements for the Technical Dry Run (7.5).

| Item | Notes |

## Lesson Sequence

### Launch: Why Rehearsal Works (5 min)

**Teacher framing — keep it brief:**

"The only way to know if your demo works is to do it. Not think about doing it. Not re-read your script. Do it. In front of a real person. With a timer running."

"Today you'll do this at least twice. The first run-through will be rough. That's why we're doing it today and not at the Expo."

"Ground rules:
1. If you finish in 3 minutes, something is missing — you don't know your material well enough yet.
2. If you go over 6 minutes, something needs to be cut.
3. If you stop in the middle — keep going. Real demos don't get pauses."

**Pair assignment:** Assign pairs now (or have students self-select, then confirm with teacher). Post pairs visually.

---

### Round 1: First Full Run-Through (15 min)

**Format:** Pairs work simultaneously across the room

**Process per pair:**
1. Partner A demos (5 min timed). Partner B listens, takes notes on Rehearsal Feedback Form.
2. Partner B gives verbal feedback (2 min). Uses feedback form as guide.
3. Switch: Partner B demos (5 min timed). Partner A listens + takes notes.
4. Partner A gives verbal feedback (2 min).

*Note: 15 min is tight for two run-throughs + feedback. Teacher should announce "Switch" at the 7-min mark if pairs haven't self-managed.*

**Teacher role during Round 1:** Float and observe. Don't interrupt demos in progress. Note which students are:
- Significantly over/under time
- Reading directly from script (no eye contact)
- Skipping sections
- Demo tech issues

Save observations for class debrief.

---

### Class Debrief — Round 1 Patterns (5 min)

Quick class share without calling out individuals:

- "How many of you were under 4 minutes? That means something is missing — what?"
- "How many went over 6? What could you cut?"
- "How many read directly from the script the whole time? That's okay for today — what do you want to do differently for the Expo?"
- "Did anything break or not work as expected? What did you do?"

**Key teaching points to reinforce:**
- *Pacing:* Speed up when describing technical context; slow down at demo moments. Audiences need time to see what's happening.
- *Eye contact:* The script is a safety net, not a teleprompter. Try to look up when narrating demo moments.
- *When things break:* "Narrate it. Say what it usually does. Then move on. Don't freeze."

---

### Round 2: Second Run-Through with Focus Area (12 min)

**Before Round 2:** Each student identifies ONE focus area from their Round 1 feedback:
- Pacing (going too fast / too slow)
- Hook (did it grab attention?)
- Demo narration (did the audience understand what they were seeing?)
- Reflect (did it feel honest and confident, not apologetic?)
- Transitions (smooth between sections?)

Student writes their focus area at the top of their script. They'll tell their partner before the run-through.

**Process:** Same as Round 1 — both partners deliver, both give feedback. Feedback this round should focus specifically on the stated focus area.

*Time note: Round 2 is slightly shorter on feedback (1 min verbal each) to preserve time for Q&A practice.*

---

### Adversarial Q&A Practice (10 min)

**Format:** Partners take turns asking hard questions. This simulates the 3-min Q&A at the Expo.

**How it works:**
1. Partner A just finished their demo (Round 2). Partner B now asks 3 adversarial questions.
2. Partner A answers honestly and fully (60–90 seconds per answer max).
3. Switch.

**Using the Adversarial Q&A Question Bank:**

Partners draw from this bank OR make up their own harder questions. The bank includes:

**Technical questions:**
- "What happens when your agent gets a question outside its domain?"
- "How does your agent handle inputs it's never seen before?"
- "What data did you use to train it? How do you know that data is accurate?"
- "Can your agent make things up? How would someone know?"
- "What would happen if your agent was used by 1,000 people at once?"

**Ethical questions:**
- "Could your agent be used to harm someone? How?"
- "What's the most dangerous thing your agent could do wrong?"
- "Does your agent have any biases? How do you know?"
- "Who's responsible if your agent gives someone bad advice?"
- "Should your agent be allowed to talk to kids without adult supervision?"

**Design questions:**
- "Why did you design the personality the way you did? What alternatives did you consider?"
- "What's the worst thing that happened during the build? How did you handle it?"
- "If you could start over with what you know now, what would you do differently?"
- "What's the most important thing you learned in Module [2/3/4] and why?"

**Meta questions (the really hard ones):**
- "Is your agent actually useful, or is it just a school project?"
- "How is your agent better than just using ChatGPT?"
- "What makes your agent yours — what makes it different from what anyone else in this class built?"

**Teacher coaching during Q&A:** Circulate and listen. Coach presenters on two things:
1. *Don't dodge.* "I don't know" is better than a non-answer. "I haven't thought about that yet, but I think..." is also fine.
2. *Don't ramble.* Answer in 60 seconds, not 5 minutes.

---

### Reflection + Commit (5 min)

**Individual written reflection:**

Students complete the "What I'm changing" section on their Rehearsal Feedback Form:

> *Before the Technical Dry Run (7.5), I will make these 2–3 specific changes:*
> 1. _______________________________________________
> 2. _______________________________________________
> 3. _______________________________________________

**Quick class share:** 2–3 volunteers share their biggest takeaway from today.

**Preview 7.5:** "Next session is Technical Dry Run. Your job: run the full demo on the Expo setup — not your personal computer setup. We find and fix any tech issues before the day. Bring everything: agent, slides, backup screenshots."

## Differentiation

### For students who need support:
- Allow one full read-through from script in Round 1 — challenge them in Round 2 to look up for the Hook and the closing line
- Pre-load 3 adversarial questions for their partner (from the bank) so their partner doesn't have to generate them
- Pair with a student who is encouraging and specific in their feedback style

### For advanced students:
- Challenge: deliver the demo without looking at the script at all in Round 2
- Add a "3-minute version" constraint: "Can you cut your demo to 3 minutes without losing the essential story?"
- Ask: "What's the single most uncomfortable question you could be asked? Practice answering it."

### For students with presentation anxiety:
- Allow them to do Round 1 with teacher as the audience instead of a peer (quieter, lower stakes)
- Reduce adversarial Q&A to 2 questions instead of 3 in Round 1; 3 in Round 2
- Normalize explicitly: "Nervousness is normal and often invisible to the audience. Your audience is rooting for you."

---

## Assessment

**Formative:** Completed Rehearsal Feedback Form (both as presenter and reviewer).

**Self-reflection:** "What I'm changing" commitments written at end of session.

**Teacher observation** during run-throughs — note students who need additional support before 7.5.

---

## Teacher Notes

- The adversarial Q&A is often students' favorite part of the module. Let it run a little long if energy is high — the prep it provides is invaluable.
- Students who freeze during run-throughs need low-stakes encouragement, not more instruction. Let them start again from the top.
- Monitor pairs where one student is significantly stronger than the other — the stronger student may dominate feedback. Prompt: "What's *their* specific focus area? Give feedback on that."
- If a student's tech isn't working during rehearsal, this is the first sign that 7.5 Tech Dry Run needs extra attention. Flag it.
- Some students will want to rewrite their script after Round 1. Encourage small changes (word-level, not structural) unless something is genuinely broken. Big rewrites at this stage usually make things worse.
