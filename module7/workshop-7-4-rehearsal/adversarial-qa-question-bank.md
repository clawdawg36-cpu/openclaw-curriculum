# Adversarial Q&A Question Bank
## Workshop 7.4 — Rehearsal Day

These questions are designed to prepare students for the toughest audience questions at the AI Expo. Partners use these during the Q&A practice segment. The goal is to ask questions the student hasn't prepared a canned answer for — to develop honest, in-the-moment responses.

---

## How to Use This Bank

1. Pick 3 questions from this list (mix categories)
2. Or make up harder ones specific to your partner's agent
3. Ask one at a time — let your partner fully answer before asking the next
4. After all 3 questions, give one piece of coaching: "That answer was strong because..." or "That answer could be stronger if..."

---

## Technical Questions

- "What happens when your agent gets a question it doesn't know the answer to?"
- "How does your agent handle inputs it's never seen before?"
- "What data did you use to train it? How do you know that data is accurate or fair?"
- "Can your agent make things up? How would someone know if it did?"
- "What would happen if 1,000 people used your agent at once?"
- "What's the slowest part of your agent? Why?"
- "What happens if someone gives it a deliberately confusing or trick input?"
- "How much does it cost to run your agent? Is it free? Who pays?"
- "If the internet went out, would any part of your agent still work?"
- "How is your agent stored? Who has access to conversations with it?"

---

## Ethical Questions

- "Could your agent be used to harm someone? How?"
- "What's the most dangerous thing your agent could do wrong?"
- "Does your agent have any biases? How do you know?"
- "Who's responsible if your agent gives someone bad advice and they follow it?"
- "Should your agent be allowed to talk to young kids without adult supervision?"
- "What happens if someone tries to jailbreak it — get it to say things it's not supposed to?"
- "Does your agent collect any personal information about users? What do you do with it?"
- "Could your agent be used to spread misinformation? What would stop that?"
- "What would you do if you found out your agent was being used for something you didn't intend?"

---

## Design Questions

- "Why did you design the personality the way you did? What other options did you consider?"
- "What's the worst thing that happened during the build? How did you handle it?"
- "If you could start over with everything you know now, what would you do differently?"
- "What's the most important thing you learned in Module [2/3/4] and why?"
- "Why did you choose that specific domain for Module 3? Why not something else?"
- "How did you decide what guardrails to put on your agent in Module 6?"
- "What's a feature you wanted to add but couldn't? Why not?"
- "Is there anything about your agent you're not proud of? What is it?"

---

## Meta Questions (The Really Hard Ones)

- "Is your agent actually useful to real people, or is it just a school project?"
- "How is your agent better than just using ChatGPT or Google?"
- "What makes your agent *yours* — what makes it genuinely different from what anyone else built?"
- "Would you trust your own agent? Why or why not?"
- "If a stranger used your agent for 10 minutes, what would they learn about you as a designer?"
- "What does your agent say about the kind of person you are?"
- "What would you need to build to make this a real product? What's missing?"
- "Would you feel comfortable if your parents, your principal, or a journalist could see every conversation your agent has ever had?"

---

## Tips for Answering Hard Questions

You don't have to know everything. But you have to engage honestly.

**Phrases that work:**
- "That's something I thought about. Here's my honest take..."
- "I don't have a perfect answer to that yet. But if I had to guess..."
- "My agent doesn't handle that well. Here's what it does instead..."
- "I deliberately made that tradeoff because..."
- "I hadn't thought of that angle — that's a good question."

**Phrases to avoid:**
- "I don't know." (Unless followed by something — "but here's what I think...")
- "That won't happen." (Overconfident; audiences notice)
- "My agent is perfect." (Nobody believes it)
- Silence for more than 5 seconds without acknowledging the question

**The secret:** Audiences trust presenters who say "here's a limitation I know about" more than those who claim perfection. Honesty is your superpower.
