# Student Presentation Schedule Template

**Module 7 | Teacher Resource**

*The AI Expo supports two main formats: open gallery (science-fair style, simultaneous) and sequential presentations. This template covers both. Most teachers prefer the open gallery — it's louder, busier, and more exciting.*

---

## Format Options

### Option A: Open Gallery (Recommended)
All students present simultaneously at their stations. Guests rotate freely. No fixed time per student — guests visit as many stations as they want.

**Pros:** More social energy, students present multiple times to different guests, no waiting around, scales to any class size
**Cons:** Harder to ensure judges see all students, some stations may get more traffic than others

### Option B: Sequential Spotlight (Alternative)
Students present one at a time while everyone (class + guests) watches. Better for formal judging.

**Pros:** Everyone hears every presentation, judges can compare in real time
**Cons:** Long if class is large (25 students × 8 minutes = ~3.5 hours), quieter energy, more performance pressure

### Option C: Hybrid (Best of Both)
Open gallery for most of the event. Then: judges select 3–5 spotlight presentations for a brief "best of" segment while gallery is paused.

**This is the recommended format for most classrooms.**

### PHASE 4: JUDGING DELIBERATION (10 minutes)

*Judges move to a separate space (corner of room or hallway) to compare scores and finalize winners. Teacher manages room while this happens.*

- [ ] Students return to stations or move to seats
- [ ] Students may do quiet finalization of their Agent Design Journal reflection while waiting
- [ ] Teacher circulates, chats with guests, answers questions
- [ ] Tabulate People's Choice ballots (teacher or designated student counter)

---

### PHASE 5: AWARDS CEREMONY (10–15 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| [T+75] | All students gather (seats or standing at stations) |
| [T+76] | Teacher brief remarks: "What I'm proud of, what I observed" |
| [T+79] | Judge(s) brief remarks (optional, 1–2 min max) |
| [T+81] | Award announcements (see awards list in `judging-panel-guide.md`) |
| [T+88] | People's Choice Award announced (last for drama!) |
| [T+90] | Certificate distribution |
| [T+93] | Group photo |
| [T+95] | Guests dismissed, class debrief begins |

**Award announcement script template:**
> "Our [Award Name] goes to... [pause for drama] ... [Student Name], for [brief reason — one sentence]. [Student Name], come on up!"

### PHASE 6: WRAP-UP AND CLOSE (5 minutes)

- [ ] Students break down stations and collect materials
- [ ] Group photo if not done yet
- [ ] Dismissal

| *Break* | | | 5 min | |
| 6 | [time] | | 5 min demo + 3 min Q&A | |
| ... | | | | |

*Note: With 8 minutes per student plus transition time, 25 students = ~220 minutes. Plan across 2 class periods.*

**Time management tips for sequential:**
- Use a visible timer on the projector screen
- Designate a "timekeeper" student
- Give 1-minute and 30-second warnings
- Be firm but kind: "We have to move on — great work!"

## Day-Of Communication Templates

**"10 minutes left" announcement (to room):**
> "Attention everyone — we have about 10 minutes left in the open gallery. If there's a station you haven't visited yet, now's the time!"

**Transition to awards:**
> "Let's bring everyone together! Students, please wrap up your current demo and come stand near your station. We're getting ready for our awards ceremony!"
