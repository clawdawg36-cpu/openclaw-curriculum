# Celebration Planning: Certificates, Awards, and Class Wrap-Up

**Module 7 | Teacher Resource**

*The celebration is the emotional peak of the Expo. Get it right and students carry it with them. This guide covers certificate templates, the awards ceremony, and how to close the course with intention.*

---

## Part 1: Certificates

Every student gets a certificate — not just winners. The Expo is a capstone event, and crossing that finish line deserves formal recognition regardless of scores.

### Certificate Types

| Certificate | Who Gets It |
|-------------|-------------|
| **AI Expo Participant** | Every student — completing the Expo is an achievement |
| **AI Expo Award** | Winners of specific categories (printed on same template, different award line) |
| **[Award Name] Award** | Named award certificate with specific wording |

### Certificate Personalization (Recommended)

Generic certificates are fine. Personalized ones are memorable. Consider adding one handwritten line per certificate:

- "Your ethics statement was the most thoughtful I've seen."
- "The way you explained your agent to Mr. [admin] was impressive — he asked me about it afterward."
- "You fixed that bug 20 minutes before the Expo and still delivered a great demo. That's resilience."

This takes 15–20 minutes for a class of 30. Worth it.

### Printing Tips

- Print on cardstock (65–80 lb) if possible — it feels more substantial than regular paper
- Standard certificates: 8.5" × 11" or 5.5" × 8.5" (half-sheet, landscape)
- Use a border template (Microsoft Word, Google Docs, or Canva have free options)
- Sign certificates by hand, not digitally — it takes longer but students notice

## Part 2: Awards Ceremony Planning

### Ceremony Structure (10–15 minutes)

**Step 1: Gather everyone**
"Let's bring it in! Students, close your demos and come join us — awards time!"

**Step 2: Teacher reflection (2 minutes)**
This is your moment to speak to what you observed — not as grader, but as witness. Choose 2–3 things that genuinely moved or impressed you.

*Example:*
> "Before we get to the awards, I want to say something. Three months ago, none of you could have explained what a large language model is. Today, I watched you explain your AI agents to parents, administrators, and people from the community — and you did it like you'd been doing it for years. I'm proud of you."

**Step 3: Judge remarks (optional, 1–2 minutes)**
Invite a judge to say a few words. Brief them in advance: "Keep it to 60–90 seconds — something that felt genuine to you today."

**Step 4: Award announcements**

Order matters. Build to the most exciting awards:

1. Category awards (Best Demo, Most Creative, Best Q&A, Best Ethics, Most Growth) — 3–5 awards
2. Best Overall — penultimate, for dramatic effect
3. People's Choice — always last. The biggest applause moment.

For each award:
- Name the award category
- Build brief suspense ("Our judges were torn on this one...")
- Announce the winner
- Student comes up, shakes teacher's hand or gets their certificate
- Brief round of applause before moving on

**Step 5: Everyone gets their certificate**
"Now, every single person in this room is getting a certificate — because you all built something real. When I call your name, come get yours."

*If class is large: hand out by station number or row to avoid a chaotic scramble.*

**Step 6: Class photo**
"Before we wrap up, I need everyone together. [Student name], can you hold up your People's Choice Award? Everyone in frame — smile!"

## Part 3: Class Wrap-Up

*The closing of the Expo should feel like the end of a journey — because it is.*

### Closing Remarks (5 minutes)

After the photo, while students are still together:

**Acknowledge the arc:**
> "Seven modules ago, we asked: what even IS an AI agent? Today you answered that question by building one. That's not a small thing."

**Name what they can do now:**
> "You know how to give an AI agent a personality. How to teach it to perceive images and audio. How to connect it to real knowledge. How to deploy it to a real channel. And how to think carefully about whether it should. You're not just users of AI anymore — you're builders."

**Leave them with something to think about:**
> "The question I want you to sit with: what do you want to build next?"

### Celebration Options

| Idea | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Snacks/treat | Coordinate with school nutrition policy |
| Play music during station breakdown | Have students suggest a playlist beforehand |
| Award a class "superlative" voted by students | e.g., "Most Likely to Work at an AI Company" |
| Share one highlight from the day on a class display | Photo or quote on classroom door |
| Give students time to sign each other's Agent Design Journals | Like a yearbook — lasting artifact |

## Part 4: After the Expo (Next Class Period)

The Expo itself is a celebration. The reflection is where the learning solidifies. Plan one more class period for:

- Final Agent Design Journal entry (written reflection — see `reflection-career-pathways.md`)
- Class debrief: "What surprised you today?"
- Preview: "Where does this go next? What would Module 8 be?"
- Collect journals as final summative artifacts
- Return certificates and award certificates (if you kept them for signing/laminating)

## Supplies Checklist

**For certificates:**
- [ ] Cardstock paper (1.5 sheets per student — 1 completion + 0.5 for awards)
- [ ] Color printer (black and white works too)
- [ ] Pens for signing (black or blue ink, medium point)
- [ ] Envelopes (optional — nice touch for mailing to families)

**For awards ceremony:**
- [ ] Trophies, medals, or ribbons (optional — even dollar-store trophies are beloved)
- [ ] A "podium" moment: where you stand to announce, clear sight lines from all students
- [ ] A way to display People's Choice ballot results (write on whiteboard as you tally)

**For celebration:**
- [ ] Snacks (check allergies!)
- [ ] Camera for group photo
- [ ] Music playlist ready to go
- [ ] Class sign-in sheet for journal signatures (optional)
