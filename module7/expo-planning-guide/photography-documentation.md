# Photography and Documentation Guide

**Module 7 | Teacher Resource**

*The AI Expo is a milestone worth documenting — for students' portfolios, school marketing, next year's cohort, and your own professional reflection. This guide covers consent, shot lists, and creative documentation ideas.*

---

## Step 1: Confirm Consent Before You Shoot

**Before any photography happens:**

- [ ] Check your school's photo/video consent policy
- [ ] Verify which students have active media release forms on file
- [ ] If a student does NOT have consent on file: do not photograph them individually
- [ ] If publishing photos (school website, newsletter, social media): confirm you have explicit consent for public use, not just internal use

**Classroom-internal sharing (projected during debrief, printed for portfolios):** Usually covered by standard consent. Verify with your admin.

**Public sharing (school website, Twitter/X, news, community newsletter):** Requires explicit written consent from parent/guardian.

> 💡 **Tip:** Send a one-question consent form with your Expo invitation: *"May we photograph your student for school publications?"* Collect responses with RSVPs.

---

## Who Takes the Photos?

**Option A: Teacher takes photos** — Easiest to control, but you'll be pulled away from managing the event.

**Option B: Designate a student photographer** — Give a reliable student a camera or iPad with the assignment: "Your job is to document the Expo. You are not presenting today — you're the photographer." Great role for a student who needs an alternative assessment format.

**Option C: Parent volunteer photographer** — If a parent RSVPs with photography/video background, ask them! Brief them in advance.

**Option D: School yearbook/journalism student** — Reach out to the journalism or yearbook teacher. Expo coverage is excellent content for them.

## Shot List

### Must-Have Shots

- [ ] **Room overview / establishing shot** — Take this before guests arrive or just as the gallery opens. Capture the full layout.
- [ ] **Student at station, ready** — Each student at their station before the gallery opens. Great for portfolios even if no guests are in the frame.
- [ ] **Student demoing to a guest** — The core image: student showing their agent, guest watching. Aim for engagement — both looking at the screen, student gesturing.
- [ ] **Guest reaction** — A visitor's face as they see something cool. These are often the best photos.
- [ ] **Awards ceremony** — Student receiving their certificate. Capture the moment and the student's expression.
- [ ] **Full class group photo** — End of event. Everyone together.

### Nice-to-Have Shots

- [ ] Admin or judge speaking with a student
- [ ] Parent and student at a station together
- [ ] Closeup of a screen showing an agent in action (may need student permission for agent content)
- [ ] Student vote: hand dropping ballot in box
- [ ] Detail shots: station signs, name cards, ballot box
- [ ] Candid moments: students laughing, high-fiving, helping each other

### Video Captures

- [ ] **30-second demo clip per student** — Student narrates what their agent does while doing it. Excellent portfolio artifact. Ask each student to record their own demo during setup or rehearsal.
- [ ] **Awards moment** — Record the announcement and reaction for each award.
- [ ] **Brief student interviews (optional)** — 20–30 seconds: "What's your agent called and what does it do?" — Great for a class highlight reel.

## Capture Checklist (Day Of)

**Before gallery opens:**
- [ ] Room overview photo (empty or with students setting up)
- [ ] Photo of welcome table and ballot box
- [ ] "Ready" photo at each station (or a sample of stations)

**During gallery:**
- [ ] At least 1 photo of each student presenting (prioritize students without many family photos coming)
- [ ] 3–5 candid interaction shots
- [ ] Guest reaction photos

**During awards:**
- [ ] Each award announcement moment
- [ ] Each certificate handoff
- [ ] People's Choice reveal

**After awards:**
- [ ] Full class group photo (everyone in)
- [ ] Small group "best of" photo (award winners)
- [ ] Teacher with class photo (optional but nice)

## Equipment Suggestions

| Equipment | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| Smartphone camera | Excellent quality; use portrait mode for station shots |
| iPad | Good for video; awkward for candid stills |
| School camera (if available) | Best image quality; check out in advance |
| Tripod or stand | Useful for video of sequential presentations |
| External light / ring light | If room is dark or has harsh overhead lighting |

**Settings tips:**
- Turn off flash in most cases — overhead fluorescent lighting is usually sufficient and flash creates harsh shadows
- Use burst mode for awards moments (catch the expression at the exact right frame)
- Portrait mode / shallow depth of field looks great for student-at-station shots

## Post-Event Documentation

### Student Portfolio Artifacts
After the Expo, each student should have:
- [ ] At least 1 photo of themselves at their station
- [ ] Their certificate (physical or scanned PDF)
- [ ] A screenshot or screen recording of their agent demo
- [ ] Their completed reflection page from the Agent Design Journal

**Delivery options:** Upload to student Google Drive folders, share via SeeSaw, or print and distribute.

### Class Documentation
- [ ] Curated photo album (Google Photos, shared folder, or printed booklet)
- [ ] Highlight video (1–3 minutes) if video was captured
- [ ] Written summary for school newsletter or website (get approval and consent first)
- [ ] Save 2–3 exemplar projects (with student permission) as examples for next year's cohort

### For Your Own Practice
- [ ] Document what worked and what didn't in your planning notes
- [ ] Note which schedule elements to adjust next year
- [ ] Save your scoring sheets (anonymized) as calibration anchors for next year's judges
- [ ] Consider writing a brief reflection: "What did this Expo reveal about my students that I didn't see in their classwork?"

---

## Privacy and Safe Sharing

**Before posting any photo publicly:**
- Confirm media consent is on file for each student in the photo
- If in doubt, crop or blur faces of non-consented students
- For school website: submit through your school's communications approval process
- Never post students' full names alongside photos on public platforms without explicit consent

**For AI-generated or agent-output screenshots:** Student agents may display text, images, or responses. Before sharing screenshots publicly, review the agent outputs for anything that shouldn't be made public (personal info, inappropriate outputs, etc.).
