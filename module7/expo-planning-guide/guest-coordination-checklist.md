# Guest Coordination Checklist

**Module 7 | Teacher Resource**

*This checklist covers every guest category: school administrators, parents/guardians, other classes, and community members. Use as your master coordination document.*

---

## Guest Categories

| Category | Lead Time | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Administrators (principal, VP) | 3–4 weeks | Often need calendar hold early |
| Parents/Guardians | 2–3 weeks | Use school communication platform |
| Other classes (grade-level or cross-grade) | 1–2 weeks | Coordinate with colleague |
| Community guests (professionals, alumni) | 3–4 weeks | May need background check |
| School board / district reps | 4–6 weeks | High lead time, high impact |

---

## Section 1: Administrators

### 4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Send a brief email/memo to principal and VP:
  - Date, time, location of AI Expo
  - What the Expo is (1–2 sentence description)
  - Invitation to stop by — even 10–15 minutes is welcome
  - Offer to send a longer brief if they want context

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Send reminder with final logistics
- [ ] Let them know what time awards happen (if they want to be present for that moment specifically)
- [ ] Provide talking points if principal may address students (optional — most will improvise)

### Day Of
- [ ] Reserve 1–2 chairs near the awards area for VIPs
- [ ] Greet admin at the door; brief 30-second orientation ("stations are numbered, feel free to explore — awards at 2:30")
- [ ] If principal visits: introduce them briefly to a student before they leave

**Sample email to admin:**

> Subject: AI Expo — [Date] — You're Invited!
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> My [class/period/grade] is holding our AI Expo on [Date] from [Time] in [Location]. It's a science-fair-style showcase where students present the AI agents they've built over the past [N weeks] in our AI curriculum.
>
> I'd love for you to stop by — even 10 minutes means a lot to the students. Awards happen at approximately [time] if you'd like to be there for that.
>
> Let me know if you have questions!
> [Your name]

## Section 2: Parents and Guardians

### 3 Weeks Before
- [ ] Post announcement in school communication platform (SeeSaw, ClassDojo, Remind, ParentSquare, etc.)
- [ ] Include:
  - Date, time, location
  - What the AI Expo is (1 paragraph, jargon-free)
  - RSVP request (helps you plan space)
  - Photo/video policy note

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Send reminder
- [ ] Include parking/entry details if Expo is after school hours
- [ ] Share schedule so parents know when their child presents (if sequential format)

### Day Of
- [ ] Welcome table at room entry with sign-in sheet
- [ ] Printable schedule available for parents to track timing
- [ ] Brief orientation when parent arrives (2–3 sentences: "Start anywhere, demos are ongoing, awards at [time]")

## Section 3: Other Classes

*Inviting another class to attend the Expo adds real audience pressure — in the best way. Even a 20-minute visit from a younger class turns the Expo into a genuine public event.*

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Email a colleague: would their class like to attend for ~20 minutes?
- [ ] Suggest what age groups work well:
  - Same grade: peer experience, relatable
  - Younger (one grade below): students become "experts" and enjoy teaching down
  - Older: aspirational — younger students see what they might build someday

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Confirm date, time window, and number of students
- [ ] Brief the visiting teacher: "Students rotate freely, demos are ongoing — you don't need to do anything except circulate with your class"
- [ ] Add visiting class to your schedule (suggest during open gallery, not awards)

### Day Of
- [ ] Welcome visiting teacher and class at door
- [ ] Quick orientation: "Each station has a student who built an AI agent — ask them to show you what it does"
- [ ] Visiting students get ballots too (or not — your call)
- [ ] Signal visiting teacher when their time is up

**Coordination template to send colleague:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm running an AI Expo on [Date] during [Period/Time]. My students have built AI agents this semester and will be presenting them science-fair style. Would you and your class like to come by for about 20 minutes during the open gallery portion?
>
> No prep needed on your end — just show up and let your students explore. It's a great real-world audience experience for my students, and your class gets to see some cool AI demos!
>
> We'd have you from [time] to [time]. Does that work?

## Section 4: Community Guests (Optional)

*Community professionals make excellent judges or gallery visitors. They bring a "real world" lens that validates student work in a way teachers and parents alone can't.*

**Good community guest profiles:**
- Software developers, data scientists, UX designers
- Local entrepreneurs or startup founders
- AI researchers (university connections)
- High school students from a CS or robotics program
- Public library staff (often have tech programs)
- Local journalists covering tech (especially good for documentation)

### 4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Identify 1–3 potential community guests
- [ ] Send a brief invitation (email or LinkedIn message):
  - What the Expo is
  - What role you're asking them to play (gallery visitor vs. judge)
  - Time commitment (1–2 hours)
  - Date, time, location

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirm RSVPs
- [ ] If serving as judge, send judging materials in advance (see `judging-panel-guide.md`)
- [ ] Confirm any background check requirements with your school administration

### Day Of
- [ ] Meet community guest at school office or entry point (many schools require escort)
- [ ] Give a 5-minute orientation before doors open
- [ ] Seat them at a judge table or orient them to gallery flow
- [ ] Introduce them in welcome remarks (builds student excitement)
- [ ] Thank them publicly at awards ceremony

**Sample community invitation:**

> Subject: Could you be a judge at our middle school AI Expo?
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I teach [subject] at [School], and my students are wrapping up a semester-long AI-building course. On [Date], we're hosting an AI Expo — a science-fair-style showcase where students present the AI agents they've built.
>
> I'm looking for 2–3 community judges who can evaluate student presentations and provide real-world feedback. Given your background in [field], I think you'd be a fantastic judge.
>
> The time commitment is about 2 hours on [Date, Time]. We'd cover parking/entry logistics.
>
> Interested? Let me know and I'll send the judging rubric and more details.

## Day-Of Welcome Desk Setup

**At the door, have:**
- [ ] Sign-in sheet (name, relation to student or organization)
- [ ] Printed schedule with station map
- [ ] People's Choice ballots + pens
- [ ] Explanation card: "This is the AI Expo. Each numbered station has a student presenting their AI agent. Feel free to visit any station, ask questions, and try the agent if the student offers! Awards begin at [time]."
- [ ] Nametag stickers for guests (optional but nice — helps students address guests by name)
