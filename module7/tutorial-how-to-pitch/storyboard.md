# Storyboard: How to Pitch Your AI Project
**Module 7 — Online Tutorial**
**Format:** ~10 scenes across ~5 minutes
**Layout:** Each scene includes timestamp, narration excerpt, visual description, on-screen text/callouts, and production cues.

---

## Scene 01 — [00:00–00:15] Opening Hook: The Stakes

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Imagine this: you've spent weeks building something. An AI agent with a name, a personality, real capabilities."

**Visual description:**
Student (gender-neutral, middle-school age) at a laptop. Behind them, an out-of-focus audience — rows of seats, warm light. The student looks calm but focused. Laptop screen glows. No text on screen yet — just the scene.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Fade in after 8 seconds: "How to Pitch Your AI Project"
- Subtitle fades in: "Module 7 — AI Expo Tutorial"

**Production cues:**
- Illustration style: flat design, muted warm palette — matches Module 6 visual language
- No dialogue yet — allow the image to establish stakes before narrator begins
- Soft ambient sound: quiet room, slight audience murmur in the background

**Transition:** Narrator begins; title card fades to presentation frame

---

## Scene 02 — [00:15–01:00] The Structure — Hook, Build, Demo, Reflect

**Narration excerpt:**
> "A great AI demo has four parts. We call them: Hook, Build, Demo, and Reflect."

**Visual description:**
Animated horizontal timeline bar fills in from left to right as narrator names each part:
1. **Hook** — orange segment — 0:00–0:30
2. **Build** — blue segment — 0:30–2:30
3. **Demo** — green segment — 2:30–4:30
4. **Reflect** — purple segment — 4:30–5:00

Each segment is labeled with:
- Name (Hook / Build / Demo / Reflect)
- Time range
- One-line description

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Timeline labels animate in one at a time as narrator says each word
- Below the bar: "Total time: 5 minutes"
- Callout: "This is your structure. Don't improvise your way through it."

**Production cues:**
- Timeline bar: clean, thick, icon-free — labels carry the weight
- Each segment fills in with a color animation (left to right, 1 second per segment)
- Timeline remains on screen through transition to next scene

**Transition:** Timeline bar shrinks to top of screen as a persistent reference strip; main visual shifts

---

## Scene 03 — [01:00–01:30] The Hook — Show Don't Tell

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Start with your agent *doing something*. Open your laptop, type a message, and let the audience watch the response appear."

**Visual description:**
Split-screen comparison:
- **Left (labeled "❌ Don't"):** Student behind a slide deck with a title slide reading "My AI Project: An Overview." Audience looks glazed.
- **Right (labeled "✅ Do"):** Student typing into an actual chat interface. Agent responds in real time. Audience leans forward — engaged.

After 10 seconds, right side expands to full screen. Chat interface shows:
- Student types: "What's the weather like in Paris?"
- Agent responds with a weather summary, including a small icon

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Split labels: "❌ Don't | ✅ Do"
- After expansion: "Your agent is the demo. Let it speak first."
- Key callout (bold): "Show, don't tell — every time."

**Production cues:**
- Left side: slightly desaturated — low energy
- Right side: warmer, brighter — high energy
- Chat interface: use a generic dark-themed chat UI; no real platform branding
- Audience reaction: subtle illustration — don't overdo it

**Transition:** Right side stays; student presenter icon moves to center frame

---

## Scene 04 — [01:30–02:30] The Build — Telling Your Agent's Story

**Narration excerpt:**
> "After your hook, give your audience the journey. Not a list of features. A story."

**Visual description:**
Animated module progress tracker — the student's full agent build arc. Each module lights up in sequence as narrator speaks:

| Module | Unlock | Icon |
|--------|--------|------|
| M1 | Name + personality | 🐣 |
| M2 | Image + audio perception | 👁️ |
| M3 | Custom knowledge | 🧠 |
| M4 | Trained model | 📚 |
| M5 | Live channel | 🎙️ |
| M6 | Guardrails + ethics | ⚖️ |
| M7 | Presented at Expo | 🎓 |

Each icon glows gold as the narrator references it.

Below the tracker: a mock narration example in speech-bubble format:
> *"In Module 1, my agent got a name and a personality — she's called Zara…"*

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Module tracker with icons
- Speech bubble: example Build narration (condensed, 2–3 lines)
- Callout: "The story of *why* is more interesting than the story of *what*."
- Bottom reminder: "Time check: 2 minutes for Build"

**Production cues:**
- Module icons should match AGENT_JOURNEY.md visual language
- Narrator example text appears at half-speed — don't rush it
- Gold glow animation on each module icon: subtle pulse, not flashy

**Transition:** Module tracker fades; live demo screen recording begins

---

## Scene 05 — [02:30–03:15] The Demo — Live and Real

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Live. Real inputs. Real outputs. Show at least three capabilities."

**Visual description:**
Screen recording insert of a student running a live demo. Three capability moments shown:

1. **Image recognition:** Student opens phone camera, holds up a photo of a handwritten math equation. Agent responds with the equation typed out + solution.
2. **Conversational response:** Student types "What should I study for a science test on ecosystems?" Agent gives a structured study plan.
3. **Channel integration:** Student sends a message on a real chat channel (blurred/generic). Agent responds in the channel.

Each capability cut lasts ~15 seconds. Between cuts: brief freeze frame with a capability label overlay.

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Label overlays between cuts:
  - "Capability 1: Image Understanding"
  - "Capability 2: Knowledge Response"
  - "Capability 3: Live Channel"
- Corner timer indicator: "Demo time: 2:30 → 4:30"
- Bottom callout: "Talk through what's happening — narrate out loud."

**Production cues:**
- Screen recording: real-looking but anonymized — no real student data
- Chat interface in capability 3: blurred enough to not identify platform
- No fast cuts — each capability should feel unhurried
- Narrator talks over screen recordings; student "presenter" voice is separate from narrator

**Transition:** Screen recording ends; freeze on final output; cut to backup plan scene

---

## Scene 06 — [03:15–03:45] When Live Demos Fail

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Here's a truth every engineer knows: live demos fail. Wi-Fi drops. APIs time out."

**Visual description:**
Three-panel sequence:
1. **The failure:** Laptop screen shows a spinning loading indicator. Wi-Fi icon has a red X. Presenter looks momentarily frozen.
2. **The backup:** Presenter calmly reaches for a tablet. Screen shows a pre-recorded 60-second video thumbnail labeled "Backup Demo — Zara (recorded yesterday)"
3. **The recovery:** Presenter plays backup video on a projected screen. Audience watches. Presenter narrates alongside it.

Speech bubble from presenter:
> *"My internet seems to be having trouble — let me show you the backup video I prepared."*

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Panel labels: "What can go wrong" / "What you prepared" / "What you say"
- Callout box: "Backup plan = 60-sec video + 3–4 screenshots + calm voice"
- Bold text: "Prepare your fallback. Use it without apology."

**Production cues:**
- Panel 1: slight red tint — problem feeling — but not panic
- Panel 2: neutral — preparation
- Panel 3: warm — confident recovery
- Speech bubble: use student-character illustration consistent with other scenes

**Transition:** Fade from panel 3 to reflect segment

---

## Scene 07 — [03:45–04:20] Reflect — The Honest Part

**Narration excerpt:**
> "This is where you reflect — honestly — on what your agent gets wrong."

**Visual description:**
Student at the front of a room, facing an audience. A thought bubble appears over the student's head:

Inside the thought bubble:
> *"My agent sometimes gives overconfident answers to questions it doesn't really know."*

Below the thought bubble: two side-by-side response cards:
- **❌ Weak reflection:** "It's pretty good, mostly works fine."
- **✅ Strong reflection:** "Here's one thing I'd fix if I had more time — and here's why I haven't fixed it yet."

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Thought bubble: example reflection quote
- Response cards with labels
- Callout: "Honest reflection = the most credible part of your pitch."
- Subtext: "The audience already knows AI isn't perfect. Show them that *you* know it too."

**Production cues:**
- Thought bubble: warm, slightly imperfect illustration — not corporate
- ❌ / ✅ cards: color-coded but also icon-coded (never color alone)
- Student illustration: same character as Scene 01 for continuity

**Transition:** Cut to Q&A scene

---

## Scene 08 — [04:20–04:45] Handling Tough Questions

**Narration excerpt:**
> "You'll get hard questions. Answer honestly. You're not selling this. You built it. You're explaining it."

**Visual description:**
Three Q&A cards animate in, each showing an audience question and a student response approach:

**Card 1:**
- Question: "What does your agent get wrong?"
- Response guide: "Tell them something real. Not the worst thing — but something true."

**Card 2:**
- Question: "Could someone misuse this?"
- Response guide: "Acknowledge the concern. Explain your guardrails. Be honest about limits."

**Card 3:**
- Question: "How does AI actually work?"
- Response guide: "Give your honest answer, including what you still don't fully understand."

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Card header: "Tough Questions — How to Answer Them"
- Cards animate in left to right with 3-second pause between each
- Footer callout: "You don't have to know everything. You built something real — that's enough."

**Production cues:**
- Cards: clean, text-forward — no decorative clutter
- Question text: italic, slightly muted; Response guide: bold, direct
- Allow full reading time before transition — these are the most actionable cards in the tutorial

**Transition:** Cards fade; return to opening scene visual

---

## Scene 09 — [04:45–05:00] Closing

**Narration excerpt:**
> "Hook. Build. Demo. Reflect. Five minutes. Go."

**Visual description:**
Return to the opening scene (Scene 01) — student at laptop, audience behind them. Same illustration. But now the student looks forward, ready. Laptop screen visible: chat interface open.

Module progress map appears:
- ✅ Tutorial: How to Pitch Your AI Project ← *You are here*
- → Checklist: Pre-Expo Technical Checklist
- → Career Pathways: What Comes Next?

**On-screen text/callouts:**
- Large text: "Hook → Build → Demo → Reflect"
- Below: "You built this. Now show the world."
- Module map with next steps

**Production cues:**
- Callback to opening visual = emotional closure
- Allow 2-second silent pause before text appears
- End card lingers 5–7 seconds for accessibility
- CTA: "Next: Pre-Expo Checklist →"
