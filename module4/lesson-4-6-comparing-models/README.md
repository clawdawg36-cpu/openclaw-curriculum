# Lesson 4.6 — Comparing AI Models

**Module:** 4 — Give Your Agent the Ability to Learn  
**Duration:** 50–60 minutes  
**Grade Level:** 6–8

---

## Overview

The capstone lesson of Module 4. Students systematically compare GPT-4/ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini on the same set of prompts — discovering that models are not interchangeable. Using the training data, alignment, and bias concepts from the previous five lessons, they explain the differences they observe. This lesson is positioned after the full training arc so students can reason about *why* models differ, not just *that* they differ.

**Note:** This lesson incorporates the standalone "[M4] Lesson — Comparing AI Models" task. It belongs here — after students understand training data, overfitting, and bias — so they have the framework to understand root causes.

## Key Activity

**The Great AI Comparison** — Students run 5 controlled prompts (factual, creative, sensitive/ethical, reasoning, and student-designed) across 2–3 AI models. Structured worksheet documents observations. Debrief connects differences to Module 4 concepts (training data, alignment, bias).

## Required Tech Access

- chat.openai.com (free tier)
- claude.ai (free tier)
- gemini.google.com (free tier)

If school policies restrict these: teacher can project live and students observe + record.

## Essential Questions

- Why do different AI models give different answers to the same question?
- How do training data and alignment choices shape a model's "personality"?
- How do you choose the right AI model for a given task?
