# Lesson 4.5 — Bias in Training Data

**Module:** 4 — Give Your Agent the Ability to Learn  
**Duration:** 50–60 minutes  
**Grade Level:** 6–8

---

## Overview

The ethics integration lesson of Module 4. Students examine how biased training data produces biased models — and the real-world harm that results. Using two case studies (Gender Shades facial recognition study and pulse oximeter accuracy disparities), students analyze representation bias, measurement bias, and feedback loop bias. A structured bias audit activity applies these concepts to a new scenario.

## Key Activities

**Case Study Analysis:** Gender Shades (Joy Buolamwini) or Pulse Oximeter bias — students examine root causes, who was harmed, who didn't notice, and what was done.

**Bias Audit:** Students audit a proposed job recommendation AI dataset for representation bias, measurement bias, and feedback loop risks — and recommend fixes.

## Content Note

This lesson deals with racial and gender bias in AI systems. The material is handled through documented, published research (not opinion). Create a respectful discussion environment. Some students may have personal experience with algorithmic discrimination.

## Essential Questions

- How does bias get into AI systems?
- Who is harmed when AI systems are biased — and who doesn't notice?
- What responsibilities do AI builders have for the harms their systems cause?
