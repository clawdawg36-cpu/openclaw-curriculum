## The Prompt

> **"What would happen if an AI was trained on data from only one city?"**

---

## Background Reading (Read Before You Write)

### The Problem of Biased Training Data

Every AI model learns from the data it's given. If that data represents only a small slice of the world, the model will only understand that slice — and will likely fail (sometimes harmfully) when it encounters anything outside it.

In 2015, a major tech company's facial recognition software was found to have significantly higher error rates for darker-skinned faces. Why? Because the training data contained far more lighter-skinned faces. The model didn't learn to recognize "faces" — it learned to recognize the kinds of faces it saw most.

This is called **training data bias**, and it's one of the most serious challenges in AI ethics today.

### Thinking About "One City"

Imagine an AI system trained only on data collected from a single city — let's say the facial expressions, voice recordings, text messages, and purchasing habits of people who live in San Francisco, California.

- San Francisco has a specific demographic makeup (age, race, income, profession)
- It has specific weather, culture, and daily routines
- The slang and language patterns are specific to that region
- The "normal" behaviors in San Francisco might look very different from "normal" in rural Mississippi, Nairobi, or rural Japan

What happens when that model is deployed globally?

## Essay Requirements

**Length:** 4–6 paragraphs (approximately 400–600 words)  
**Format:** Structured essay with clear introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion

### Your essay MUST address ALL of the following:

**☐ Paragraph 1 — Introduction**  
State your thesis: What is your main argument about what would happen, and why does it matter?

**☐ Paragraph 2 — Describe the specific problem**  
Pick ONE specific type of AI system (e.g., facial recognition, language model, sentiment detector, medical diagnostic AI, hiring algorithm). Explain concretely what would go wrong if it was trained on data from only one city.

**☐ Paragraph 3 — Who gets harmed?**  
Identify which groups of people would be most harmed by this AI's errors. Be specific. Consider factors like race, language, income level, age, disability, and geography.

**☐ Paragraph 4 — Is this happening already?**  
Provide at least one real-world example where AI bias has caused documented harm. Describe the situation, who was affected, and what the consequences were.

**☐ Paragraph 5 — What should be done?**  
Propose at least TWO concrete solutions. These can be technical (e.g., more diverse data collection), policy-based (e.g., regulations), or social (e.g., requiring diverse teams to build AI). Explain WHY each solution would help.

**☐ Paragraph 6 — Conclusion**  
Bring it back to the big picture. Why does this matter beyond just one AI system? What kind of world do we want AI to help build?

## Thinking Prompts — Use These to Brainstorm First

Before you write, spend 5–10 minutes answering these questions in your head or on scratch paper:

1. What is "normal" in one city that might be very different somewhere else?

2. If the AI makes a mistake, who suffers the consequences? (Hint: is it the people who built it, or the people it's used on?)

3. Is it possible to collect perfectly representative data? What trade-offs are involved?

4. Who gets to decide what "normal" looks like in a dataset? Is that power a problem?

5. Have you or someone you know ever felt like a system "didn't understand" them? What was that like?

## A Note Before You Write

There's no single "right answer" to this prompt. This is a **normative ethics question** — it asks what *should* happen, not just what *does* happen. Your job is to think carefully, argue clearly, and support your position with evidence and reasoning.

The best essays won't just describe the problem — they'll grapple with the difficulty of solving it.

Good luck. Think hard. ✍️
