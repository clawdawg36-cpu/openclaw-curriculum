# Machine Learning for Kids — Walkthrough Video Script
**Module 4 | Step-by-Step Tutorial | Runtime: ~6 minutes**
**Platform: Machine Learning for Kids (machinelearningforkids.co.uk)**

---

## Production Notes
- Format: Screen recording + webcam overlay (talking head)
- Audience: Grades 5–8
- Tone: Casual, encouraging, "I'll show you exactly what to do"
- Captions: captions-mlkids.vtt

---

## INTRO (0:00–0:30)

**[SCREEN: Blank desktop or browser homepage]**

**NARRATOR (on-cam):**
Hey! In this walkthrough, I'm going to show you step-by-step how to use a free tool called Machine Learning for Kids to train your *own* AI model — and then test it out. It takes about 15 minutes and you don't need to install anything. Let's go!

---

## STEP 1 — GO TO THE WEBSITE (0:30–1:00)

**[SCREEN: Type URL into browser]**

**NARRATOR:**
First, open your browser and go to: **machinelearningforkids.co.uk**

**[SCREEN: Homepage loads with colorful splash and "Get started" button]**

**NARRATOR:**
You'll see this homepage. Click "Get started." You can log in with a school account if your teacher set one up, or use "Try it without registering" to jump straight in.

---

## STEP 2 — CREATE A NEW PROJECT (1:00–2:00)

**[SCREEN: Projects dashboard]**

**NARRATOR:**
Once you're in, click **"Add a new project."**

**[SCREEN: New project dialog — name it, pick type]**

**NARRATOR:**
Give your project a name. Let's call it **"Animal Classifier."**
For the type, select **"images"** — we're going to train it to recognize images.
Then click **"Create."**

**[SCREEN: Project appears in list]**

**NARRATOR:**
Boom — your project exists! Click on it to open it.

---

## STEP 3 — TRAIN (2:00–3:30)

**[SCREEN: Project overview showing three tiles: Train, Learn & Test, Make]**

**NARRATOR:**
You'll see three stages: **Train**, **Learn & Test**, and **Make**. We'll go through all three. Click **"Train."**

**[SCREEN: Training page — empty buckets]**

**NARRATOR:**
This is where you add your training data. Click **"Add new label"** and type your first category. Let's do **"cat."**

**[SCREEN: "Add new label" → "cat" bucket appears]**

**NARRATOR:**
Now add another label: **"dog."**

**[SCREEN: Two buckets: cat, dog]**

**NARRATOR:**
Now we need examples. Click **"Add example"** under **cat**. You can take a picture with your camera, or upload an image from your computer.

**[SCREEN: Image capture/upload interface]**

**NARRATOR:**
Add at least **10 examples per category** — the more the better! Mix up your angles, backgrounds, and lighting. The AI needs variety to learn well.

**[SCREEN: Buckets filling with image thumbnails]**

**NARRATOR:**
Once you have at least 10 images in each bucket, you're ready to move on!

---

## STEP 4 — LEARN & TEST (3:30–5:00)

**[SCREEN: Back to project overview, click "Learn & Test"]**

**NARRATOR:**
Go back and click **"Learn & Test."** Then click the big **"Train new machine learning model"** button.

**[SCREEN: Training progress bar]**

**NARRATOR:**
This sends your labeled images to a cloud server that trains your model. It usually takes 1–3 minutes. You'll see a progress bar. While you're waiting — this is *exactly* what we talked about in the animated video! Data going in, model being trained, accuracy coming out.

**[SCREEN: Model trained — accuracy metrics appear]**

**NARRATOR:**
It's done! You can see your model's accuracy. Now let's test it. Click **"Test your model."**

**[SCREEN: Camera/upload window opens]**

**NARRATOR:**
Show it a cat picture — or take a photo. Look at that — your AI made a prediction! It says "cat" with 87% confidence. Let's try a dog.

**[SCREEN: Dog image → prediction "dog" with confidence score]**

**NARRATOR:**
Nice! It got that right too. Try a tricky one — a blurry photo, or one with weird lighting. See how confident it is.

---

## STEP 5 — MAKE (5:00–5:40)

**[SCREEN: Back to overview, click "Make"]**

**NARRATOR:**
The third stage is **Make** — this is where you can take your model into a real project in Scratch or a web app. If you're doing the extension activity, click **"Scratch 3"** to open a Scratch editor with your AI model built right in!

**[SCREEN: Scratch opens with custom AI blocks]**

**NARRATOR:**
You can see there are special blocks just for your model. You could build a game where a sprite reacts differently when it sees a cat vs. a dog!

---

## OUTRO (5:40–6:00)

**[ON-CAM: Narrator]**

**NARRATOR:**
That's it! You just trained and tested your own machine learning model. If you want to make it smarter, go back to **Train** and add more examples. More data, better model — that's the golden rule of machine learning.

**NARRATOR:**
Now go take the Module 4 quiz and show off what you know. You've got this!
